# Finding: B. NONIDIOPATHIC (15%) (a) infectious / inflammatory

**Source section:** PERICARDITIS
**Quality score:** 1.0
**Differential count:** 25

## Differentials

- Virus (Coxsackie B, influenza)
- Bacterium
- Tuberculosis (formerly most common etiology) ◊  TB is major cause in developing countries + in HIV-immunocompromised patients
- Fungus / parasite
- Connective tissue disease: rheumatoid arthritis, lupus erythematosus (b)  traumatic
- Cardiac surgery (most frequent today)
- Radiotherapy to mediastinum: breast cancer, mediastinal tumor
- Percutaneous coronary intervention, pacemaker insertion, catheter ablation
- Acute myocardial infarction ›  Epistenocardiac pericarditis =  inflammation in close temporal relationship to acute transmural infarction
- dyspnea + weakness, peripheral edema, neck vein distension
  - abdominal enlargement (ascites + hepatomegaly)
  - pericardial knock sound = loud early-diastolic sound
  - Kussmaul sign = failure of venous pressure to fall (= elevation of jugular venous pressure) with inspiration
  - prominent X and Y descent on venous pressure curve ◊  Other causes of right heart failure must be excluded first, ie, pulmonary hypertension, severe tricuspid insufficiency, myocardial infarction
- Cardiac tamponade
- Restrictive cardiomyopathy: eg, amyloid (b)  nonrestrictive physiology:
- S/P recent cardiac surgery
- Organized intrapericardial hematoma
- focal chest pain varying with changes in patient's position
  - normal levels of troponin
- Myocardial infarction (acute)
- Uremia
- Surgery (cardiac)
- Infection
- Cancer
- typical often severe chest pain
  - pericardial friction rub (← pericarditis sicca)
  - elevated serologic markers of inflammation (eg, C-reactive protein); widespread ST elevation
- Progression to chronic sclerosing pericarditis (< 0.5% for viral pericarditis, relatively frequent for purulent + tuberculous pericarditis)
- Myocarditis = atypical ECG changes, transient regional / global wall motion abnormalities, increase in cardiac enzymes
- Chronic sclerosing pericarditis
- Chronic fibrosing pericarditis
