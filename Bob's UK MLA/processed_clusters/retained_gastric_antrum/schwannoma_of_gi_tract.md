# Finding: SCHWANNOMA OF GI TRACT

**Source section:** RETAINED GASTRIC ANTRUM
**Quality score:** 0.4
**Differential count:** 3

## Differentials

- usually incidental endoscopic finding
  - GI bleeding, abdominal pain, obstructive symptoms
- Ulceration ← pressure necrosis of overlying mucosa
- Central necrosis ← outgrowing its blood supply

## Context

=  rare benign nerve sheath tumor arising from Schwann cell Frequency:   0.2% (4%) of all (benign) gastric tumor; 2-7% of gastrointestinal mesenchymal tumors Origin:   myenteric plexus within muscularis propria of GI tract Histo:   well-circumscribed unencapsulated tumor characterized by interlacing bundles of spindle cells with wavy cigar-shaped nuclei + collagen; peripheral cuff of peritumoral lymphoid aggregates; areas of hypo- and hypercellularity (Antoni A & B) and Verocay bodies (= lining up of nuclei) are less frequent Immunohisto:   strong + diffuse stain for S-100 protein Age:   3rd-5
