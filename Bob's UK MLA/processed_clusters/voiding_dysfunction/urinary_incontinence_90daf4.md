# Finding: Urinary Incontinence

**Source section:** VOIDING DYSFUNCTION
**Quality score:** 0.7
**Differential count:** 30

## Differentials

- Stress incontinence
- Vesicovaginal / ureterovaginal fistula
- Urge incontinence
- Psychogenic incontinence
- Overflow incontinence ← lesions of sacral spinal cord / sacral reflex arc or severe outlet obstruction
- Reflex voiding (a)  hyperreflexive lesion ← lesion of upper spinal cord (b)  uninhibited / unstable bladder
- Continual dribbling =  extravesical ectopic termination of ureter
- Stress urinary incontinence
- Overactive bladder = urge incontinence
- Bladder outlet obstruction
- frequency, urgency (involuntary filling of bladder neck)
- Symptoms of nocturnal enuresis + frequency / incontinence dating back to childhood
- Idiopathic instability occurring in middle age
- Outflow obstruction commonly in men with BPH / women with pelvic floor dysfunction
- Degenerative instability ← cardiovascular + neurologic disease later in life
  - frequency, urgency, urge incontinence, occasionally nocturia
  - hesitancy + difficulty in voiding may occur in men without significant prostatic hypertrophy
- frequency, urgency, sometimes nocturia
- difficulty in voiding ± frequency
  - lifelong history of poor stream
- clinical criteria:
- Intact perineal sensation + anal tone
- Normal anatomy + function of lower extremities
- Absence of skin lesions overlying sacrum
- Normal lumbosacral spine at plain radiography
- Normal spinal cord at MR imaging
- Enuresis =  manifestation of neuromuscular vesicourethral immaturity; M÷F = 3÷2
  - intermittent wetting, usually at night during sleep
  - often positive history of enuresis from one parent
  - normal physical examination
- Epispadia
- Sacral agenesis =  segmental defect (below S2) with deficiency of nerves that innervate bladder, urethra, rectum, feet ◊  Children of diabetic mothers are affected in 17%!
- Extravesical infrasphincteric ectopic ureter only affects girls, as boys do NOT have infrasphincteric ureteral orifices (a)  ureter draining upper pole of duplex system → exits below urethral sphincter (90%) (b)  ureter draining single system → ectopic extravesical orifice (10%)
- Synechia vulvae =  adhesive fusion of minor labia → directs urine primarily into vagina, from where it dribbles out post micturition
- Vaginal reflux in obese older girls with fat thighs and fat labia
- Miscellaneous posterior urethral valves, urethral stricture, urethral diverticula

## Context

Associated with pelvic floor dysfunction: Stress Incontinence =  SPHINCTER WEAKNESS INCONTINENCE= URETHRAL INCOMPETENCE =  involuntary loss of urine during physical activity such as coughing, sneezing, laughing, exercise Cause: (a)  male: S/P prostatectomy with damage to distal sphincter (b)  female: congenital bladder neck weakness, pregnancy, childbirth, aging ← changes in anatomic relationship of urethra + bladder base √  opening of bladder neck during coughing √  impairment of milk-back mechanism (= retrograde emptying of urethra during interruption of voiding phase does not occur) √  uret
