# Finding: Density of Carotid Plaque

**Source section:** Carotid Plaque
**Quality score:** 0.9
**Differential count:** 9

## Differentials

- Hypoechoic = low-echogenicity plaque =  fibrofatty plaque / hemorrhage
- Isoechoic plaque =  smooth muscle cell proliferation / laminar thrombus
- Hyperechoic = moderately echogenic (fibrous) plaque
- Calcification = strongly echogenic plaque
- Homogeneous (stable) plaque
- Heterogeneous (unstable) plaque =  mixture of high, medium, and low-level echoes with smooth / irregular surface; may fissure / tear resulting in intraplaque hemorrhage / ulceration + thrombus formation → embolus / increasing stenosis
- Intimal thickening
- Ulcerated plaque
- Error in proper localization of stenosis (6%)

## Context

√  echogenicity less than sternocleidomastoid muscle √  flow void / flow disturbance on color Duplex √  echogenicity equal to sternocleidomastoid muscle + lower than adventitia √  echogenicity higher than sternocleidomastoid muscle + similar to adventitia √  acoustic shadow impairs visualization of intima Texture of Carotid Plaque Histo:  deposition of fatty streaks + fibrous tissue; rarely shows intraplaque hemorrhage / ulcerations √  homogeneous uniform echo pattern with smooth surface (acoustic impedance similar to blood) B-mode ultrasound has 90-94% sensitivity, 75-88% specificity, 90% acc
