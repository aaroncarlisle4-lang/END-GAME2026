# Finding: Muscular Rings of Esophagus

**Source section:** ESOPHAGUS
**Quality score:** 0.5
**Differential count:** 6

## Differentials

- rarely symptomatic / dysphagia
- Mucosa  echogenic
- Muscularis mucosae  hypoechoic
- Submucosa  echogenic / hyperechoic
- Muscularis propria  hypoechoic
- Serosa  echogenic / hyperechoic Most benign conditions preserve the gut signature involving long segments of bowel, while malignant

## Context

A Ring =  contracted / hypertrophied muscles in response to incompetent GE sphincter Location:   at tubulovestibular junction = superior aspect of vestibule √  usually 2 cm proximal to GE junction at upper end of vestibule √  varies in caliber during the same examination, may disappear on maximum distension √  broad smooth narrowing with thick rounded margins √  visible only if tubular esophagus above + vestibule below are distended B Ring =  sling fibers representing a U-shaped thickening of inner muscle layers with open arm of U toward lesser curvature = inferior aspect of vestibule Location
