## Differentials

- Choroid plexus
- become clinically apparent early before ventricular obstruction occurs
  - ipsilateral progressive multiple cranial nerve palsies
  - cerebellar dysfunction: ataxia, nystagmus
  - contralateral hemiparesis, eventually respiratory insufficiency Location:   pons > midbrain > medulla; often unilateral at medullopontine junction ◊  Medullary + mesencephalic gliomas are more benign than pontine gliomas!
- diminished visual acuity (50%) ← optic atrophy
  - diencephalic syndrome (in up to 20%): marked emaciation, pallor, unusual alertness,
- short stature (in 20%) ← reduction in growth hormone
  - obese child, sexual precocity, diabetes insipidus
