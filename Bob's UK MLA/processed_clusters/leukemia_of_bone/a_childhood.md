# Finding: A. CHILDHOOD

**Source section:** LEUKEMIA OF BONE
**Quality score:** 0.733
**Differential count:** 4

## Differentials

- Acute Lymphocytic Leukemia (ALL in 75%) most often in children < 5 years of age
- Acute Myelogenous Leukemia (AML)
- migratory paraarticular arthralgias (25-50%) ← adjacent metaphyseal lesions (may be confused with acute rheumatic fever / rheumatoid arthritis)
  - low-grade fever, bruising, fatigue
  - bone pain ← increased intraosseous pressure from proliferation of malignant cells
  - elevated erythrocyte sedimentation rate, anemia
  - hepatosplenomegaly, occasionally lymphadenopathy ◊  Peripheral blood smears may be negative in aleukemic form!
- ADULTHOOD ◊  Death usually occurs before skeletal abnormalities manifest
