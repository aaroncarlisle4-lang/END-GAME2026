# Finding: Hypogenetic Lung Syndrome

**Source section:** D. SEPTIC EMBOLI
**Quality score:** 0.733
**Differential count:** 10

## Differentials

- Pulmonary agenesis =  complete absence of a lobe + its bronchus
- Pulmonary aplasia =  rudimentary bronchus ending in blind pouch + absence of parenchyma + vessels
- Pulmonary hypoplasia =  completely formed but congenitally small bronchus with rudimentary parenchyma + small vessels
- Oligohydramnios (renal agenesis, bilateral cystic renal disease, obstructive uropathy, premature rupture of membranes)
- Fetal ascites (c)  Thoracic cage compression
- Thoracic bone dysplasia: Ellis-van Creveld, Jeune, thanatophoric dystrophy, severe achondroplasia
- Muscular disease (d)  Intrathoracic compression
- Diaphragmatic defect
- Excess pleural fluid
- Large intrathoracic cyst / tumor

## Context

=  collective name for congenital underdevelopment of one / more lobes of a lung separated into 3 forms: CT: √  missing bronchus + lobe(s) Incidence:   1÷10,000; R÷L = 1÷1 CT: √  absence of ipsilateral pulmonary artery √  bronchus terminates in dilated blind pouch √  absence of ipsilateral pulmonary tissue Developmental causes: resulting in intrauterine compression of chest (a)  Idiopathic (rare) (b)  Extrathoracic compression (= Potter syndrome)
