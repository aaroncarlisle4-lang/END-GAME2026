# Finding: Contrast Extravasation

**Source section:** CONTROL OF HEART RATE
**Quality score:** 0.367
**Differential count:** 8

## Differentials

- may be asymptomatic; edema, erythema
  - swelling, tightness, tenderness, stinging, burning pain
- Palpate catheter venipuncture site during initial seconds of injection
- Ask patient to report any sensation of pain / swelling at injection site
- Elevate affected extremity / site
- Apply a warm / cold compress
- Monitor for a sufficiently long period
- Discharge with instructions to watch for symptoms that indicate a need for surgical evaluation
- Initiate surgical consultation for progressive pain / swelling, decreased capillary refill, paresthesia, skin ulceration / blistering

## Context

=  escape of contrast material from vascular lumen + infiltration of interstitial tissue during injection Incidence:  < 1%; no direct correlation with injection flow rate Dx: Severe Cx (uncommon):   compartment syndrome, skin ulceration, tissue necrosis Rx:
