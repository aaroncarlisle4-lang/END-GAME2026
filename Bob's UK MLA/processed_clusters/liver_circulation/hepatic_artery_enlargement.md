# Finding: Hepatic Artery Enlargement

**Source section:** LIVER CIRCULATION
**Quality score:** 0.533
**Differential count:** 4

## Differentials

- Cirrhosis → compensatory response to decreased portal venous flow
- Intrahepatic arteriovenous shunting (a)  vascular neoplasm (b)  hepatic artery-portal vein fistula
- Hereditary hemorrhagic telangiectasia
- Chronic active hepatitis

## Context

A high RI is not specific for liver disease; therefore, it is less meaningful as an isolated finding than is a low RI. Cause:   biopsy, trauma √  turbulent high-velocity low-resistance flow √  soft-tissue bruit (= random assignment of color in perivascular soft tissue due to tissue vibration) √  arterialized frequently retrograde flow in portal vein √  large tortuous feeding arteries with high velocity + aliased flow √  multiple dilated vessels (representing AVMs) √  large draining veins √  areas of fatty change + fibrosis
