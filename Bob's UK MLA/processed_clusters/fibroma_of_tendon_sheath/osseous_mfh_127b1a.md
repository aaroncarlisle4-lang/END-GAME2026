## Context

Prevalence:   5% of all primary malignant bone tumors Associated with: prior radiation therapy, bone infarcts, Paget disease, fibrous dysplasia, osteonecrosis, fibroxanthoma (= nonossifying fibroma), enchondroma, chronic osteomyelitis ◊  20% of osseous MFH arise in areas of abnormal bone! Location:   femur (45%), tibia (20%), 50% about knee; humerus (10%); ilium (10%); spine; sternum; clavicle; rarely small bones of hand + feet Site:  central metaphysis of long bones (90%); eccentric in diaphysis of long bones (10%) Size:  2.5-10 cm in diameter √  radiolucent defect with ill-defined margins √
