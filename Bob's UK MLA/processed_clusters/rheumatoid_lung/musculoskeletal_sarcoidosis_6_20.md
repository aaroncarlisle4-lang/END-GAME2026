# Finding: Musculoskeletal Sarcoidosis (6-20%

**Source section:** RHEUMATOID LUNG
**Quality score:** 0.533
**Differential count:** 7

## Differentials

- unimpaired joint function
- pain and swelling
- cranial neuropathy (facial > acoustic > optic > trigeminal nerves) ← granulomatous infiltration + leptomeningeal fibrosis (50-75%) of rapid onset with spontaneous resolution
  - peripheral neuropathy + myopathy
  - aseptic meningitis (20%)
  - diffuse encephalopathy, dementia
  - pituitary + hypothalamic dysfunction (eg, diabetes insipidus in 5-10%)
  - generalized / focal seizures (herald poorer prognosis)
  - multiple sclerosislike symptoms (from multifocal parenchymal involvement)
  - prompt improvement following therapy with steroids
    - spinal fluid: normal (in up to 30%) or nonspecific (↑ protein, ↑ leukocyte count, ↔ / ↓ glucose) Location:   leptomeninges, dura mater, subarachnoid space, peripheral nerves, brain parenchyma, ventricular system ◊  Affects meninges + cranial nerves more often than brain!
- Erythema nodosum = nongranulomatous panniculitis:
- often associated with fever + arthralgia (= painful swollen adjacent joints) Onset:   typically associated with benign self-limiting acute sarcoidosis (eg, Löfgren syndrome)
- Lupus pernio = indurated red-brown to violaceous (= bluish purple) papules + plaques
- Asymptomatic pink-yellow / red-brown papules + plaques
