# Finding: Single-cavity Cochlea

**Source section:** OTIC CAPSULE DYSPLASIA
**Quality score:** 0.9
**Differential count:** 9

## Differentials

- profound hearing loss discovered in early childhood
- some high-frequency hearing preserved; vertigo
  - otorrhea, rhinorrhea, recurrent meningitis (perilymphatic fistula caused by absence / defect of stapes footplate)
- total sensorineural hearing loss
- Anomalies of Membranous Labyrinth
- Scheibe dysplasia = abnormal cochlea + saccule
- Alexander dysplasia = dysplasia of basal turn
- total sensorineural hearing loss
- sensorineural hearing loss (most common cause)
- unilateral congenital deafness (commonly missed) ◊  in 12% of children with congenital SNHL
  - vertigo, tinnitus (in 50%)

## Context

=  cochlear cavity malformation =  saccular defect / cavity in otic capsule in the position normally occupied by cochlea without recognizable modiolus, osseous spiral lamina, interscalar septum May be associated with:   recurrent bacterial meningitis, perilymphatic fistula of oval window √  cystic cochlea (= developed basal turn, middle + apical turn occupy common nondeveloped space) Type: (a)   incomplete partition type I =  cystic cochleovestibular malformation =  complete lack of partitioning of cochlea √  cystic structure at CT (b)  incomplete partition type II (see below) =  classic Mondi
