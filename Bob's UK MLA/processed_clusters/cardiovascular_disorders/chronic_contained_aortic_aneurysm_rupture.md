# Finding: Chronic Contained Aortic Aneurysm Rupture

**Source section:** CARDIOVASCULAR DISORDERS
**Quality score:** 1.0
**Differential count:** 15

## Differentials

- hemodynamically stable
  - ± previous episode of abdominal pain
- asymptomatic (most)
  - chest pain; symptoms related to compression of adjacent structures (dysphagia, hoarseness, lobar atelectasis, pneumonia, parenchymal hemorrhage, superior vena cava syndrome) Location:   distal abdominal aorta (66%) > iliac a. > popliteal a. > common femoral a. > aortic + descending thoracic aorta > carotid a. > ascending aorta
- Infrarenal aorta (associated with thoracic aneurysm in 29%)
- Descending thoracic aorta distal to left subclavian artery
- Thoracoabdominal aorta
- Genetically transmitted metabolic disorder: Marfan syndrome, Ehlers-Danlos syndrome
- Acquired: result of repetitive aortic injury + repair associated with aging
- thickened wall of aneurysm
- extensive perianeurysmal + retroperitoneal fibrosis
- dense adhesions of adjacent abdominal organs Cause:  slow leakage from aneurysm related to periaortic retroperitoneal fibrosis and autoimmune diseases (rheumatoid arthritis, systemic lupus erythematosus, giant cell arteritis)
- abdominal / back pain; weight loss + anorexia (20-41%)
  - elevated ESR (40-88%), fever
  - tender pulsatile abdominal mass (15-30%) Comorbidities:   arterial hypertension (34-69%), arterial occlusive disease (10-47%), diabetes mellitus (3-13%), coronary artery disease (33-55%)
- Entrapment of ureters (10-21%) + hydronephrosis
- Aortic-sigmoid colon fistula + bleeding
- Secondary bacterial infection (eg, Salmonella)
- Enlargement + rupture irrespective of size (lower rate than in noninflammatory aneurysm)

## Context
