- Bacterial endocarditis (perforation / prolapse of cusp)
- Rupture of congenitally fenestrated cusp
- Myxomatous valve associated with cystic medial necrosis
- Atherosclerotic degeneration ◊  Severely calcified aortic valves are often regurgitant
- Connective tissue disorder: Marfan syndrome
- Ergot toxicity, anorectic drugs
- Systemic inflammation: lupus erythematosus, Crohn disease, Takayasu arteritis, giant cell arteritis

  - Austin Flint murmur = soft mid-diastolic or presystolic bruit
