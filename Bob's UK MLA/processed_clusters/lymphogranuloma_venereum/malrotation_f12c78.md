# Finding: MALROTATION

**Source section:** LYMPHOGRANULOMA VENEREUM
**Quality score:** 0.667
**Differential count:** 11

## Differentials

- symptoms of partial / complete proximal bowel obstruction ← volvulus / peritoneal (Ladd) bands / internal hernia:
  - bilious vomiting (77% of neonates; in 39% within 1st week of life ± abdominal distension
  - recurrent attacks of vomiting + distension (in older children)
  - insidious onset in adulthood with chronic nonspecific symptoms ← intermittent volvulus
- Midintestinal / midgut volvulus
- Duodenal obstruction
- Ladd bands
- Internal herniation
- Mortality:   3-5%
- Rx:   Ladd procedure
- Nonrotation
- generally asymptomatic: often incidental finding in older children + adults
- prearterial segment of midgut reenters abdomen first toward left side
- postarterial segment of midgut reenters abdomen first =  cecum migrates first passing behind SMA toward right thus unwinding the normal counterclockwise rotation of the first stage with additional final 90° clockwise rotation
