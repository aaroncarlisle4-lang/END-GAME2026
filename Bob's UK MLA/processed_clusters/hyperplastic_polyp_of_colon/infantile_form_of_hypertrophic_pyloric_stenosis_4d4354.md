# Finding: Infantile Form of Hypertrophic Pyloric Stenosis

**Source section:** HYPERPLASTIC POLYP OF COLON
**Quality score:** 0.833
**Differential count:** 7

## Differentials

- nonbilious projectile vomiting (sour formula / clear gastric contents) → progression over a period of several weeks after birth (15-20%)
  - palpable olive-shaped mass (80% sensitive in experienced hands, up to 14% false positive)
  - nasogastric aspirate > 10 mL (92% sensitive, 86% specific)
- Empty stomach via nasogastric tube before study
- Remove contrast at end of study
- Infantile pylorospasm =  temporary thickening of pyloric muscle
- Gastritis / milk allergy
- Duodenal obstruction from midgut volvulus
- Gastric diaphragm
