# Finding: Abnormal Nephrogram due to Impaired Perfusion

**Source section:** Persistent Nephrogram
**Quality score:** 0.5
**Differential count:** 6

## Differentials

- SYSTEMIC HYPOTENSIVE REACTION as a reaction to contrast material / cardiac failure / dehydration / shock
- RENAL ARTERY STENOSIS
- IMPAIRED PERFUSION OF SMALL ARTERIES
- ACUTE VENOUS OUTFLOW OBSTRUCTION in renal vein thrombosis
- EXTRARENAL: ureteric obstruction (eg, stone)
- INTRARENAL (a)  segmental: limb of duplication system, caliceal obstruction, interstitial edema

## Context

Pathophysiology: drop in perfusion pressure after contrast reaches kidney → increased salt + water reabsorption and slowed tubular transit √  prolonged bilateral dense nephrograms = persistent increasing nephrogram √  decrease in renal size √  loss of pyelogram after initial opacification NUC (use of glomerular filtration agent [eg, 99mTc-DTPA] preferred) √  prolonged cortical transit + reduced excretion √  decreased nephrographic opacity + rim nephrogram √  hyperconcentration in collecting system √  ureteral notching NUC (glomerular filtration agent [eg, 99mTc-DTPA] preferred): √  decreased p
