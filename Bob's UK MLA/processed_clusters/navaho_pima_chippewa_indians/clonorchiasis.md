# Finding: CLONORCHIASIS

**Source section:** Navaho, Pima, Chippewa Indians
**Quality score:** 0.467
**Differential count:** 5

## Differentials

- remittent incomplete obstruction + bacterial superinfection Location:   medium-sized + small intrahepatic bile ducts, primarily in subcapsular region
- Bile duct obstruction ← conglomerate of worms / adenomatous proliferation of ducts
- Calculus formation ← stasis / dead worms / epithelial debris
- Jaundice in 8% ← stone / stricture / tumor
- Generalized dilatation of bile ducts (2%)

## Context

Rarely of clinical significance Country:   endemic to Southeast Asia: Japan, Korea, Central + South China, Taiwan, Indochina; Eastern Europe Organism:   Chinese liver fluke = Clonorchis sinensis Closely related:   Opisthorchis viverrini (Thailand and Laos); Opisthorchis felineus (Siberia) Cycle:   parasite cysts digested by gastric juice → larvae migrate up the bile ducts → remain in small intrahepatic ducts until maturity (10-30 mm in length) → travel to larger ducts to deposit eggs Infection:   snail + freshwater fish serve as intermediate hosts; infection occurs by eating raw fish; hog, dog
