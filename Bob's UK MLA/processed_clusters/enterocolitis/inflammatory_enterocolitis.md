# Finding: Inflammatory Enterocolitis

**Source section:** ENTEROCOLITIS
**Quality score:** 0.7
**Differential count:** 13

## Differentials

- PRIMARY: inflammatory bowel disease (ulcerative colitis, Crohn disease), vasculitis
- SECONDARY: chemotherapy, radiation therapy, graft versus host disease, angiotensin- converting enzyme inhibitor-induced enteritis
- tenesmus, diarrhea, weight loss
  - extraintestinal features: large joint arthritis, erythema nodosum, pyoderma gangrenosum
- feeding intolerance, abdominal distension, bilious emesis
  - blood-streaked stools (in 50%); explosive diarrhea
  - mild respiratory distress, generalized sepsis with shock Location:  usually in terminal ileum (most commonly involved), cecum, right colon; rarely in stomach, upper bowel
- Inflammatory stricture after healing in 10-30%, in 30% multiple, in 80% in left colon (BE follow-up in survivors)
- Bowel perforation in 12-32%
- abdominal pain, may be localized to RLQ
  - watery / bloody diarrhea; fullness / palpable mass in RLQ
  - fever, neutropenia; hematochezia / occult blood Location:  right colon (cecum + appendix + ascending colon); distal ileum + transverse colon may become secondarily involved The length of cecum and right colon involved by typhlitis is generally much greater than that associated with appendicitis.
- Abscess formation
- Early aggressive medical support (high doses of antibiotics + IV fluids), bowel rest, total parenteral nutrition, electrolyte replacement prior to development of transmural necrosis
- Surgery for uncontrollable GI bleeding, obstruction, abscess, transmural necrosis, free perforation, uncontrollable sepsis
- Appendicitis with periappendicular abscess (normal cecal wall thickness)
- Diverticulitis
- Inflammatory bowel disease
