# Finding: Thoracic Inlet Lesion

**Source section:** MEDIASTINUM
**Quality score:** 1.0
**Differential count:** 14

## Differentials

- Thyroid mass 1-3% of all thyroidectomies have a mediastinal component; ⅓ of goiters are intrathoracic
- Cystic hygroma 3-10% involve mediastinum; childhood
- Lymphoma
- Other tumors: adenoma, carcinoma, ectopic thymoma
- Aberrant right subclavian artery
- Aberrant left subclavian a. with right aortic arch
- Aneurysm
- Posterior descending goiter
- Enlarged lymph node
- Esophageal mass / duplication cyst
- Thymoma
- Teratoma
- Thyroid tumor / goiter
- Terrible lymphoma

## Context

Location:   anterior (80%) / posterior (20%) mediastinum √  displacement of trachea posteriorly and laterally ← anterior goiter √  displacement of trachea anteriorly and laterally + esophagus posteriorly ← posterior goiter √  inhomogeneous density ← cystic spaces + high-density iodine contents of > 100 HU √  focal calcifications (common) √  marked + prolonged contrast enhancement √  connection to thyroid gland √  vascular displacement + compression NUC (rarely helpful as thyroid tissue may be nonfunctioning): √  ± uptake on 123I / 131I scan (pertechnetate is sufficient with modern gamma camera
