# Disease: RENAL FAILURE

**Source section:** DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS OF UROGENITAL DISORDERS

## Definition

reduction in renal function clinical condition associated with rapid steadily increasing azotemia ± oliguria (< 500 mL decrease in renal function over months / years

## Key Facts

- **Frequency:** 70% of community-acquired ARF (glomerulonephritis); 40% of hospital-
- **Incidence:** end-stage renal disease in 0.01% of USA population; annually 85,000 patients
- **Path:** amyloid consists of β2-microglobulin

## Imaging Findings

### General

- abnormally elevated resistive index
- resistive index < 0.75 in 80% of kidneys
- resistive index ≥ 0.75 in 91% of kidneys
- normal resistive index < 0.70
- abnormal resistive index
- hydronephrosis
- diskovertebral junction erosion + sclerosis
- vertebral body compression
- disk space narrowing
- Schmorl node formation
- lack of osteophytosis
- facet involvement with subluxation

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** 20-70% mortality
