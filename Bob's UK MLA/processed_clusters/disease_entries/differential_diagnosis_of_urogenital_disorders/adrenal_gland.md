# Disease: ADRENAL GLAND

**Source section:** DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS OF UROGENITAL DISORDERS

## Definition

incidental discovery of a clinically silent adrenal mass of > 10 mm in a patient without CT washout scan (most effective test) [HUCECT 1 min - HUCECT 15 min] / [HUCECT 1 min] • 100% [HUCECT 1 min - HUCECT 15 min] / [HUCECT 1 min - HUNECT] • 100%

## Key Facts

- **Prevalence:** 4-9% of all CT exams (in 0.2% of patients 20-29 years, in 7% of elderly); 1.0-
- **Frequency:** in < 30% of adenomas
- **Distribution:** 70% of adenomas are lipid-rich; 30% of adenomas are lipid-poor +
- **Age:** 1st week of life

## Imaging Findings

### General

- usually bilateral homogeneous adrenal enlargement in acute phase
- sometimes cystic / calcified adrenals in chronic phase
- > 10% of pixel count below 0 HU indicates adenoma (sensitivity of 71% for NECT,
- fat + water summate to intermediate SI on in-phase images
- fat + water signals cancel each other out to a low signal intensity on out-of-
- adrenal-to-spleen SI ratio (SIR) < 0.71
- signal intensity index (SII) > 16.5%
- < 10 HU imply an adenoma (in 96%)
- diffuse enlargement / discrete mass
- ± central cystic changes ± calcification

## Clinical

- **Cx:** tuberculous adrenal atrophy → adrenal hypofunction → Addison disease
- **Rx:** excision for incidentaloma > 5 cm
- **N.B.:** several deaths reported after Bx of pheochromocytoma
