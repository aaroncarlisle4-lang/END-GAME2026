# Disease: RETROPERITONEAL ONCOCYTOMA

**Source section:** NEUROGENIC BLADDER

## Definition

ONCOCYTIC ADRENOCORTICAL ADENOMA / NEOPLASM PROXIMAL TUBULAR ADENOMA = BENIGN OXYPHILIC ADENOMA rare form of a benign hypervascular adenoma

## Key Facts

- **Histo:** abundant deeply eosinophilic (mitochondria-rich) cells; positive for vimentin, melan
- **Location:** L÷R = 1.5÷1
- **Prevalence:** 3-7% of solid renal masses
- **Path:** well-encapsulated tan-colored tumor of well-differentiated proximal tubular cells +

## Imaging Findings

### General

- well-marginated often large mass with heterogeneous enhancement of nonspecific
- renal mass of homogeneously low attenuation / hypoechogenicity (in > 50%)
- well-demarcated with pseudocapsule
- central stellate scar in 30% (in lesions > 3 cm in diameter ← organization of central
- invasion of renal capsule / renal vein in large tumors

### MR

- typically spherical well-defined mass
- hypointense relative to renal parenchyma on T1WI (70%)
- hyperintense relative to renal parenchyma on T2WI (67%)
- prominent enhancement of mass
- central stellate scar:
- hypointense on T1WI + hyperintense on T2WI
- less enhancement than remainder of mass
- scar may show delayed enhancement
- nonspecific well-defined hypointense capsule (in < 50%)

### ANGIO

- spoke-wheel configuration in 80% (DDx: renal cell ca.)
- homogeneously dense parenchymal phase (71%)
- NO contrast puddling / arteriovenous shunting / renal vein invasion

### NUC

- photopenic area on 99mTc-DMSA ← tubular cells do not function normally

## Clinical

- **Cx:** malignant transformation
- **Rx:** local resection / heminephrectomy
- **Prognosis:** death from malignancy following surgery (3%)
