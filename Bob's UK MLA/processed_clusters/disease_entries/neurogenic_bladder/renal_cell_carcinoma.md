## Key Facts

- **Prevalence:** 2-3% of all adult visceral cancers (frequency approximates ovarian cancer, gastric
- **Age:** 6th-7th decade (generally > 40 years); median age of 55 years; 2% occur in children in
- **Path:** arises from proximal tubular cells
- **Associated with:** Birt-Hogg-Dubé syndrome
- **Histo:** round to polygonal cells with well-defined cytoplasmic border, abundant pale
- **Genetics:** translocation of short arm of chromosome 3; mutation of von Hippel-Lindau tumor
- **Location:** in cortex as exophytic hypervascular solid mass
- **Frequency:** 7% of all primary renal tumors during first 2 decades of life;
- **Mean age:** 9 years

## Imaging Findings

### General

- well-marginated often lobulated solitary mass:
- focal bulge in renal contour
- enlargement of affected part of kidney
- calcification (15-20%): usually central + amorphous or peripheral + curvilinear in cystic
- extrinsic compression / displacement / invasion of renal pelvis + calices
- cysts:
- tumor growth into renal vein (23%) / IVC (in up to 16%) → conveys poor prognosis
- infiltrative growth pattern (6%) with ill-defined margin
- diminished function ← parenchymal replacement, hydronephrosis
- absence of contrast excretion ← renal vein occlusion
- pyelotumoral backflow = necrotic part of tumor fills with contrast material

### ANGIO

### CT

- tumor metastases tend to be hypervascular
- some metastases (eg, clear cell RCC) are detected only during arterial phase
- hypovascular metastases are best detected during the nephrographic phase
- negative uptake for bone metastases if lesion purely lytic without associated reactive
- typically hypovascular → enhances to a lesser degree compared with clear cell RCC
- homogeneous slow growing well-encapsulated tumor
- peripheral calcifications frequent

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** 5-year survival rate of 90%; better than for cRCC
- **Cx:** intravascular extension (25%)
