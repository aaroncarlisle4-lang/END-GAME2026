# Disease: RENAL TUBULAR ACIDOSIS

**Source section:** NEUROGENIC BLADDER

## Definition

clinical syndrome characterized by tubular insufficiency to resorb bicarbonate, excrete TYPE 2 RTA impaired capacity to absorb HCO3 - in proximal tubule → bicarbonate in urine at lower tubular defect of bicarbonate resorption associated with other tubular dysfunction / TYPE 1 RTA (first type discovered) impaired ability to secrete H+ in distal tubule despite low levels of plasma bicarbonate ADULT TYPE OF PRIMARY DISTAL RTA = BUTLER-ALBRIGHT SYNDROME INFANTILE TYPE OF PRIMARY DISTAL RTA LIGHTWOOD SYNDROME = SALT-LOSING NEPHRITIS transient self-limited form of infancy (only observed within 1st year of life) with unclear

## Key Facts

- **Age:** diagnosed within first 18 months of life; usually male patient
- **Path:** calcium deposits accompanied by chronic interstitial nephritis with cellular
- **Genetics:** mostly sporadic; may be autosomal dominant

## Imaging Findings

### General

- rickets / osteomalacia
- rickets / osteomalacia
- moderately retarded bone age
- medullary nephrocalcinosis / nephrolithiasis (as early as 1 month of age)

- **Rx:** administration of alkali ± potassium ± hydrochloro-thiazide
- **Prognosis:** transient type with spontaneous remission
