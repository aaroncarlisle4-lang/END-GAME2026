# Disease: POLYCYSTIC KIDNEY DISEASE

**Source section:** NEUROGENIC BLADDER

## Definition

ADPKD = ADULT POLYCYSTIC KIDNEY DISEASE= Potter type III characterized by bilaterally enlarged kidneys with multiple expansile cysts along nephron ARPKD = INFANTILE POLYCYSTIC KIDNEY DISEASE = POLY-CYSTIC DISEASE OF CHILDHOOD = most common ciliopathy affecting kidney + liver to varying degrees

## Imaging Findings

### General

- enlargement of both kidneys with innumerable cysts of varied size + diffuse distribution
- cysts may calcify in curvilinear rim- / ringlike irregular amorphous fashion
- elongated + distorted + attenuated collecting system
- nodular puddling of contrast material on delayed images
- "Swiss cheese" nephrogram = multiple lesions of varying size with smooth margins
- polycystic kidneys shrink after beginning of renal failure, after renal transplantation, or on
- liver cysts (may be identified without renal involvement):
- isolated from bile ducts (DDx ARPKD)
- liver cyst size + number increase with advancing age → liver function preserved

### NUC

- multiple areas of diminished activity, cortical activity only in areas of functioning cortex

## Clinical

- **Cx:** mass effect, intracyst hemorrhage / infection
- **N.B.:** A normal US examination cannot exclude ADPKD until after age 35 years!
- **Prognosis:** death from renal failure / respiratory insufficiency (pulmonary hypoplasia)
