# Disease: TESTICULAR TRAUMA

**Source section:** NEUROGENIC BLADDER

## Definition

SCROTAL TRAUMA break / discontinuity in testicular parenchyma tear / rupture of tunica albuginea with protrusion of seminiferous tubules

## Key Facts

- **Frequency:** < 1% of all traumas
- **Peak age:** 10-30 years

## Imaging Findings

### General

- hematoma in scrotal wall / between layers of tunica vaginalis (= hematocele) / in epididymis /
- uriniferous hydrocele ← perforated bulbous urethra
- linear hypoechoic + avascular band disrupting the architecture (= fracture line)
- smooth outer contour with intact tunica albuginea
- usually preserved vascular flow

### US

- over time age-dependent rapid change in echo character of hematoma + decrease in size
- initially hyperechoic acute hematoma
- later isoechoic → reexamination after 12-24 hours
- hypoechoic / anechoic mass decreasing in size
- absence of internal vascularity (DDx: tumor)
- peripheral hyperemia in infected hematoma (40% incidence)
- developing testicular cyst ← subacute / chronic liquefaction of hematoma

### MR

- hyperintense hematoma on T1WI
- hypointense hemosiderin rim on T2WI
- disruption of tunica albuginea = discontinuity of echogenic testicular outline (50%
- abnormal testicular contour = extrusion of testicular parenchyma (difficult DDx:
- heterogeneous echotexture of testis with areas of ⇓ / ⇑ echogenicity ← hemorrhage ±
- avascular region on color duplex ← disruption of tunica vasculosa (DDx: hematoma)
- thickened scrotal wall ← hematoma of scrotal wall
- hematocele, may show thickening + calcification of tunica vaginalis if chronic
- area of heterogeneously low SI on T2WI
- interruption of hypointense fibrous tunica albuginea

## Clinical

- **Cx:** torsion in 5-8% ← sudden forceful cremasteric contraction on "bell and clapper"
- **Rx:** conservative / debridement along fracture line
