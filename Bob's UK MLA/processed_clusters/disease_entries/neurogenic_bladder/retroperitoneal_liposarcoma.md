# Disease: RETROPERITONEAL LIPOSARCOMA

**Source section:** NEUROGENIC BLADDER

## Definition

slow-growing tumor that displaces rather than infiltrates surrounding tissue and rarely

## Key Facts

- **Frequency:** most common (33%) of primary malignant retroperitoneal sarcomas; 95% of all
- **Age:** most commonly 50-70 years; M = F
- **Size:** average diameter > 20 cm

## Imaging Findings

### General

- radiodensity of fat
- radiodensity between water + muscle
- solid appearance on US
- radiodensity of muscle for (c), (d), (e)

### CT

- solid pattern: inhomogeneous poorly marginated infiltrating mass with contrast
- mixed pattern: focal fatty areas (-40 to -20 HU) + areas of higher density (+20 HU)
- pseudocystic pattern: water-density mass ← averaging of fatty + solid connective-tissue
- calcifications in up to 12% (= important sign of dedifferentiation of an initially well-

### MR

- hyperintense on T1WI
- intermediate SI on T2WI; more hyperintense for myxoid type ← mucopolysaccharide
- loss of fat signal intensity on fat-suppressed sequence
- enhancing thick irregular nodular septa

### ANGIO

- hypovascular without vessel dilatation / capillary staining / laking

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** local recurrence in 90%; most radiosensitive of soft-tissue sarcomas; 32% overall
