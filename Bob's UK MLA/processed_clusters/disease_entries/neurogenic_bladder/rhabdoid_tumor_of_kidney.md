# Disease: RHABDOID TUMOR OF KIDNEY

**Source section:** NEUROGENIC BLADDER

## Key Facts

- **Frequency:** < 2% of pediatric renal tumors
- **Mean age:** 11-17 months; 6-12 months of age (25%), < 1 year [< 2 years] of age 60% [80%];
- **Histo:** monomorphic noncohesive large cells with prominent nucleoli + abundant eosinophilic
- **Associated with:** syn- / metachronous primary brain tumor of neuroectodermal origin

## Imaging Findings

### General

- centrally located heterogeneous renal mass:
- indistinct borders with infiltration of medulla + sinus
- PROMINENT peripheral crescent-shaped subcapsular fluid collection in 70% (subcapsular
- SUGGESTIVE multilobulated architecture, esp. if surrounded by low-attenuation material ←
- curvilinear calcifications outlining tumor lobules (²/³)
- may invade renal vein and IVC
- midline mass in posterior fossa of skull
- metastatic (in 80%) to lung, liver, retroperitoneal lymph nodes, abdomen, brain, bone

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** 20% 18-month survival rate
