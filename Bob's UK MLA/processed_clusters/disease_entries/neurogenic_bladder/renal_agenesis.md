# Disease: RENAL AGENESIS

**Source section:** NEUROGENIC BLADDER

## Definition

failure of ureteral bud to form rare congenital abnormality of the mesonephric (Wolffian) duct = TRIAD of (1) POTTER SYNDROME hypoplasia of lungs, bowing of legs, broad hands, loose skin, growth retardation

## Key Facts

- **Prevalence:** 1÷600 to 1÷1,000 pregnancies; M÷F = 1.8÷1
- **Location:** L > R

## Imaging Findings

### General

- visualization of single kidney (DDx: additional kidney in ectopic location)
- absent adrenal gland (11%)
- absent / rudimentary renal vessels
- colon occupies renal fossa
- compensatory contralateral renal hypertrophy (50%)
- severe oligohydramnios (after 14 weeks MA)
- bilateral absence of kidneys, ureters, renal arteries (after 12 weeks)
- inability to visualize renal arteries by color duplex
- inability to visualize urine in fetal bladder (after 13 weeks) ← bladder agenesis /
- flattened discoid shape of adrenals ← absence of pressure by kidney
- bell-shaped thorax in mid to late 3rd trimester ← pulmonary hypoplasia
- compression deformities of extremities → clubfoot, flexion contractures, joint dislocations

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** stillbirths (24-38%); invariably fatal in the first days of life ← pulmonary
