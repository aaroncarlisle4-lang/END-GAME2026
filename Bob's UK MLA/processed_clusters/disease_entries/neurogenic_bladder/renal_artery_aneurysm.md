# Disease: RENAL ARTERY ANEURYSM

**Source section:** NEUROGENIC BLADDER

## Key Facts

- **Incidence:** 0.01-0.10%; 22% of visceral aneurysms
- **Age:** 50-61 years; M÷F = 1÷1
- **Location:** R > L; 1st-order branch of main renal artery (usually)

## Imaging Findings

### General

- incomplete / complete ring of calcification
- variable enhancement (depending on amount of thrombus)
- saccular aneurysm close to vascular bifurcations, may calcify
- often bilateral
- multiple small aneurysms in interlobar branches near corticomedullary junction
- inhomogeneous spotty nephrogram

## Clinical

- **Rx:** Endovascular therapy (> 80% success rate)
