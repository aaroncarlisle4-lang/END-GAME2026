# Disease: RENAL CYST

**Source section:** NEUROGENIC BLADDER

## Definition

acquired lesion possibly secondary to tubular obstruction; accounts for 62% of all renal artificial elevation of Hounsfield unit measurements of renal cyst measured on CECT completely homogeneous cyst content ≥ 20 HU (often 50-90 HU) without enhancement generic term for spherical fluid-filled mass intimately attached to renal pelvis without PERIPELVIC CYST = PARAPELVIC LYMPHATIC CYST = PARAPELVIC LYMPHANGIECTASIA multiple small confluent cysts arising from renal sinus protrusion of medial parenchymal cyst into sinus fat

## Key Facts

- **Frequency:** in 1-2% (3-5%) of all urograms (autopsies)
- **Age:** peak incidence after age 30; increasing frequency with age (0.22% in pediatric age
- **Path:** low cuboidal / flattened epithelium surrounded by 1-2-mm thick fibrous wall
- **Histo:** inflammatory cells + granulation tissue + neovascularity ← host reparative response
- **Mean age:** 61 years; in 94% females
- **Prevalence:** 1.5% (autopsies); 4-6% of all renal cysts
- **Etiology:** probably ectatic lymphatic channels from lymphatic obstruction; ?
- **Location:** frequently bilateral

## Imaging Findings

### General

- large and unifocal when peripheral
- focal attenuation + displacement of collecting system
- focally replaced nephrogram with smooth margin
- "beak / claw" sign = effaced wedge of renal parenchyma
- delicate filamentous often undulating septa (10-15%)
- curvilinear calcification (1-3%) in wall / septa
- milk of calcium in dependent portion = always benign
- spherical / ovoid in shape
- anechoic content without internal echoes
- smooth clearly demarcated back wall
- acoustic enhancement of tissue beyond cyst (= positive through transmission) ←
- refraction along sidewalls
- near-water-density lesion (0-20 HU) with thin wall + smooth interface with renal
- no enhancement (< 12 HU)

### MR

- most sensitive modality for cysts < 10 mm
- < 15% enhancement after 0.1 mmol/kg gadolinium on spoiled GE T1WI without fat sat
- smooth wall, clear aspirate with low lactic dehydrogenase, no fat content
- simple fluid attenuation of < 10 HU on NECT images
- simple renal cyst mischaracterized as solid mass
- usually iso- to hyperintense on T1WI ← methemoglobin + hyperintense on T2WI ←
- NO suppression on fat-saturated T1WI
- variable signal intensities (dependent on amount + acuity of hemorrhage, hemoglobin
- hematocrit effect ← settling of RBCs at cyst bottom

### US

- cyst content of low-level echoes / layers of echoes
- cyst septations ± reverberation artifacts
- ± shadowing calcifications
- thickened irregular cyst wall (22%)
- internal septations (11%)
- wall calcification (occasionally)
- minute debris either diffusely / fluid-fluid level in dependent portion of cyst
- amorphous solid conglomerates
- round sharply marginated lesion
- simple cyst on US
- complex cyst on US
- pseudoenhancement (see above)
- soft-tissue density in renal sinus
- anechoic mass(es) with acoustic enhancement
- multiple small renal sinus cysts
- focal displacement + smooth effacement of collecting system
- rarely curvilinear calcification of cyst wall (4%)
- stretching of collecting system when generalized (indistinguishable from sinus
- multiple confluent cysts (mimicking hydronephrosis)

### CT

- fine calcifications ± short segment of slightly thickened calcification in cyst wall /
- ± few hairline-thin septa
- nonenhancing homogeneously hyperattenuating (> 20 HU) lesion measuring ≤ 3 cm
- several thin internal septations without measurable enhancement
- few nodular calcifications
- smooth thickening of cyst wall
- nonenhancing hyperattenuating (> 20 HU) cyst > 3 cm ± completely intrarenal
- thick irregular walls ± numerous septa with measurable enhancement
- ± thick nodular calcifications in cyst wall and septa
- enhancing nodular soft-tissue component
- uni- / multilocular cyst separated by thick septa
- thick fibrous ± calcified wall
- fibrin ball inside cyst (rare)
- increased density ← acute hemorrhage / high protein contents (= hyperattenuating cyst
- no contrast enhancement

### CECT

- high-attenuation contrast fills collecting system but not cyst
- simple cortical renal cyst that extends into renal sinus fat

## Clinical

- **N.B.:** measurement of cyst wall thickness not accurate in a completely intrarenal cyst
- **Rx:** serial follow-up, surgery
- **Prognosis:** NO change on long-term follow-up
