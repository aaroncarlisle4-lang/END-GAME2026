# Disease: RETROPERITONEAL LYMPHANGIOMA

**Source section:** NEUROGENIC BLADDER

## Definition

exceedingly rare tumor usually discovered incidentally RENAL LYMPHANGIECTASIA / LYMPHANGIOMATOSIS exacerbation during pregnancy

## Key Facts

- **Prevalence:** 0.06%
- **Age:** any; peaks during 3rd-6th decade of life
- **Frequency:** rare benign renal tumor (50 cases in literature)
- **Histo:** multiple communicating cysts lined by flattened endothelial cells; multiple septa
- **Location:** renal sinus, perinephric region; (commonly) bilateral

## Imaging Findings

### CT

- thin-walled multilocular cystic lesion near fluid attenuation
- thin septa WITHOUT internal enhancement

### MR

- decreased SI on T1WI + increased intensity on T2WI
- uni- / multilocular cystic mass
- hypointense on T1WI + uniformly hyperintense on T2WI
- variable SI of intracystic fluid (depending on presence of hemorrhage / debris / protein
- increasing perinephric collection, ascites

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** spontaneous regression (occasionally)
