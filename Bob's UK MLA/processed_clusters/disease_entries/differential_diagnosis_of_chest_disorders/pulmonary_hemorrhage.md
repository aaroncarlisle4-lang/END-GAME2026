# Disease: PULMONARY HEMORRHAGE

**Source section:** DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS OF CHEST DISORDERS

## Definition

bleeding from lower respiratory tract

## Imaging Findings

### CXR

- bilateral heterogeneous + homogeneous opacities
- multifocal patchy segmental / lobar consolidation

### HRCT

- bilateral patchy / confluent ground-glass opacities / consolidation
- ground-glass centrilobular nodules
- may delineate underlying etiology (bronchiectasis, lung cancer, TB, pulmonary embolism)

### NUC

- typically matched defect on V/Q scan in bronchial artery bleeding
- bronchial blood flow may increase by 300% in the weeks following pulmonary

### CT

- cluster of avidly enhancing "nodular" bronchial arteries in posterior mediastinum below
- abnormal bronchial arteries show tortuosity, dilatation, hypervascularity, neovascularity,

### ANGIO

- vasodilated enlarged tortuous bronchial arteries → bronchial-to-pulmonary-artery

## Clinical

- **N.B.:** multidetector CT mapping prior to angiography reduces the rate of catheterization
- **Prognosis:** 50-100% mortality rate of conservatively treated massive hemoptysis; death
- **Rx:** transcatheter particulate embolization of bronchial aa. using polyvinyl alcohol (PVA) +
