# Disease: PSEUDOANGIOMATOUS STROMAL HYPERPLASIA

**Source section:** BREAST CYST

## Definition

PASH = benign hormonally stimulated mesenchymal myofibroblastic proliferation of mammary

## Key Facts

- **Histo:** (a) incidental focal microscopic finding in 23% of all breast specimens
- **Age:** 4th-5th decade (range, 14-67 years); frequently in pre- and perimenopausal women
- **Associated with:** gynecomastia in men (in 20-47%); immunosuppressive condition (eg,
- **Path:** well-defined mass with pseudocapsule

## Imaging Findings

### General

- dense well / partially circumscribed noncalcified mass
- rarely indistinct / spiculated border

### US

- hypoechoic solid ovoid mass with long axis parallel to chest wall:
- slightly heterogeneous texture ± echogenic component
- rarely predominantly hyperechoic
- ± circumscribed margins
- ± small cystic component
- usually no posterior phenomena

### MR

- commonly persistent (type 1) or less frequently plateau (type 2) / washout (type 3)

## Clinical

- **Rx:** excision for symptomatic / growing mass
- **Prognosis:** recurrence after excisional biopsy (in 5-18%) requires 1-2 cm excisional
