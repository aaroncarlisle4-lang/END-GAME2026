# Disease: CARCINOMA OF MALE BREAST

**Source section:** BREAST CYST

## Key Facts

- **Incidence:** 1.3÷100,000 per year (in 2000); 2,600 new cases / year with 440 deaths (in 2016);
- **Peak age:** 60-69 (mean, 59) years
- **Histo:** same as in females; invasive ductal carcinoma NOS (80-85%), associated DCIS (35-
- **Location:** L > R breast; bilateral in < 1%

## Imaging Findings

### General

- high-density irregular mass:
- spiculated / lobulated / microlobulated margin resembling scirrhous carcinoma of female
- usually located eccentrically
- microcalcifications (in only 13-30%): fewer + less linear (= more scattered) + more round
- solid nodules / papillary projections arising along a cyst wall (= papillary carcinoma)
- enlarged axillary nodes (in 50% at time of presentation)

### US

- invasive ductal carcinoma appears as a solid hypoechoic subareolar mass of irregular
- metastases to pleura, lung, bone, liver

## Clinical

- **Rx:** simple / modified radical mastectomy + sentinel node biopsy, hormonal manipulation
