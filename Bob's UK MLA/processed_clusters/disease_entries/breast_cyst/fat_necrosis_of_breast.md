# Disease: FAT NECROSIS OF BREAST

**Source section:** BREAST CYST

## Definition

TRAUMATIC LIPID CYST = OIL CYST nonsuppurative saponification of fat by tissue lipase ← local destruction of fat cells with nonsuppurative panniculitis with recurrent bouts of inflammation = areas of fat necrosis,

## Key Facts

- **Etiology:** direct external trauma (seat belt injury), lumpectomy, reduction mammoplasty, implant
- **Prevalence:** 0.5% of breast biopsies
- **Histo:** cavity with oily material surrounded by "foam cells" (= lipid-laden macrophages)

## Imaging Findings

### General

- early: ill-defined irregular spiculated dense mass
- later: well-circumscribed mass with translucent areas at center (= homogeneous fat density of
- calcifies in 4-7% (= liponecrosis macrocystica calcificans):
- occasionally curvilinear / eggshell calcification in wall
- fine spicules of low density vary with projection
- localized skin thickening / retraction possible

### US

- hypo- / anechoic mass with ill- / well-defined margins ± acoustic shadowing
- occasionally echogenic ± acoustic shadowing
- complex cyst with mural nodules / echogenic bands

### MR

- signal intensity nearly isointense to fat
- round lesion with hyperintense fat signal (oil cyst)
- signal loss with macrocalcifications
- localized ill-defined area of moderate enhancement + continuous postinitial increase (=
- rim-enhancement / no enhancement in late lesion / oil cyst
- ill-defined hyperintense area (= reactive edema of fresh lesion)
- round lesion with central SI of fat (in oil cyst)
