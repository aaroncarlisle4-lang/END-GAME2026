# Disease: RADIAL SCAR

**Source section:** BREAST CYST

## Definition

SCLEROSING DUCT HYPERPLASIA = INDURATIVE MASTOPATHY = FOCAL FIBROUS DISEASE = benign proliferative breast lesion (malignant potential is controversial) unrelated to prior

## Key Facts

- **Prevalence:** 0.1-2.0÷1,000 screening mammograms; in 2-16% of mastectomy specimens
- **Path:** "scar" = sclerotic center composed of acellular connective tissue (= fibrosis) and

## Imaging Findings

### General

- mean diameter of 0.33 cm (range, 0.1-0.6 cm)
- typically no central mass (BUT: irregular noncalcified mass often with architectural distortion)
- variable appearance in different projections (= radial scars are typically planar in
- oval / circular translucent areas at center
- very thin long spicules, clumped together centrally
- radiolucent linear structures (= fat) paralleling spicules ("black star" appearance)
- no skin thickening / retraction

### MR

- stellate lesion with SI equal to parenchyma (difficult / impossible to visualize within
- slight to moderate uptake with nonspecific curve
- no characteristic finding

## Clinical

- **Rx:** surgical excision required for definitive diagnosis
