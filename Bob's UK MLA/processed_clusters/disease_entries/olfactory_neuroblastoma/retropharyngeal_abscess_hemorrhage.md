# Disease: RETROPHARYNGEAL ABSCESS / HEMORRHAGE

**Source section:** OLFACTORY NEUROBLASTOMA

## Definition

PREVERTEBRAL ABSCESS spread of infection within retropharyngeal space

## Key Facts

- **Etiology:** tonsillitis, suppurative lymphadenitis, pharyngitis, otitis media, infection of oral
- **Path:** cellulitis > abscess
- **Age:** usually 2-4 (range, 1-6) years (atrophy of nodes with age)

## Imaging Findings

### General

- thickness of retropharyngeal space > ¾ of AP diameter of vertebral body:
- may contain locules of gas / gas-fluid levels
- reversal of cervical lordosis
- anterior bowing / displacement of airway

### US

- hypoechoic areas + adenopathy
- hypoattenuating ovoid fluid collection
- loss of normal bow-tie shape of retropharyngeal space
- complete irregular circumferential rimlike enhancement (HALLMARK)

## Clinical

- **N.B.:** look carefully for possible ingested foreign body
- **Cx:** (1)  Inferior extension through anatomic "danger space" into mediastinum
