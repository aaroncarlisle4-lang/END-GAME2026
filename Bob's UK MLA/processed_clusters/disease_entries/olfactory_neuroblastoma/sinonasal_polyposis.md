# Disease: SINONASAL POLYPOSIS

**Source section:** OLFACTORY NEUROBLASTOMA

## Definition

benign sinonasal mucosal lesions benign antral polyp, which widens the sinus ostium and extends into nasal cavity; 5% of all derivative of choanal polyp (following ischemia of polyp with secondary neovascularity

## Key Facts

- **Frequency:** in 25% of patients with allergic rhinitis;
- **Location:** commonly maxillary antrum
- **Age:** teenagers + young adults

## Imaging Findings

### General

- multiple homogeneous soft-tissue masses with smooth convex borders within nasal cavity
- remodeling / expansion of sinus with enlargement
- thinning of bony trabeculae ± erosive changes at anterior skull base
- usually peripheral / occasionally solid heterogeneous enhancement
- antral clouding
- ipsilateral nasal mass
- smooth mass enlarging the sinus ostium
- NO sinus expansion

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** 75% surgical failure rate
