# Disease: THYROID CARCINOMA

**Source section:** OLFACTORY NEUROBLASTOMA

## Definition

{(thyroid weight [g] x 80-120 µCi/g) π % uptake of 123I by 24 hours} x 100

## Key Facts

- **Incidence:** 25,000 new cancers per year in USA (2005); clinically silent cancers in up to 35%
- **Age:** < 30 years; M÷F = 1÷3 to 1÷2
- **Frequency:** papillary carcinoma (in up to 40-90%) > medullary carcinoma (in up to
- **Peak age:** 5th decade; F > M
- **Histo:** unencapsulated well-differentiated tumor
- **Path:** encapsulated well-differentiated tumor without papillary elements; in 25% multifocal
- **Prevalence:** 2-15%
- **Mean age:** 60 years for sporadic variety;

## Imaging Findings

### General

- hypoechoic / hypoattenuating mass
- irregular ill-defined border without / disrupted halo
- NO hemorrhage / liquefaction necrosis
- ancillary findings:
- lymphadenopathy (in 19%)
- increased size with round bulging shape
- replaced fatty hilum
- heterogeneous echotexture
- irregular margins
- psammomatous calcifications
- cystic change (70% in metastatic papillary carcinoma)
- color Doppler vascularity throughout instead of central hilar vessels only
- destruction of adjacent structures
- loss of fat planes
- distant metastasis

### PET

- well-differentiated thyroid carcinomas are not FDG-avid
- no change from pre-ablation: on suppression therapy
- new foci (in up to 16%): consider therapy
- decreased uptake: may be due to "stunning"

### NUC

- tumor usually concentrates radioiodine (even some purely papillary tumors) → 131I
- usually concentrates pertechnetate, but fails to accumulate 123I
- NO radioiodine uptake
- NO uptake by radioiodine / pertechnetate
- frequently shows increased uptake of 201Tl
- concentrates 123I-MIBG, pentavalent 99mTc-DMSA, 111In-pentetreotide (41% sensitive)
- FDG-PET (96% sensitive)

### US

- psammoma bodies = punctate echogenic foci less than 1 mm in size WITHOUT acoustic
- tumor of decreased echogenicity
- purely solid / complex mass with areas of necrosis, hemorrhage, cystic degeneration
- indistinguishable from benign follicular adenoma
- intrathoracic extension in up to 50%
- ± invasion of carotid artery, internal jugular vein, larynx

### XRAY

- punctate / linear psammomatous calcifications at tumor periphery
- psammoma bodies + stromal calcium deposits

### CT

- mass with inhomogeneous attenuation
- areas of necrosis (74%)
- calcifications (58%)
- regional lymphadenopathy (74%)
- mass of 2-26 mm
- granular calcifications within fibrous stroma / amyloid masses (50%)
- local invasion (common)
- nodal spread to neck + mediastinum (in up to 50%) at time of presentation
- distant metastases to liver, lung, bone (15-25%)
- mass of low attenuation (no iodine concentration)

## Clinical

- **N.B.:** posttherapy scan (1 week after therapeutic dose) identifies more lesions than
- **Cx:** radiation thyroiditis; radiation parotitis; GI symptoms (nausea, diarrhea); minimal
- **Rx:** total thyroidectomy (← commonly multifocal); radioactive iodine ablation
- **Prognosis:** 90-95% 20-year survival; 60% 10-year survival for extrathyroidal cancer;
