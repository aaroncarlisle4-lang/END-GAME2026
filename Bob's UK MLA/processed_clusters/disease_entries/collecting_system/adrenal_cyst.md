# Disease: ADRENAL CYST

**Source section:** COLLECTING SYSTEM

## Key Facts

- **Frequency:** 0.06-0.18% (at autopsy)
- **Age:** 3rd-5th decades (most commonly); M÷F = 1÷3 to 1÷2
- **Path:** (a) vascular / endothelial cyst (45-48%)
- **Location:** mostly solitary; R÷L = 1÷1; bilateral in 8-15%
- **Histo:** lined by cells resembling normal endothelium

## Imaging Findings

### General

- well-circumscribed encapsulated uni- / multilocular lesion with internal septa
- thin wall of < 3 mm in thickness (unless pseudocyst)
- lack of central enhancement ± wall and septal enhancement
- calcifications:

### RADIOGRAPHY

- inferior displacement of ipsilateral kidney

### US

- well-circumscribed hypo- to anechoic lesion

### CT

- well-circumscribed nonenhancing hypoattenuating lesion of < 20 HU (near-water density);

### MR

- uniformly hypointense on T1WI + hyperintense on T2WI
- NO enhancement
- T1 hyperintense signal ← hemorrhage
- ± septations / soft-tissue component ← hemorrhage / hyalinized thrombus
- multilocular thin-walled cyst containing serous / serosanguinous / clear fluid
- scattered / central septal calcifications in 15-30%
- typically unilocular thick-walled cyst > 3.5 mm
- internal scattered echogenicity ← debris / fluid-fluid levels from recent hemorrhage
- peripheral calcifications
- unilocular thin-walled cyst
- variable internal complexity ← daughter vesicles, freed scolices, brood capsules
- septal / mural calcifications
- "water lily" sign
- extra-adrenal foci of hydatid disease

## Clinical

- **Cx:** hypertension; hemorrhage; infection; rupture with retroperitoneal hemorrhage
- **Prognosis:** interval increase in cyst size in 60%
