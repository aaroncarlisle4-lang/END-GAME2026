# Disease: BLADDER EXSTROPHY

**Source section:** COLLECTING SYSTEM

## Definition

EPISPADIA-EXSTROPHY COMPLEX failure of lower abdominal wall + anterior bladder wall to close normally persistent large cloacal membrane without rupture

## Key Facts

- **Prevalence:** 1÷30,000 to 1÷40,000 live births; M > F
- **Etiology:** incomplete retraction of cloacal membrane prevents normal midline migration of

## Imaging Findings

### General

- persistently "absent" urinary bladder (HALLMARK) → urine is released immediately into amniotic cavity
- irregular thickening of everted posterior bladder wall ← inflammation of exposed mucosa
- ventral defect of infraumbilical abdominal wall
- low position of umbilicus
- pubic diastasis = widening of pubic symphysis
- infraumbilical musculoskeletal defect
- subcutaneous position of bladder

## Clinical

- **Cx:** urinary incontinence, infertility, pyelonephritis, bladder carcinoma (4%)
- **Rx:** primary closure, bladder excision with urinary diversion
