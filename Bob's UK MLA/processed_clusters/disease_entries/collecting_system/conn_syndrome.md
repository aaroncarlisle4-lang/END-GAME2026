# Disease: CONN SYNDROME

**Source section:** COLLECTING SYSTEM

## Definition

PRIMARY HYPERALDOSTERONISM = PRIMARY ALDOSTERONISM autonomous excess secretion of the mineralocorticoid aldosterone with hypertension + idiopathic hyperaldosteronism = focal / diffuse hyperplasia of glomerulosa zone

## Key Facts

- **Prevalence:** 2-15% of hypertensive population
- **Age:** 3rd-5th decade; M÷F = 1÷2
- **Location:** L > R; bilateral in 6%

## Imaging Findings

### General

- mass of soft-tissue density / low attenuation
- usually hypervascular, rarely hypovascular
- both glands normal in size ← micronodules
- nodular / multinodular adrenal gland(s) ← hyperplasia

### NUC

- 131I NP-59 uptake following dexamethasone suppression:
- bilateral early visualization (< 5 days) implies adrenal hyperplasia
- unilateral early visualization implies adenoma
- late bilateral visualization (> 5 days) may be normal

## Clinical

- **Rx:** adrenalectomy for neoplasms (75% long-term cure rate for hypertension); medical
