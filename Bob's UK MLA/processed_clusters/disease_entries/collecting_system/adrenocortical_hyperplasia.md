# Disease: ADRENOCORTICAL HYPERPLASIA

**Source section:** COLLECTING SYSTEM

## Definition

ADRENAL CORTICAL HYPERPLASIA MACRONODULAR HYPERPLASIA WITH MARKED ADRENAL ENLARGEMENT

## Key Facts

- **Age:** 70-80% in adults; 19% in children
- **Location:** typically bilateral
- **Prevalence:** increasing with age (autopsy)
- **Histo:** mild nodularity (50%), distinct nodularity (15%)
- **Etiology:** unknown
- **Associated with:** Carney complex

## Imaging Findings

### General

- smooth to slightly lobular thickening of entire adrenal gland while maintaining its
- focal hypo- to isoattenuating micro- / macronodules (= nodules up to 2.5 cm)
- background of normal adrenal tissue / atrophic intervening cortex

### ANGIO

- minimally increased hypervascularity
- focal accumulation of contrast medium
- normal venogram / may show enlarged gland
- adrenal venous sampling to direct therapy in symptomatic patients ← results NOT ALWAYS

### NUC

- asymmetric bilateral NP-59 uptake (related to urinary cortisol excretion) WITHOUT
- bilateral foci of NP-59 uptake WITH dexamethasone suppression (nondiagnostic ≥ 5 days)
- bilateral enlarged adrenal glands (often > 5 cm) with macronodules
- normal to slightly enlarged adrenals + small nodules + atrophy of intervening cortex

## Clinical

- **Rx:** no treatment in the absence of clinical / biochemical evidence of adrenocortical
