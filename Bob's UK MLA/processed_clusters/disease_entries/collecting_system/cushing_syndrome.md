# Disease: CUSHING SYNDROME

**Source section:** COLLECTING SYSTEM

## Definition

HYPERCORTISOLISM excessive glucocorticoid from either exogenous / endogenous sources autonomous cortisol production by adrenal gland

## Key Facts

- **Prevalence:** 1÷1,000 autopsies; M÷F = 1÷4
- **Age:** 30-40 years (highest incidence); more often following pregnancy

## Imaging Findings

### General

- normal adrenal glands
- diffuse adrenal enlargement
- macronodular adrenal hyperplasia (in up to 85%)
- often > 2 cm in size
- atrophy of remaining adrenal tissue ← adenoma suppresses ACTH levels
- multiple small bilateral nodules / normal adrenal
- multiple bilateral macronodules
- retarded bone maturation
- most often axial osteoporosis
- stippled calvarium
- demineralized dorsum sellae
- excess callus formation

- diffuse uniform thickening = Cushing disease
- macronodules = multinodular hyperplasia of long-standing Cushing disease
- large nodules (autonomous ACTH-independent) = massive macronodular hyperplasia
- small nodules = primary pigmented nodular adrenocortical disease
