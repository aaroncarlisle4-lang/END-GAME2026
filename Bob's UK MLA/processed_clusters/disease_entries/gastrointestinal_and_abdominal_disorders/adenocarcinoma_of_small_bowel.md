# Disease: ADENOCARCINOMA OF SMALL BOWEL

**Source section:** GASTROINTESTINAL AND ABDOMINAL DISORDERS

## Definition

rare tumor that typically abuts but spares / only partially involves the major duodenal

## Key Facts

- **Frequency:** 35-40% of primary malignant neoplasms of small bowel; ~ 50 x less common than
- **Histo:** mostly moderately to well differentiated; may arise in villous tumors / de novo; no
- **Prevalence:** 1.7÷1,000,000; M < F
- **Location:** most often in 2nd + 3rd portion = periampullary neoplasm

## Imaging Findings

### General

- variety of shapes:
- annular stricture with "overhanging edges" (60%)
- lobulated / ovoid polypoid sessile mass (41%)
- ulcerated plaque / mass (27%)

### CT

- soft-tissue mass with heterogeneous attenuation
- moderate contrast enhancement
- eccentric duodenal wall thickening ± ulceration:
- annular lesion with relatively short segment involvement
- fungating polypoid / intraluminal mass
- gradual luminal narrowing (most common)
- luminal obstruction + gastric distention ← larger tumor
- ± biliary dilatation
- hypovascular during all phases

## Clinical

- **Cx:** progressive small bowel obstruction; intussusception; perforation (rare)
- **Prognosis:** poor (often disseminated at time of presentation); 30% 5-year survival rate
