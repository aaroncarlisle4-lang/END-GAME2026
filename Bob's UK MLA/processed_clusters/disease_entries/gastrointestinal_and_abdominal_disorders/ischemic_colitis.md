# Disease: ISCHEMIC COLITIS

**Source section:** GASTROINTESTINAL AND ABDOMINAL DISORDERS

## Definition

most common manifestation of ischemic injury to GI tract (50%) characterized by acute onset rare spontaneous potentially life-threatening entity

## Key Facts

- **Age:** usually > 50 years; M = F
- **Associated with:** chronic heart disease, cardiopulmonary bypass surgery, systemic

## Imaging Findings

### General

- segmental thumbprinting = marginal indentations on mesenteric side (rare finding on plain
- thumbprinting (75%) ← submucosal hemorrhage + edema
- transverse ridging = markedly enlarged mucosal folds with some preserved wall pliability
- serrated mucosa ← inflammatory edema + superficial longitudinal / circumferential
- deep penetrating ulcers (late)
- symmetric / lobulated segmental thickening of colonic wall between 2 and 20 (mean 8) mm:
- shaggy configuration + alternating layers of high and low attenuation (= "double halo"
- sharply defined homogeneously enhancing wall + mild mural thickening (dry appearance
- hyperattenuating mucosa in early stage ← hemorrhage
- blood clot in SMA / SMV
- loss of haustral markings
- irregular narrowed atonic lumen (= thumbprinting)
- curvilinear collection of intramural gas (pneumatosis coli in 6%) → suggests bowel
- portal + mesenteric venous gas
- pneumo- and retropneumoperitoneum (= the only PATHOGNOMONIC sign of transmural

### CECT

- hyperenhancing mucosa in early stage ← hyperemia
- reduced / absent enhancement in later stage ← intense vasospasm accompanied by bowel
- absent opacification in SMA / SMV distribution

### US

- circumferential hypoechoic > 3 mm thick bowel wall
- variable loss of mural stratification (= indistinct layers)
- abrupt transition from ischemic to normal bowel
- absent / diminished color flow in bowel wall = indicator of severity of ischemia (82%
- absence of arterial signals; however, blood flow on Doppler may represent reperfusion of
- usually unaltered perienteric fat
- commonly associated with:
- ascites, luminal bowel distention
- pneumatosis intestinalis, gas within portal veins
- normal / slightly attenuated arterial supply
- mild acceleration of arteriovenous transit time
- small tortuous ectatic draining veins
- narrowed foldless segment of several cm in length with smooth tapering margins

### CT

- circumferential cecal wall thickening
- mural stratification pattern ← submucosal edema
- mild pericolic stranding with normal appendix and absence of diverticula
- isolated pneumatosis coli
- mesenteric / portal venous gas
- pneumoperitoneum
