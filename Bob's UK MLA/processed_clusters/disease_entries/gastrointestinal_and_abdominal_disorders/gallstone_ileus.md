# Disease: GALLSTONE ILEUS

**Source section:** GASTROINTESTINAL AND ABDOMINAL DISORDERS

## Definition

gastric outlet obstruction ← impacted gallstone in duodenum / pylorus ← cholecystoenteric

## Key Facts

- **Frequency:** 0.4-5.0% of all intestinal obstructions ← risk increases with age = 20% [24%] of
- **Etiology:** biliary disease (90%), peptic ulcer disease, cancer, trauma
- **Age:** average 65-75 years; M÷F = 1÷4 - 1÷7

## Imaging Findings

### General

- Rigler triad on plain film (in 15%), on CT (in 78%):
- change in position of previously identified gallstone
- smooth polypoid filling defect (= gallstone) at site of obstruction
- well-contained localized barium collection lateral to first portion of duodenum (barium-
- air / enteric contrast in GB (fistula from GB to duodenum)
- thickened duodenal wall
- gallstone at transition point of bowel obstruction

### CT

- obstructing ectopic gallstone in distal stomach / duodenum
- air in gallbladder / biliary tree

## Clinical

- **Cx:** recurrent gallstone ileus in 5-10% (additional silent calculi more proximally)
- **Rx:** removal of stone(s) + cholecystectomy + fistula repair
- **Prognosis:** high mortality of up to 18%
