# Disease: GRAFT-VERSUS-HOST DISEASE

**Source section:** GASTROINTESTINAL AND ABDOMINAL DISORDERS

## Definition

T lymphocytes from donor bone marrow cause selected epithelial damage of recipient target

## Key Facts

- **Frequency:** 30-70% of patients with allogeneic (= donor genetically different from host)
- **Histo:** centrilobular hemorrhagic necrosis, venular obliteration, sinusoidal congestion,
- **Path:** severe mucosal atrophy / destruction
- **Location:** distal ileum > other small bowel > colon

## Imaging Findings

### CT

- moderate bowel wall thickening < 5.5-8.0 mm: small bowel involved in 75-100%
- discontinuous involvement (in 41-54%)
- strictly right colonic involvement (uncommon)
- fluid-filled distended bowel: colon > 8 cm, small bowel > 3 cm
- barium (from prior contrast enema weeks ago) may become incorporated into bowel
- misty mesentery = mesenteric fat stranding without lymphadenopathy
- ascites

### CECT

- abnormally enhancing thin layer of mucosa diffusely involving thickened bowel
- engorgement of vasa recta = "comb" sign

### Skin

- thickening of gallbladder wall ± pericholecystic fluid
- biliary sludge
- dilatation of common bile duct
- enhancement of wall of gallbladder and CBD
- portal hypertension + fluid retention
- painful hepatomegaly

### GI tract

- separation of bowel loops ← wall thickening
- air-fluid levels + small bowel dilatation
- decreased luminal gas
- shaggy fold thickening
- "ribbon bowel" = small bowel fold effacement with tubular appearance (DDx: viral
- "toothpaste / leadpipe / moulage" sign = featureless segment of bowel (chronic
- loss of haustration, spasm, edema, ulceration, granular mucosal pattern of colon
- small bowel "cast" HIGHLY SUGGESTIVE = prolonged coating of abnormal bowel for
- circular collections of contrast material on cross section + parallel tracks on
- severely decreased transit time

## Clinical

- **N.B.:** oral contrast material should not be given!
- **Cx:** infection with opportunistic organisms, eg, Candida albicans, herpes virus, invasive fungal
- **Prognosis:** fatal in up to 15% ← opportunistic infection
- **Rx:** steroids + cyclosporine
