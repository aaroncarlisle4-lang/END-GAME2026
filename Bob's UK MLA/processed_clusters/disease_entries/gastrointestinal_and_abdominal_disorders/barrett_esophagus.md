# Disease: BARRETT ESOPHAGUS

**Source section:** GASTROINTESTINAL AND ABDOMINAL DISORDERS

## Definition

BARRETT SYNDROME progressive replacement of stratified squamous epithelium by metaplastic columnar

## Key Facts

- **Age:** 0-15 years and 40-88 years (mean of 55 years); M > F; mainly among Whites
- **Location:** middle to lower esophagus
- **Distribution:** circumferential / focal

## Imaging Findings

### General

- fine reticular / granular mucosal pattern (3-30%) resembling areae gastricae of the stomach
- netlike web of thin linear / intersecting barium-filled grooves or crevices surrounding
- thickened irregular mucosal folds (28-86%)
- large deep wide-mouthed peptic ulcer (= Barrett ulcer) at upwardly displaced
- uptake of 99mTc-pertechnetate by columnar epithelium
- several-cm-long stricture (71%) in lower esophagus (60%) or upper to midesophagus
- ringlike constrictions / smooth tapered segments of concentric narrowing in
- hiatal hernia (75-94%)
- gastroesophageal reflux (45-63%)
- distal esophageal widening (34-66%) ← abnormal motility
- plaquelike / focal irregularity / nodularity / sessile polyps
