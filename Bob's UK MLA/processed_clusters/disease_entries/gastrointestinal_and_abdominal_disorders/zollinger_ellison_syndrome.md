# Disease: ZOLLINGER-ELLISON SYNDROME

**Source section:** GASTROINTESTINAL AND ABDOMINAL DISORDERS

## Definition

peptic ulcer diathesis ← marked hypersecretion of gastric acid ← gastrin-secreting islet cell

## Key Facts

- **Incidence:** 0.1% of all peptic ulcer disease
- **Age:** middle age; M > F
- **Location:** duodenal bulb (65%) + stomach (20%), jejunum near ligament of Treitz (25%),

## Imaging Findings

### General

- peptic ulcers
- recurrent / intractable ulcers
- marginal ulcers in postgastrectomy patient
- prominence of areae gastricae ← hyperplasia of parietal cells
- enlarged thickened rugal folds in gastric fundus + upper body
- sluggish gastric peristalsis ← ? hypokalemia
- "wet stomach" = dilution of barium with poor coating by excess secretions in nondilated
- gastroesophageal reflux (common) + esophagitis
- dilatation of duodenum + upper small bowel ← fluid overload
- thickened folds in duodenum + jejunum ← edema
- rapid small-bowel transit time
