# Disease: COLONIC VOLVULUS

**Source section:** GASTROINTESTINAL AND ABDOMINAL DISORDERS

## Definition

most common form of volvulus VOLVULUS OF CECUM twisting of cecum along its long axis ← closed-loop obstruction ← excess mobility anteromedial + upward folding of cecum over ascending colon WITHOUT twist VOLVULUS OF SIGMOID COLON

## Key Facts

- **Frequency:** 2% of bowel obstruction; 11% of intestinal volvulus
- **Age:** usually in elderly > 70 years / psychiatrically disturbed

## Imaging Findings

### General

- cecum occupies right lower quadrant
- cecum occupies left upper quadrant
- dilated cecum anterior to ascending colon
- air- or fluid-filled dilated small bowel
- collapsed left colon
- cecal gaseous distension:
- "kidney-shaped" distended cecum inverts + rotates into left upper quadrant (type II)
- dilated cecum rotates upward + anterior to ascending colon without twisting (type III)
- "bird beak" sign = tapering of afferent + efferent bowel loops pointing to point of torsion
- "coffee bean" sign = dilated air- and fluid-filled cecum with haustral creases
- decompressed distal colon
- inability to reflux much contrast material into dilated proximal colon and terminal ileum

### CT

- cecum ectopically positioned in upper mid + left abdomen
- "whirlpool / whirl" sign = twisted loop of bowel with its mesentery + ileocecal vessels
- "coffee bean / bird beak" sign
- "northern exposure" sign = greatly distended paralyzed loop with fluid-fluid levels
- "coffee-bean sign" = distinct midline crease corresponding to mesenteric root of large
- "white stripe" sign = obliquely oriented center line of the U-shaped closed-loop
- "three-line" sign = obliquely oriented outer wall + center lines of the U-shaped closed-
- "bird-of-prey" sign = tapered beaklike distal aspect of twist on barium enema
- enema may help achieve reduction of volvulus
- "whirl" sign = tightly torsed mesentery formed by twisted afferent + efferent loop
- characteristic beaklike tapering at level of twist
- "whirl" sign adjacent to transverse colon

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** highest mortality of all cases of colonic volvulus ← rare + unsuspected
