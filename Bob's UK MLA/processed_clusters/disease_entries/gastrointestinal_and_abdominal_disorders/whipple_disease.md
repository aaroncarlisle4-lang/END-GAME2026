# Disease: WHIPPLE DISEASE

**Source section:** GASTROINTESTINAL AND ABDOMINAL DISORDERS

## Definition

INTESTINAL LIPODYSTROPHY sporadically occurring chronic bacterial multisystem disease

## Key Facts

- **Etiology:** infection with gram-positive bacterium (Tropheryma whipplei) closely related to
- **Incidence:** 30 cases annually
- **Genetics:** whites of European heritage predisposed → colonization of bacterium throughout GI
- **Histo:** PAS-positive material (periodic acid Schiff) = glycoprotein (from bacterial cell wall)
- **Mean age:** 50 years (range, 4th-6th decade); M÷F = 8÷1; Caucasians
- **Location:** mesial temporal lobe, midbrain, thalamus, hypothalamus

## Imaging Findings

### CT

- bulky 3-4-cm low-density lymph nodes in mesenteric root + retroperitoneum ←
- thickening of small bowel wall
- hepatosplenomegaly
- ascites
- pneumatosis intestinalis
- pleuropericarditis
- sacroiliitis

### US

- echogenic lymph nodes

### Abdomen

- moderate irregular diffuse fold thickening of jejunum + duodenum + (to a lesser degree)
- micronodularity (= swollen villi) + wild mucosal pattern
- hypersecretion, segmentation, fragmentation (occasionally if accompanied by
- NO / minimal distension of small bowel
- NO rigidity of folds, NO ulcerations
- NORMAL transit time
- hepatosplenomegaly
- mesenteric and retroperitoneal nodes of low attenuation are CHARACTERISTIC of Whipple

### CNS

- patchy nodular enhancement
- hyperintense regions on FLAIR ← atrophy / gliosis

## Clinical

- **Rx:** long-term broad-spectrum antibiotics (tetracycline) with 17-35% relapse rate; long-term
