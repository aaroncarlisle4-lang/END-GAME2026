# Disease: ANORECTAL MALFORMATION

**Source section:** GASTROINTESTINAL AND ABDOMINAL DISORDERS

## Definition

complex group of congenital anomalies involving distal anus, rectum, urinary and genital

## Key Facts

- **Prevalence:** 1÷5,000 live births; M > F

## Imaging Findings

### General

- oligohydramnios (26%) + distended bladder
- abdominal / pelvic cystic mass (52%) ← distended bladder + septate fluid-filled vagina
- fetal hydronephrosis (49%)
- fetal ascites (22%)
- intestinal distension (18%)
- bowel-skin distance on midline SAG (rectal pouch to anus)
- linear tract / fistula may be identified

### MR

- position of rectal pouch
- size + morphology + development of levator ani muscle group (levator prostate m. /
- low small bowel / colonic obstruction
- "M" line accurately represents level of puborectal muscle = line drawn horizontally

## Clinical

- **Rx:** corrective surgery on day 2-3 of life ± colostomy
