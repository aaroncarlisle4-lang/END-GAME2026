# Disease: FOREIGN BODY INGESTION

**Source section:** GASTROINTESTINAL AND ABDOMINAL DISORDERS

## Key Facts

- **Location:** predominantly in ileocecal region, rectosigmoid, areas of bowel

## Imaging Findings

### CT

- wood difficult to recognize:
- air attenuation in acute phase mimicking bubbles of gas
- becoming progressively isoattenuating in subacute phase
- hyperattenuating ← granulomatous reaction
- calcified in chronic phase
- ± pneumoperitoneum (rare ← progressive impaction of foreign body in intestinal wall

## Clinical

- **Cx:** (1)  Perforation(< 1%): fish / chicken bone, toothpick
- **Rx:** most foreign bodies are amenable to endoscopic removal
