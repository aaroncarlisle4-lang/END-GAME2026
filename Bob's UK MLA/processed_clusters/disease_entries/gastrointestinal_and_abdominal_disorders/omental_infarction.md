# Disease: OMENTAL INFARCTION

**Source section:** GASTROINTESTINAL AND ABDOMINAL DISORDERS

## Definition

rare cause of self-limiting abdominal pain

## Key Facts

- **Path:** hemorrhagic infarction ← vascular compromise ← (a) tenuous blood supply to right
- **Age:** pediatric patients
- **Location:** commonly RLQ on inferior right side of omentum
- **Size:** diameter > 5 cm

## Imaging Findings

### CT

- solitary encapsulated triangular / oval fatty mass:
- soft-tissue stranding adjacent to ascending colon = haziness of fat anterior to colon (early
- internal heterogeneity ← soft-tissue stranding adjacent to ascending colon
- whorled pattern of concentric linear fat stranding (in torsion)
- NO enhancement
- omental torsion (rare) with swirling of vessels in omentum
- absent / (rarely) mild thickening of adjacent bowel wall corresponding to site of focal

### US

- nonmobile noncompressible hyperechoic tender mass

### PET

- mild uptake of 18F-fluorodeoxyglucose (FDG)

## Clinical

- **Cx:** omental abscess (rare)
- **Rx:** supportive
