# Disease: POSTINFLAMMATORY POLYPOSIS

**Source section:** GASTROINTESTINAL AND ABDOMINAL DISORDERS

## Definition

PSEUDOPOLYPOSIS reepithelialized inflammatory polyps as sequelae of mucosal ulceration

## Key Facts

- **Etiology:** ulcerative colitis (10-20%); granulomatous colitis (less frequent); schistosomiasis
- **Location:** most common in left hemicolon, may occur in stomach / small intestine

## Imaging Findings

### General

- sessile + frondlike appearance (often)
- filiform polyposis = multiple wormlike projections only attached at their bases

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** NO malignant potential
