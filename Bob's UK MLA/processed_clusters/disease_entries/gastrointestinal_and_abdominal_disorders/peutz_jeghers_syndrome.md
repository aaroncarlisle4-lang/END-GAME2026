# Disease: PEUTZ-JEGHERS SYNDROME

**Source section:** GASTROINTESTINAL AND ABDOMINAL DISORDERS

## Definition

rare syndrome characterized by intestinal polyposis and mucocutaneous pigmentation (= 1-5-mm small elongated melanin spots on mucous membranes (lower lips, gums, palate) +

## Key Facts

- **Genetics:** autosomal dominant with incomplete penetrance; often spontaneous mutations
- **Frequency:** 1÷7,000 live births; in 50% familial, in 50% sporadic; most frequent of polyposis
- **Age:** at presentation 25 (range 10-30) years; M÷F = 1÷1
- **Path:** multiple small sessile / large pedunculated polyps
- **Histo:** benign hamartomatous (?) polyp with a smooth muscle core arising from muscularis
- **Mean age:** 40 years of age
- **Location:** stomach, duodenum, colon; small bowel (least common)

## Imaging Findings

### General

- multiple usually broad-based polyps separated by wide areas of intervening flat mucosa
- multilobulated surface of larger polyps
- myriad of 1-2-mm nodules of up to several cm = carpet of polyps
- intussusception usually confined to small bowel

### Colon + rectum

- multiple scattered 1-30-mm polyps; NO carpeting

### Stomach + duodenum

- diffuse involvement with multiple polypoid lesions:
- small / large, sessile / pedunculated polyps

### Extraintestinal sites: kidney, ureter, gallbladder, bronchial tree, nasal passages

- adenoma of bronchus + urinary bladder

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** decreased life expectancy (risk of cancer approaching 40% by 40 years of age)
