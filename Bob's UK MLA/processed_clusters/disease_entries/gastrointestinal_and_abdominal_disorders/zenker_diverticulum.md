# Disease: ZENKER DIVERTICULUM

**Source section:** GASTROINTESTINAL AND ABDOMINAL DISORDERS

## Definition

PHARYNGOESOPHAGEAL DIVERTICULUM outpouching of posterior hypopharyngeal wall = pulsion diverticulum with herniation of

## Key Facts

- **Prevalence:** 0.01-0.11% (overall); higher in elderly women
- **Etiology:** cricopharyngeal dysfunction (cricopharyngeal achalasia / premature closure) →
- **Associated with:** hiatal hernia, gastroduodenal ulcer, midesophageal diverticulum, esophageal
- **Age:** > 60 years (50% occur in 7th-8th decade); M÷F = 1÷3

## Imaging Findings

### General

- posterior + inferior barium extension in upper half of semilunar depression on posterior
- barium-filled sac extending caudally behind + usually to left of esophagus
- partial / complete obstruction of esophagus from external pressure of sac contents →
- partial barium reflux from diverticulum into hypopharynx
- continual growth with successive enlargement

### CXR

- air-fluid level in superior mediastinum

## Clinical

- **Cx:** overflow aspiration pneumonia (30%); esophageal perforation; carcinoma (0.48%)
- **Rx:** surgical excision + cricopharyngeal myotomy
