# Disease: FIBROVASCULAR POLYP OF ESOPHAGUS

**Source section:** GASTROINTESTINAL AND ABDOMINAL DISORDERS

## Definition

HAMARTOMA = LIPOMA = FIBROMA = FIBROLIPOMA = FIBROMYXOMA = FIBROEPITHELIAL POLYP intraluminal esophageal polyp

## Key Facts

- **Prevalence:** 1-2% of benign esophageal tumors
- **Path:** elongated intraluminal mass of fibrous + adipose tissue of varying proportions covered
- **Age:** 53 (range, 1-88) years; M > F
- **Size:** 7 cm long at time of presentation extending inferiorly as far as into the stomach

## Imaging Findings

### General

- giant sausage-shaped intraluminal mass originating from cervical esophagus (pedicle
- heterogeneous mass with areas of soft-tissue and fat attenuation
- ± punctate calcifications (occasionally)

## Clinical

- **Cx:** asphyxia + sudden death ← after regurgitation of polyp into pharynx / mouth
- **Rx:** surgical removal (10% recurrence)
