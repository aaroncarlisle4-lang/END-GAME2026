# Disease: SCHATZKI RING

**Source section:** GASTROINTESTINAL AND ABDOMINAL DISORDERS

## Definition

LOWER ESOPHAGEAL MUCOSAL RING constant lower esophageal ring (mucosal thickening) presumed to result from reflux

## Key Facts

- **Frequency:** 6-14% of population; old age > young age; M > F
- **Histo:** usually squamous epithelium on upper surface + columnar epithelium on undersurface;
- **Associated with:** hiatal hernia (typical)

## Imaging Findings

### General

- nondistensible concentric transverse ring of shelflike projection with smooth symmetric
- constant shape + size
- visible only with adequate distension of esophagogastric region and when located above the
- best demonstrated in prone position during arrested deep inspiration with Valsalva maneuver
- short esophagus + intrahiatal / intrathoracic gastric segment = sliding hiatal hernia if Schatzki

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** decrease in caliber over 5 years (in 25-33%)
- **Cx:** impaction of food bolus (associated with severe chest pain)
