# Disease: HEMANGIOMA OF GASTROINTESTINAL TRACT

**Source section:** GASTROINTESTINAL AND ABDOMINAL DISORDERS

## Definition

unusual single / multiple vascular tumors anywhere in GI tract

## Key Facts

- **Frequency:** 7-10% of all benign small bowel tumors
- **Age:** any; commonly during infancy / early childhood
- **Path:** submucosal soft infiltrative polypoid mass
- **Histo:** cavernous / capillary / mixed
- **Location:** jejunum (55%), ileum (42%), duodenum (2%); rare in stomach + colon (mostly

## Imaging Findings

### General

- polypoid intramural intraluminal subepithelial mass
- sessile nodular compressible intraluminal filling defect
- segmental transmural circumferential bowel wall thickening = diffuse infiltrating abnormality
- ± virtually PATHOGNOMONIC phleboliths in intestinal wall ← calcified thrombi

### CECT

- well-circumscribed avidly enhancing often intraluminal lobulated mass of mixed
- slow diffuse enhancement + vascular engorgement within adjacent mesentery around mass

## Clinical

- **Rx:** resection ← risk of massive gastrointestinal bleeding
- **Prognosis:** involution during childhood
