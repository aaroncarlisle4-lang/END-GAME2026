# Disease: RADIATION INJURY

**Source section:** GASTROINTESTINAL AND ABDOMINAL DISORDERS

## Definition

obliterative endarteritis with irradiation in excess of 4,000-4,500 rad from high doses of mediastinal radiation (usually > 5,000 cGy); Adriamycin® can

## Key Facts

- **Frequency:** 5%; increased risk after pelvic surgery
- **Location:** ileum; concomitant radiation damage to colon / rectum

## Imaging Findings

### General

- radiographic changes within field of radiation only
- smooth relatively long segment of concentric tapered narrowing within a preexisting
- gastric ulceration + deformity (pylorus)
- enlargement + effacement of gastric folds
- antral narrowing + rigidity (similar to linitis plastica)

### CT

- increased attenuation of mesentery
- ridgelike appearance of mucosa ← submucosal fibrosis
- irregularly outlined ulcerations (rare)
- narrowed partially distensible rectum
- thick homogeneous rectal wall
- "target" sign = submucosal circumferential lucency
- proliferation of perirectal fat > 10 mm
- thickening of perirectal fascia
- "halo" sign = increase in pararectal fibrosis

### ACUTE radiation enteropathy

- mucosal hyperenhancement
- irregular nodular thickening of folds with straight transverse course ± ulcerations
- separation of adjacent bowel loops by > 2 mm

### CHRONIC radiation enteropathy (after months to years)

- thickened bowel wall with luminal narrowing
- multiple strictures + partial mechanical obstruction
- serrated bowel margin
- shortening of small bowel
- fixation + immobilization of bowel loops with similar radiographic appearance
