# Disease: DUODENAL DIVERTICULUM

**Source section:** GASTROINTESTINAL AND ABDOMINAL DISORDERS

## Definition

congenital lesion ← elongation of an incomplete duodenal diaphragm

## Key Facts

- **Frequency:** 1-5% of GI studies; 22% of autopsies
- **Location:** almost invariably in 1st portion of duodenum

## Imaging Findings

### General

- may be fluid-filled (mimicking pancreatic pseudocyst)
- may contain foci of air (mimicking pancreatic abscess)
- contains normal bowel gas / oral contrast material directly communicating with adjacent
- barium-filled sac within duodenal lumen of "windsock, comma, teardrop" appearance
- anchored to lateral wall of duodenum
- "halo" sign = duodenal mucosa covers outer + inner wall of diverticulum
- fluid- / contrast material filled duodenum with thin diverticular wall on CT / MR
