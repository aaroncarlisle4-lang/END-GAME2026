# Disease: CONGENITAL INTESTINAL ATRESIA

**Source section:** GASTROINTESTINAL AND ABDOMINAL DISORDERS

## Key Facts

- **Prevalence:** 1÷300 livebirths
- **Location:** jejunum + ileum (70%), duodenum (25%), colon (5%); may involve multiple sites

## Imaging Findings

### General

- "triple bubble" sign = intraluminal gas in stomach + duodenal bulb + proximal jejunum as
- "bulbous bowel segment" sign with curvilinear termination = dilated loop of bowel just
- gasless lower abdomen: gut usually air-filled by 4 h after birth
- meconium peritonitis (6%)
- polyhydramnios (in 50% ← duodenal / proximal jejunal atresia; rarely in ileal / colonic

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** 88% survival for isolated atresia
