# Disease: PSEUDOMYXOMA PERITONEI

**Source section:** GASTROINTESTINAL AND ABDOMINAL DISORDERS

## Definition

"jelly belly" = "gelatinous ascites" slow insidious accumulation of large amounts of thick mucinous or gelatinous material on benign / borderline / low-grade malignant epithelial cells from ruptured mucocele of the mucinous tumor, cystadenoma, tumor with low malignant potential, invasive carcinoma

## Key Facts

- **Incidence:** 1÷1,000,000 annually
- **Mean age:** 49 (range, 23-83) years; M< F

## Imaging Findings

### General

- thickening of peritoneal + omental surfaces
- omental cake
- posterior fixation of bowel loops + mesentery
- voluminous septated / loculated pseudoascites
- several thin-walled cystic masses of different size throughout abdominal cavity:
- Hellmer sign = focal collection of mucin in right subhepatic space displacing tip of liver
- central displacement of ascending + descending colon and lateral displacement of
- central displacement of intestine
- scalloped contour of liver + spleen
- annular / semicircular calcifications (rare but highly suggestive)

### CT

- intraperitoneal collection of very low attenuation (common) / soft-tissue density (rare)
- may contain enhancing septa ± calcifications
- discrete hypoattenuating masses (infrequent)

### US

- hypoechoic collection (common) / more solid appearance (rare)
- nonmobile echoes from proteinaceous / bloody / fibrinous exudate
- echogenic septations

## Clinical

- **Rx:** amenable to surgical debulking
- **Prognosis:** 50% 5-year survival
- **Cx:** bowel obstruction
