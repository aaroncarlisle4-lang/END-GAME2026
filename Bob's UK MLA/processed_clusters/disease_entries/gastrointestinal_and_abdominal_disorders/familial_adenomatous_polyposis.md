# Disease: FAMILIAL ADENOMATOUS POLYPOSIS

**Source section:** GASTROINTESTINAL AND ABDOMINAL DISORDERS

## Definition

FAMILIAL MULTIPLE POLYPOSIS

## Key Facts

- **Frequency:** 1÷7,000 to 1÷24,000 live births
- **Genetics:** autosomal dominant disease with 80% penetrance; gene localized on chromosome 5;
- **Path:** tubular / villotubular adenomatous polyps; usually about 1,000 adenomas
- **Age:** polyps appear around puberty

## Imaging Findings

### General

- "carpet of polyps" = myriad of 2-3 mm (up to 2 cm) polypoid lesions

### Colon (100%): more numerous in distal colon; always affecting rectum

- normal haustral pattern

## Clinical

- **Cx:** malignant transformation of adenomas: colon > stomach > small bowel in 12% [30%]
- **Rx:** prophylactic total colectomy in late teens / early twenties before symptoms develop
