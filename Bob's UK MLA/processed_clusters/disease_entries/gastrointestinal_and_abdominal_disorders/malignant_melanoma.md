# Disease: MALIGNANT MELANOMA

**Source section:** GASTROINTESTINAL AND ABDOMINAL DISORDERS

## Definition

develops from melanocytes derived from neural crest cells, arising in preexisting benign nevi

## Key Facts

- **Frequency:** 1% of all cancers; increasing at 3.9% annually
- **Prevalence:** 30-40% at autopsy
- **Location:** axial skeleton (80%), ribs (38%)

## Imaging Findings

### Lymphadenopathy

- intraoperative intradermal injection of dye
- preoperative lymphoscintigraphy

### Bone (7-17%)

- predominantly osteolytic
- hypo- / isointense on T1WI + T2WI + STIR images (most commonly)
- hyperintense on T1WI + hypointense on T2WI ← T1-shortening effect of the

### Lung (70% at autopsy): most common site of relapse

- single / multiple lesions 0.5-15 cm in size
- larger lesion often necrotic
- may be partially calcified

### Spleen (1-5%; 33% at autopsy)

- single / multiple lesions of variable size
- solid / cystic

### GI tract + mesentery (4-8%)

- multiple hypervascular subepithelial nodules:
- "bull's-eye / target" appearance on BE = central ulceration ← tendency to outgrow
- localized to antimesenteric border of bowel wall ← embolic metastases to intramural
- irregular amorphous cavity (exoenteric growth) in larger lesion ← necrosis, hemorrhage,
- intussusception (10-20%)

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** 30-40% eventually die from this tumor
