# Disease: EOSINOPHILIC GASTROENTEROPATHY

**Source section:** GASTROINTESTINAL AND ABDOMINAL DISORDERS

## Definition

manifestation of immune-mediated inflammation (allergy) in genetically susceptible uncommon occasionally self-limited form of gastroenteritis with remissions + non-IgE-mediated allergic disease

## Key Facts

- **Incidence:** 1÷10,000 [2-6÷10,000] annually in children [adults]
- **Prevalence:** 4÷10,000 [27÷10,000] in child [adult]
- **Peak age:** 3rd + 4th decade; M÷F = 3÷1
- **Histo:** PATHOGNOMONIC infiltration of squamous epithelium with > 15-20 eosinophils per
- **Location:** mid / distal esophagus
- **Associated with:** hiatal hernia, reflux esophagitis
- **Age:** infants, young adults

## Imaging Findings

### General

- smooth long-segment stricture without ulceration + mild mucosal irregularity (most
- "ringed esophagus" = multiple fixed rings appearing as closely stacked indentations on

### CT

- diffuse mural thickening (nonspecific)
- occasionally impacted food

### Small bowel

- separation of small bowel loops
- mucosal fold thickening, polyps, nodular thickening, ulcers
- "araneid limblike" sign = "spider leg" appearance ← diffuse mucosal thickening with
- "halo" sign = layered appearance of bowel wall ← submucosal edema
- strictures, bowel wall thickening, intestinal obstruction
- concentric hypoattenuating bowel wall thickening
- reduced distensibility + narrowing of lumen
- motility disturbance → small-bowel obstruction
- effacement of mucosal pattern
- ascites with peripheral eosinophilia
- pleural effusion
- peritoneal stranding, omental thickening
- mesenteric lymphadenopathy with central necrosis
- clustering of small bowel loops
- serosal enhancement

### Stomach (80%; almost always limited to antrum)

- "wet stomach"
- enlarged gastric rugae / cobblestone nodules / polyps
- ulcers (rare)
- thickened + rigid wall with narrowed gastric antrum / pylorus
- bulky intraluminal mass up to 9 cm in size
- volvulus, intussusception, perforation
- ascites
- segmental / diffuse colonic wall thickening, wall edema
- pericolonic fat stranding

## Clinical

- **Cx:** spontaneous esophageal rupture
- **Prognosis:** tendency toward spontaneous remission (in ⅓) with frequent relapse
- **Rx:** corticosteroids / removal of sensitizing agent; dietary modification
