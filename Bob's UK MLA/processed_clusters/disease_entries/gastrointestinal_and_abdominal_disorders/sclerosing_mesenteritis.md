# Disease: SCLEROSING MESENTERITIS

**Source section:** GASTROINTESTINAL AND ABDOMINAL DISORDERS

## Definition

CHRONIC FIBROSING MESENTERITIS = MULTIFOCAL SUBPERITONEAL SCLEROSIS = SCLEROSING rare idiopathic disorder characterized by tumorlike masses in mesentery composed of chronic degeneration and necrosis of mesenteric fat predominantly chronic inflammatory stage of mesenteritis predominantly fibrotic stage of mesenteritis

## Key Facts

- **Etiology:** ? trauma, previous surgery, ischemia
- **Path:** solitary / multifocal well-demarcated / diffusely infiltrating (mesenteric thickening)
- **Histo:** chronic mesenteric inflammation with lipid-laden macrophages (= lipophages) +
- **Location:** root of small bowel mesentery, colonic mesocolon

## Imaging Findings

### General

- soft-tissue mass with calcifications
- ± thumbprinting ← vascular congestion
- SBO: intestinal dilatation, fluid levels, fluid-filled bowel
- compression / distortion of duodenum near ligament of Treitz
- separation of small bowel loops with fixation + kinking + angulation ← shortening +
- irregular fold thickening of small bowel ← reactive mural thickening
- narrowing + thumbprinting of colon (occasionally)

### CT

- well-demarcated / ill-defined mesenteric mass:
- heterogeneous texture of fat density:
- misty attenuation interspersed with
- soft-tissue density (= fibrous tissue)
- single mesenteric root mass (= fibroma)
- multiple nodules throughout mesentery (= fibromatosis)
- multiple cystic masses in mesentery (= loose myxomatous composition / lymphatic
- tumoral pseudocapsule = peripheral band of soft-tissue attenuation at the edge of
- ± small < 5 mm lymph nodes within lesion
- "fat ring" sign = preservation of fat as a halo of low attenuation surrounding nondisplaced
- punctate / coarse calcifications (uncommon)
- mesenteric thickening with fine stellate pattern (= radiating strands of fibrosis) extending to
- retraction of small bowel loops (= desmoplastic response)
- rarely associated with retroperitoneal lymphadenopathy

### US

- heterogeneous mass with hypo- and hyperechoic features

### MR

- low to intermediate SI on T1WI + very low signal on T2WI
- high SI on T2WI (= predominant myxomatous change)
- ± chylous ascites
- "misty mesentery" = mesenteric fat stranding
- narrowing of bowel loop with spiculations → intestinal obstruction
