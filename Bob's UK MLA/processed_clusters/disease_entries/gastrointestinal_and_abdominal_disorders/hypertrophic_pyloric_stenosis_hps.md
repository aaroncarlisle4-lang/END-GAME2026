# Disease: HYPERTROPHIC PYLORIC STENOSIS (HPS

**Source section:** GASTROINTESTINAL AND ABDOMINAL DISORDERS

## Definition

idiopathic hypertrophy and hyperplasia of circular muscle fibers of pylorus with proximal TORUS HYPERPLASIA localized muscle hypertrophy on lesser curvature milder atypical form of HPS

## Key Facts

- **Etiology:** inherited as a dominant polygenic trait; increased incidence in firstborn boys;
- **Age:** manifestation between 3rd-12th week of life; peak presentation during 4th week
- **Frequency:** 3÷1,000 births; M÷F = 4-5÷1; 5 x fold increase in 1st-degree relatives

## Imaging Findings

### General

- fluid-filled distended stomach after 2 hours of fasting
- little bowel gas
- elongation + narrowing of pyloric canal (2-4 cm in length):
- "string" sign = passing of small barium streak through elongated pyloric channel
- "double / triple track" sign = crowding of mucosal folds in pyloric channel
- Twining recess = "diamond" sign = transient triangular tentlike cleft / niche in
- abnormal configuration of antrum:
- "pyloric teat" = outpouching along lesser curvature due to disruption of antral
- "shoulder" sign = impression of hypertrophied muscle on distended gastric antrum
- "antral beaking" = mass impression upon antrum with streak of barium pointing
- "olive pit" sign = impression of pyloric muscle upon antrum with a tiny amount of
- Kirklin sign = "mushroom" sign = indentation of base of duodenal bulb (in 50%)
- gastric distension with fluid
- active gastric hyperperistalsis:
- "caterpillar" sign = gastric hyperperistaltic waves
- "target" sign = hypoechoic ring of hypertrophied pyloric muscle around echogenic
- elongated pylorus with thickened muscle:
- pyloric muscle wall thickness ≥ 3.0 -3.2 mm
- pyloric volume > 1.4 cm3 (= ¼ π x [maximum pyloric diameter]2 x pyloric length);
- pyloric length (mm) + 3.64 x muscle thickness (mm) ≥ 25 mm
- pyloric transverse diameter ≥ 13 mm with pyloric channel closed
- elongated pyloric canal ≥ 16-17 mm in length
- "cervix" sign = indentation of muscle mass on fluid-filled antrum on longitudinal section
- "antral nipple" sign = redundant pyloric channel mucosa protruding into gastric antrum
- exaggerated peristaltic waves
- delayed gastric emptying of fluid into duodenum
- variable muscle thickness between 1.5 and 3.0 mm
- variable caliber of antral narrowing
- antral peristalsis
- delayed gastric emptying (assure adequate length of real-time observation)
- elongation of pylorus
- gastric content / fluid passes through pyloric channel
- circumferential / eccentric thickening of antral mucosa > 2-3 mm
- distended descending duodenum
- reversal of SMA and SMV relationship
- "whirlpool" sign = twisting of small bowel mesentery
- persistent elongation (2-4 cm) + concentric narrowing of pyloric channel
- parallel + preserved mucosal folds
- antispasmodics show no effect on narrowing
- proximal benign ulcer (74%), usually near incisura
- flattening of distal lesser curvature

## Clinical

- **Cx:** hypochloremic metabolic alkalosis
- **Rx:** pyloromyotomy
- **Prognosis:** 2% mortality
