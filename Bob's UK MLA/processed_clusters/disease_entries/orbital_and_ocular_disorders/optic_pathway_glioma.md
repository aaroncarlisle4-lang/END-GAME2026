# Disease: OPTIC PATHWAY GLIOMA

**Source section:** ORBITAL AND OCULAR DISORDERS

## Definition

JUVENILE PILOCYTIC ASTROCYTOMA = OPTIC NERVE GLIOMA isolated to single prechiasmatic optic nerve anywhere along optic tract ± extension to other low-grade glial neoplasm with cystic components

- **Frequency:** 1% of all intracranial tumors; 4% of orbital masses; 80% of primary tumors of
- **Path:** optic nerve is embryologically part of hypothalamus and develops gliomas instead of
- **Histo:** proliferation of well-differentiated astrocytes
- **Mean age:** 4-5 years; 1st decade (75%); rare in adults without NF1 (GBM); M÷F = 1÷2 to 2÷3
- **Associated with:** neurofibromatosis type 1 (NF1) in 10-33-50% (± bilateral optic gliomas)
- **Prevalence:** extremely rare; 30 cases in this century
