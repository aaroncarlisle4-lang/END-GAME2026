# Disease: OPTIC NERVE SHEATH MENINGIOMA

**Source section:** ORBITAL AND OCULAR DISORDERS

## Definition

PERIOPTIC MENINGIOMA

## Key Facts

- **Frequency:** 2% of space-occupying orbital masses; < 2% of intracranial meningiomas
- **Mean age:** 49 years; M÷F = 1÷4; slightly more aggressive in children
- **Histo:** syncytial growth pattern composed of meningioma cells with indistinct cytoplasmic

## Imaging Findings

### General

- tubular (most common) / fusiform / eccentric (excrescentic) thickening of optic nerve
- calcifications in 20-50% (HIGHLY SUGGESTIVE)
- sphenoid bone hyperostosis

### US

- hypoechoic tumor with irregular border

### CECT

- tumor enhancement around nonenhancing optic n.:
- "tram-track" configuration on axial CECT = enhancing tumor on either side of spared
- ringlike / doughnut configuration on coronal CECT
- linear high-attenuation area parallel to optic nerve (= tumor spread along subarachnoid
- bone remodeling ± enlargement of optic canal
- minimal extension into optic canal (not uncommon)
- hypointense to fat on T1WI
- iso- to slightly hyperintense relative to optic nerve on T2WI
- "tram-track" sign = intensely enhancing extrinsic soft-tissue mass surrounding optic nerve

## Clinical

- **Rx:** surgical intervention to prevent disease dissemination to contralateral eye if disease
