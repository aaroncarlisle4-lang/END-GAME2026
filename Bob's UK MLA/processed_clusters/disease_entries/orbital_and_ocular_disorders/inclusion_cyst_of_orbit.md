# Disease: INCLUSION CYST OF ORBIT

**Source section:** ORBITAL AND OCULAR DISORDERS

## Definition

congenital unilocular closed sac containing derivatives of single germ layer (monodermal) DERMOID INCLUSION CYST

## Key Facts

- **Etiology:** failure of surface ectoderm to separate completely from underlying developing neural
- **Prevalence:** 5% of all orbital masses
- **Age:** 1st decade; < ¼ diagnosed at birth
- **Location:** in anterior extraconal orbit in close relation to frontozygomatic suture = upper
- **Histo:** thick vascularized capsule composed of stratified squamous epithelium identical to
- **Path:** white "pearly" tumor consisting of dry waxy / flaky keratin ← desquamated inner

## Imaging Findings

### General

- thick surrounding capsule ± enhancement
- ± expansion / erosion of bony orbit:
- periosteal and intradiploic remodeling of bone
- reactive sclerotic margin / shell of dense bone

### US

- encapsulated heterogeneous mass with variable cystic component

### CT

- encapsulated well-circumscribed unilocular cystic mass:
- ± fat-density of −60 to −90 HU
- ± high-attenuation foci in wall ← calcification

### MR

- high signal intensity on T1WI + T2WI ← lipids of sebum
- ↓ SI of lipid on fat-suppressed images
- water / CSF density ← high proteinaceous content
- high SI on T2WI
- internal signal NOT suppressed on FLAIR
- ↑ signal intensity on DWI + ↓ ADC map
