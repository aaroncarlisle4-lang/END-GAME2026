# Disease: INFANTILE FIBROMATOSIS OF ORBIT

**Source section:** ORBITAL AND OCULAR DISORDERS

## Definition

rare nonmetastasizing locally invasive fibrous proliferation with tendency to recur after

## Key Facts

- **Age:** congenital (54%), < 2 years of age (in 89%)
- **Location:** head & neck > trunk > lower extremity > upper extremity

## Imaging Findings

### XRAY

- nonspecific expansion of orbit

### US

- round well-circumscribed mass of heterogeneous texture
- ± target appearance ← central necrosis / hemorrhage

### CT

- ± orbital bone erosion ± small foci of calcifications
- enhancing mass hyperattenuating to muscle

### MR

- multilobulated / infiltrative margins
- low to intermediate SI relative to muscle on T1WI + (frequently) foci of hyperintensity
- heterogeneous predominantly hyperintense SI on T2WI:
- T2 hyperintensity ← high cellularity + myxoid change
- T2 hypointensity ← high collagen content
- target appearance ← central hemorrhage / necrosis / cystic / myxoid change
- intense diffuse / peripheral enhancement

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** spontaneous resolution (common); 7% recurrence rate after surgical resection
- **Rx:** conservative; surgical resection for local complications
