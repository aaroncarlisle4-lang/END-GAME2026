# Disease: TOLOSA-HUNT SYNDROME

**Source section:** ORBITAL AND OCULAR DISORDERS

## Definition

rare disorder characterized by painful ophthalmoplegia ← cavernous sinus inflammation

## Key Facts

- **Path:** lymphofibroblastic lesion (similar to orbital pseudotumor)
- **Age:** 5th decade
- **Location:** unilateral, bilateral (5%)

## Imaging Findings

### CT

- inflammatory soft-tissue density around orbital apex
- asymmetric enlargement of cavernous sinus
- abnormal nodular enhancement in prepontine cistern
- loss of SI of orbital fat
- enlargement of the cavernous sinus with convex outer margin
- abnormal soft tissue in cavernous sinus:
- isointense to gray matter on T1WI
- hypo- to isointense to gray matter on T2WI ← fibrous tissue
- homogeneous enhancement
- extension of lesion into orbital apex through superior orbital fissure with involvement of
- narrowing of cavernous segment of ICA (in 50%)
- enhancement of dura, trigeminal n., facial n. (uncommon)

### ANGIO

- narrowing of carotid siphon
- occlusion of superior ophthalmic vein
- nonvisualization of cavernous sinus

## Clinical

- **Rx:** corticosteroids, immunosuppressive agents (methotrexate, azathioprine)
