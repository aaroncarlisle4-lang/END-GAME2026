# Disease: BRONCHOGENIC CYST

**Source section:** CHEST DISORDERS

## Definition

CONGENITAL BRONCHOGENIC CYST fluid-filled blind pouch of ventral primitive foregut esophagus)

## Key Facts

- **Frequency:** most common intrathoracic foregut cyst (54-63% in surgical series)
- **Histo:** thin-walled cyst filled with mucinous material, lined by columnar respiratory
- **Location:** mediastinum (near carina) >> lung parenchyma > pleura > diaphragm
- **Associated with:** spinal abnormalities

## Imaging Findings

### General

- sharply outlined round / oval mass
- may contain air-fluid level ← infection / communication with airway or GI tract

### CT

- hypoattenuating lesion with smooth imperceptible wall
- cyst contents of water density (50%) / higher density (50%) ← proteinaceous-mucoid
- single unilocular fluid-filled pulmonary cyst in middle / posterior mediastinum
- echogenic distended lung obstructed by bronchogenic cyst
- hyperintense signal relative to lung + CSF on T2WI
- occasionally hyperintense signal relative to lung on T1WI (mucoid / hemorrhagic content)
- hyperintense obstructed lobe on T2WI
- rarely communicates with tracheal lumen
- may show esophageal compression
- 36% will eventually contain air
