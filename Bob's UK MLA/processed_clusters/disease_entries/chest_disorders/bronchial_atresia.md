# Disease: BRONCHIAL ATRESIA

**Source section:** CHEST DISORDERS

## Definition

rare anomaly resulting from focal obliteration of proximal lumen of a lobar / segmental /

## Key Facts

- **Path:** normal bronchial tree distal to occlusion is patent and filled with mucus; alveoli distal
- **Associated with:** lobar emphysema, cystic adenomatoid malformation
- **Mean age:** 17 years; M÷F = 2÷1
- **Location:** apicoposterior segment of LUL (>> RUL / ML)

## Imaging Findings

### CXR

- lobular / ovoid / round / branching perihilar mass:
- CHARACTERISTIC "gloved finger" sign ← accumulation of mucus in dilated central
- overexpanded hyperlucent lung segment (collateral air drift with expiratory air-trapping)

### CT

- mucus-filled bronchus near hilum WITHOUT connection between mucocele + hilum
- air trapping in surrounding area
- decreased perfusion with focal parenchymal oligemia
- large echogenic fetal lung mass ← lung fluid-filled distal to occlusion (indistinguishable
- dilated fluid-filled bronchus
- focal lung mass of homogeneously high SI on T2WI

## Clinical

- **Rx:** no treatment in asymptomatic patients; segmentectomy in symptomatic infected patients
