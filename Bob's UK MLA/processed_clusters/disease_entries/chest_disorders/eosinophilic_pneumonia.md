# Disease: EOSINOPHILIC PNEUMONIA

**Source section:** CHEST DISORDERS

## Key Facts

- **Path:** interstitial + alveolar edema containing a large number of eosinophils
- **Location:** mainly middle and upper lobes
- **Distribution:** uni- or bilateral, nonsegmental distribution, predominantly in lung periphery
- **Etiology:** idiopathic (no evidence of infection / exposure to potential antigens) with abrupt
- **Age:** 32 ± 17 years; M > F
- **Histo:** eosinophilic infiltrates + pulmonary edema ← release of eosinophilic granules

## Imaging Findings

### General

- nonspecific peripheral airspace consolidation with ground-glass-opacity
- reticulonodular densities
- hilar adenopathy
- pleural effusion
- bronchiectasis ± mucoid impaction in central upper lungs
- nonspecific focal mass / lobular consolidation with atelectasis
- subpleural consolidation in a lobular distribution
- centrilobular nodules
- pulmonary opacities

### CXR

- peripheral patchy migratory / fleeting (= transient and shifting) noncavitary pleural-based
- NO associated lymphadenopathy, NO pleural effusion

### HRCT

- peripheral ground-glass and/or airspace consolidation with upper and middle lobe
- solitary / multiple pulmonary nodular opacities with surrounding ground-glass halo (rare)
- diffuse bilateral reticular opacities mimicking pulmonary interstitial edema
- peripheral air space opacities (rare)
- bilateral patchy areas of ground-glass opacities
- smooth interlobular septal thickening (frequent)
- crazy-paving pattern (= ground-glass attenuation with intralobular reticular opacities)
- small pleural effusion
- poorly defined centrilobular nodules
- airspace consolidation
- thickening of bronchovascular bundle
- enlarged lymph nodes
- (CLASSIC) homogeneous peripheral airspace consolidation:
- frequently multifocal bilateral > unilateral nonsegmental with predilection for upper lung
- CHARACTERISTIC photographic negative of pulmonary edema (in 25-34%)
- unchanged for many days to weeks (DDx to Löffler syndrome)
- fast regression of infiltrates under steroids

### CT

- patchy consolidation / nodules with peripheral and upper lobe predominance
- ground-glass opacities with crazy paving
- subpleural reticulations (less common early, but frequent in later stages of disease)
- linear bandlike opacities parallel to pleural surface (> 2 months after onset of symptoms)
- pleural effusion (in < 10%)
- endocardial fibrosis
- valvular damage (= mitral + tricuspid insufficiency)
- formation of mural cardiac thrombus
- nodules ± surrounding ground-glass opacities
- focal / diffuse areas of ground-glass opacities in a patchy + peripheral distribution
- interlobular septal thickening

### Lungs (involved in 40%)

- congestive heart failure with pulmonary edema
- nonspecific focal / diffuse interstitial or alveolar nonlobar opacities
- occasionally peripheral pulmonary opacities
- pleural effusion (in 50%)

### CNS

- peripheral neuropathy + encephalopathy

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** typically spontaneous resolution within 1 month
- **Rx:** IV corticosteroids
