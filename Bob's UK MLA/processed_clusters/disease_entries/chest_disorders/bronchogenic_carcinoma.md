# Disease: BRONCHOGENIC CARCINOMA

**Source section:** CHEST DISORDERS

## Definition

LUNG CANCER = LUNG CARCINOMA intermediate malignant potential → slow growth, high incidence of early metastases most heterogeneous of primary lung cancers EPIDERMOID CARCINOMA OAT-CELL CARCINOMA = SMALL CELL LUNG CANCER without extrathoracic extension hematogenous metastatic disease at presentation

## Key Facts

- **Prevalence:** 225,000 new cases + 160,000 deaths (2012)
- **Frequency:** 7% of lung tumors; 1% of autopsies
- **Histo:** adenocarcinoma (72%), squamous cell ca. (18%)
- **Location:** upper lobes (75%)
- **Path:** difficult DDx from poorly differentiated adenoca. / SCC
- **Age:** 60-70 years; M÷F = 2.6÷1 to 1÷1

## Imaging Findings

### General

- apical pleural thickening / mass
- ± soft-tissue invasion / bone destruction
- coronal + sagittal MR images improve evaluation
- solitary peripheral mass with corona radiata / "pleural tail" sign / satellite lesion
- cavitation (16%): usually thick-walled with irregular inner surface; in 80% squamous cell
- central mass (38%): common in small cell carcinoma
- unilateral hilar enlargement ← primary tumor / enlarged lymph nodes
- calcified enlarged nodes frequently benign
- nodes in short axis diameter:
- PET (89% sensitive, 99% specific)
- anterior + middle mediastinal widening (suggests small cell carcinoma)
- segmental / lobar / lung atelectasis (37%) ← airway obstruction (particularly in squamous
- postobstructive lung enhances to a greater extent than tumor
- distal lung atelectasis has a higher SI on T2WI than the central mass (in 77%) ←
- "(reverse) S" sign of Golden on PA CXR = combination of:
- RUL collapse (inferiorly concave margin of lateral portion of minor fissure, which
- bulge of central tumor (inferiorly convex margin of medial portion of minor fissure)
- rat tail termination of bronchus
- "bronchial cuff "sign = focal / circumferential thickening of bronchial wall imaged end-on

- mucoid impaction of segmental / lobar bronchus ← endobronchial obstruction
- persistent peripheral infiltrate (30%) = postobstructive pneumonitis
- NO air bronchogram
- pleural effusion (8-15%): most commonly due to adenoca.
- chest wall invasion:
- tumor interdigitation with chest wall musculature on T2WI
- obliteration of high-intensity extrathoracic fat on T1WI
- bone erosion of ribs / spine (9%)
- involvement of main pulmonary artery (18%); lobar + segmental arteries (53%) may result
- calcification in 7% on CT (histologically in 14%) usually eccentric / finely stippled
- lung nodule > 1 cm (97% sensitive, 78% specific)
- lymph node with short-axis diameter > 1 cm by CT + NOT FDG avid = 100%

- bone scan:  80-94% sensitive, 67-75% specific

- increased FDG uptake at sites of residual radiographic abnormality > 8 weeks after

### ANGIO

- bronchogenic carcinoma supplied by bronchial arteries
- distortion / stenosis / occlusion of pulmonary arterial circulation

### CT

- typically large hilar / perihilar parenchymal mass involving at least one hilum (85%):
- extensive necrosis + hemorrhage
- central mass often produced by a combination of primary neoplasm + mediastinal
- centrally obstructing lesion → atelectasis of entire lung / major lobar atelectasis (30%)
- "bronchus cut-off" sign
- lobulated peripheral nodule without adenopathy (5%):
- fine spiculations ← vascular or lymphatic invasion / irregular intraalveolar spread
- marginal ground-glass opacity ← focal edema / hemorrhage / intraalveolar invasion
- enhancement reflecting tumor angiogenesis
- intratumoral calcification (in up to 23%)
- large confluent mediastinal (92%) / hilar 84%) adenopathy
- displacement / narrowing of tracheobronchial tree (68%) or major vessels (68%)
- atelectasis of entire lung / major lobar atelectasis (30%)

### PET

- avid FDG uptake (100%)

### Bone

- osteolytic metastases (¾)
- osteoblastic metastases (¼) in small cell carcinoma / adenocarcinoma
- occult metastases in 36% of bone scans

### Lung-to-lung metastases (in up to 10%, usually in late stage)

- pleural involvement
