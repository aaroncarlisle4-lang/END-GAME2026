# Disease: BLUNT CHEST TRAUMA

**Source section:** CHEST DISORDERS

## Definition

TRACHEOBRONCHIAL TEAR acute traumatic injury to alveoli disruption / tear of lung parenchyma resulting in lung cavity ← elastic recoil of lung direct compressive force severe sudden blow to lower thorax → shift of lower lobes across spine rib fracture + pneumothorax laceration at preexisting pleuroparenchymal adhesion pleura-covered part of lung extruding through a traumatic defect in the chest wall

## Key Facts

- **Incidence:** 100,000 hospital admissions / year (in USA)
- **Prevalence:** 3rd most common site (after injury to head and extremities)
- **Location:** (a)   mainstem bronchus within 2.5 cm of carina (80%); R > L
- **Path:** exudation of edema + hemorrhage into airspace + interstitium
- **Distribution:** nonsegmental
- **Age:** children + young adults are particularly prone
- **Associated with:** rib fracture

## Imaging Findings

### General

- fracture of first 3 ribs (53-91%), rare in children
- fracture of clavicle, sternum, scapula (40%)
- pneumothorax (70%) without improvement after chest tube placement + suction
- increasing pneumomediastinum ± subcutaneous emphysema of neck
- absence of pleural effusion
- "fallen lung" sign = collapsed lung droops to dependent position posterolaterally away
- inadequate reexpansion of lung despite adequate placement of one / more chest tubes ←
- elevation of hyoid bone above level of C3 vertebra / elevation of greater cornu to < 2 cm
- atelectasis (may be late development)

### CT

- discontinuity / irregularity of bronchial wall

- overdistension of tube cuff
- protrusion of tube wall beyond expected margins of trachea
- extraluminal position of tip of tube
- irregular patchy / diffuse extensive homogeneous consolidation
- opacity may enlarge for 48-72 hours
- rapid resolution beginning after 24-48 hours
- complete clearing in 3-10 days
- overlying rib fractures (frequent)

- single / multiple uni- / multilocular pulmonary lesions consisting of round / oval lung cavity
- thin-walled air-filled cavity (50%) ± air-fluid level preceded by homogeneous well-

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** 30% mortality (in 15% within 1 hour)
- **Cx:** pneumothorax
