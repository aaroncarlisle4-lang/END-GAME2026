# Disease: CONGENITAL LOBAR OVERINFLATION

**Source section:** CHEST DISORDERS

## Definition

CONGENITAL LOBAR EMPHYSEMA (technically inaccurate term) progressive overdistension of one / multiple lobes

## Key Facts

- **Histo:** airspace enlargement without maldevelopment
- **Associated with:** CHD in 12-15% (PDA, VSD)
- **Location:** LUL (42-43%) > RML (32-35%) > RUL (20%) > either lower lobe (< 1%)

## Imaging Findings

### General

- hazy masslike dense opacity immediately following birth ← delayed clearance of lung fluid in
- hyperlucent overinflated lobe (after clearing of fluid via lymphatic + capillary system)
- air trapping:
- compression atelectasis of adjacent ipsilateral lobes
- contralateral mediastinal shift
- widening of rib spaces
- depression of ipsilateral hemidiaphragm
- widely separated but organized vascular markings
- homogeneously hyperechoic mass
- mass of homogeneously high SI on T2WI

## Clinical

- **Rx:** ventilatory support (low-volume, low-pressure); surgical resection
