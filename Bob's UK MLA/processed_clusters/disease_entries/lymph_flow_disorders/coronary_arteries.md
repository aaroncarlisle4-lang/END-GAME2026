# Disease: CORONARY ARTERIES

**Source section:** LYMPH FLOW DISORDERS

## Definition

LAD LAD LCx RCA watershed areas artery that supplies the inferior portion of LV 2nd most common cause of sudden cardiac death among young adults

## Key Facts

- **Location:** on epicardial surface, in atrial / ventricular septum, in myocardium
- **Prevalence:** 0.3-1.3%; in 4-25% responsible for nontraumatic sudden death in young adults

## Imaging Findings

### General

- forms circle of Vieussens = anastomosis with LAD
- courses along anterior interatrial groove toward superior cavoatrial junction
- at cavoatrial junction circling either anteriorly (precaval) / posteriorly (retrocaval) to
- penetrates base of posterior interatrial septum at crux
- may course beneath endocardium near ostium of coronary sinus + septal isthmus
- ribs slanting to left side of image
- catheter in descending aorta on right side of image
- ribs slanting to right side of image
- catheter in descending aorta on left side of image
- tortuous course outside the normal path
- fibrous connection between LAD-LCx junction + left coronary cusp
- anomalous artery surrounded by epicardial fat
- slitlike coronary artery lumen
- impaired coronary artery blood flow
- prominent straight connection near crux
