# Disease: CARDIAC CONDUCTION DEVICES (CCD

**Source section:** LYMPH FLOW DISORDERS

## Definition

electronic device providing small electrical stimuli for cardiac contraction as substitute for AUTOMATIC IMPLANTABLE CARDIOVERTER-DEFIBRILLATOR (AICD) electronic device generating a large amount of electrical energy to defibrillate the heart

## Key Facts

- **Location:** electrode tip in right atrial appendage

## Imaging Findings

### General

- single lead with 2 shock coils
- electrode tip pointing upward + anteriorly
- electrode tip may be shaped into a preformed "J loop"
- slight redundancy of lead desirable to avoid tension during deep inspiration / arm
- electrode tip to left of spine (frontal view)
- electrode tip pointing anteriorly (lateral view)
- (rarely) electrode tip intended for RVOT
- electrode tip posteriorly (lateral view)
- inferiorly + laterally (frontal view)

## Clinical

- **Rx:** cardiac resynchronization therapy (CRT) in combination with implantable
