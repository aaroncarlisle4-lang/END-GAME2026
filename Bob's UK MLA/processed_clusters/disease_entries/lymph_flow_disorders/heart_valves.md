# Disease: HEART VALVES

**Source section:** LYMPH FLOW DISORDERS

## Definition

separates LV outflow tract from ascending aorta by a valve composed of annulus, bicuspid valve (= 2 leaflets) anchored on mitral valve annulus + connected to LV papillary D-shaped ring within L atrioventricular groove; imbedded within myocardium as part of separates RV outflow tract from main pulmonary artery by a semilunar valve composed of 3 right atrioventricular valve separating RA from RV; anchored on tricuspid valve annulus +

## Imaging Findings

### General

- aortic valve resides in profile superior to this line overlying the thoracic spine
- pulmonic valve just inferior to left mainstem bronchus
- mitral valve resides inferior to this line centrally located within cardiac silhouette
- tricuspid valve inferior to this line more basilar and midline
- aortic valve resides superior to this line
- pulmonic valve anterior + superior to aortic valve
- mitral valve resides inferoposteriorly to this line
- tricuspid valve inferior to this line anteriorly

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** vulnerable to ischemia + rupture
