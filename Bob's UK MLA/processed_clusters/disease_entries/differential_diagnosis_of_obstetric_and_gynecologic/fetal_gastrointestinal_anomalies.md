# Disease: FETAL GASTROINTESTINAL ANOMALIES

**Source section:** DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS OF OBSTETRIC AND GYNECOLOGIC

## Definition

fluid-filled stomach + proximal duodenum

## Key Facts

- **Prevalence:** 6÷2,000 pregnancies; 6÷10,000 births
- **Etiology:** intestinal atresia / stenosis secondary to vascular accident, volvulus, meconium
- **Associated with:** GI anomalies in 45% (malrotation, duplication, microcolon, esophageal
- **Incidence:** abdominal cyst in 1÷500 - 1÷1,000 live births; 1÷2,625 pregnancies

## Imaging Findings

### General

- stomach on contralateral side (situs inversus)
- congenital diaphragmatic hernia
- multiple distended bowel loops > 7 mm in diameter
- increased peristalsis
- polyhydramnios (if obstruction above level of mid jejunum; exceptions are esophageal

## Clinical

- **Rx:** repeat ultrasound scan
- **Cx:** Meconium peritonitis (50%)
- **Management:** parental testing for cystic fibrosis, careful fetal anatomic survey, follow-up
- **Prognosis:** resolution before / after delivery
