# Disease: FETAL NECK ANOMALIES

**Source section:** DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS OF OBSTETRIC AND GYNECOLOGIC

## Definition

NUCHAL SONOLUCENCY / FULLNESS / EDEMA skin thickening of posterior neck measured between calvarium + dorsal skin margin

## Key Facts

- **Prevalence:** among the most common anomaly in 1st trimester + early 2nd trimester

## Imaging Findings

### General

- larger lymphangiomas with radiating septations are usually found with trisomy 18
- nuchal fullness ≥ 3 mm during 1st trimester is seen in trisomy 21 / 18 / 13 (30-50% PPV)
- often reverting to normal by 16-18 weeks
- septations within nuchal translucency carries a 20- to 200-fold risk for chromosomal
