# Disease: LIMB REDUCTION ANOMALIES

**Source section:** DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS OF OBSTETRIC AND GYNECOLOGIC

## Definition

flexion contracture of PIP joint of any finger index finger overlaps clenched fist formed by other digits fixed abnormal deviation of 5th finger in radioulnar plane (= radial angulation of DIP permanently deviated axis of wrist + usually closed hand SPLIT / CLEFT HAND = LOBSTER CLAW HAND longitudinal deficiency of central digits overgrowth of all structures of affected (commonly radial) fingers missing / foreshortened arm / forearm with presence of hand presence of supernumerary digits association of polydactyly with syndactyly abnormal connection between adjacent digits same length of last 4 fingers sedative-hypnotic nonbarbiturate (sold 1954-1961)

## Key Facts

- **Prevalence:** 0.49 ÷10,000 births
- **Location:** often asymmetric
- **Incidence:** 1÷683 pregnancies;
- **Frequency:** 10%

## Imaging Findings

### General

- proximal interphalangeal articulation of index finger flexed + ulnarly deviated
- adducted thumb
- deep V- or U-shaped central defect
- narrowing, disorganization, destruction of joints
- hemimelia = absence / gross shortening of distal portion of one / more limbs (esp. radius
- phocomelia = attachment of hands + feet to abbreviated arms and legs
- amelia = congenital absence of one / more arms / legs
- fusion / absence of paired bones
- diminished bone mineral density
- poorly developed diaphyses of long bones
- anomalies of phalanges:
- hypoplastic terminal phalanges associated with dysplastic nails
- hyperphalangism
- congenital heart disease, cleft lip + palate, urinary tract anomalies, syndromes of
- growth retardation
- osteoporosis
- talipes equinovarus, foreshortened extremities, absent digits, syndactyly, anomalous ribs
- CNS anomalies, cardiac abnormalities
- CNS, craniofacial, cardiovascular, thymic malformations
- hydrocephalus, microcephalus
- stippled ossification centers in vertebrae, proximal femora, tarsal + carpal bones

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** may progress during infancy / childhood
