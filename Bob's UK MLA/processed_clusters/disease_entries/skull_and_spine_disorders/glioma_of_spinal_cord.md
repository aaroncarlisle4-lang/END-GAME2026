# Disease: GLIOMA OF SPINAL CORD

**Source section:** SKULL AND SPINE DISORDERS

## Key Facts

- **Frequency:** 30% of spinal cord tumors; 2nd in prevalence to ependymoma in adults
- **Mean age:** 29 years; M÷F = 58÷42
- **Path:** ill-defined fusiform cord enlargement without cleavage plane / capsule
- **Histo:** hypercellularity with infiltrative growth along scaffold of normal astrocytes,
- **Location:** thoracic cord (67%), cervical cord (49%), conus medullaris (3%); on average
- **Prevalence:** 13% of all spinal ependymomas; most common neoplasm of conus

## Imaging Findings

### General

- eccentric irregular tumor cysts + polar cysts + syrinx (common):
- water-soluble myelographic contrast enters cystic space on delayed CT images
- scoliosis (24%)
- widened interpedicular distance
- bone erosion
