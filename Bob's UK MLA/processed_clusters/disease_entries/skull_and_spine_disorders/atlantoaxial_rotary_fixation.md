# Disease: ATLANTOAXIAL ROTARY FIXATION

**Source section:** SKULL AND SPINE DISORDERS

## Definition

AARF = ATLANTOAXIAL ROTATORY SUBLUXATION (AARS) ATLANTOAXIAL ROTATORY FIXATION (AARF) SPONTANEOUS HYPEREMIC DISLOCATION ATLANTOAXIAL ROTATORY / ROTARY DISLOCATION DISTENTION LUXATION = NASOPHARYNGEAL TORTICOLLIS nontraumatic subluxation / rotational disorder of atlantoaxial joint leading to limited rotation /

## Key Facts

- **Age:** more prevalent in children than in adults ← mostly flat articular facets allow ample

## Imaging Findings

### General

- atlanto-odontoid asymmetry (open mouth odontoid view):
- decrease in atlanto-odontoid space + widening of lateral mass on side ipsilateral to
- increase in atlanto-odontoid space + narrowing of lateral mass on side contralateral to
- atlantoaxial asymmetry remains constant with head turned into neutral position
- posterior arch of C1 not identified in true lateral projection
- obscuration of craniovertebral junction in true lateral view
- facet displacement in neutral head position
- asymmetrically fixed C1-C2 rotation

### MR

- disruption of alar + transverse ligg.
- spinal cord compression (rare)
- pivot around dens, NO anterior displacement of atlas
- center of rotation shifted to one of lateral masses
