# Disease: NEURENTERIC CYST

**Source section:** SKULL AND SPINE DISORDERS

## Definition

incomplete separation of foregut and notochord with persistence of canal of Kovalevski RETRORECTAL CYSTIC HAMARTOMA = RECTAL DUPLICATION CYST = MUCIN- rare congenital abnormality in presacral space ← persistent remnant of embryonic gut

## Key Facts

- **Frequency:** rarest of bronchopulmonary foregut malformations (like pulmonary sequestration,
- **Associated with:** neurofibromatosis; meningocele; spinal malformation (stalk connects cyst and
- **Location:** anterior to spinal canal on mesenteric side of gut
- **Age:** manifested in childhood / adulthood
- **Histo:** NO smooth muscle layer

## Imaging Findings

### General

- posterior mediastinal mass
- air-fluid level (if communicating with GI tract through diaphragmatic defect)
- spinal dysraphism at the same level:
- midline cleft in centra (accommodates stalk)
- anterior / posterior spina bifida
- vertebral body anomalies: absent vertebra, butterfly vertebra, hemivertebra, scoliosis
- diastematomyelia
- thoracic myelomeningocele
- uni- / multilocular cyst
- high signal intensity on T1WI ← mucoid content
