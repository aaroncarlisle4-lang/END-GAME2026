# Disease: IDIOPATHIC SPINAL CORD HERNIATION

**Source section:** SKULL AND SPINE DISORDERS

spinal cord displacement through anterior / lateral dura mater defect → uncommon cause of

## Key Facts

- **Mean age:** 51 (range, 21-78) years; M÷F = 2÷3

## Imaging Findings

### General

- variable degree of cord deformity / kinking:
- acute anterior kink of thoracic spinal cord with enlargement of dorsal subarachnoid space
- free flow of contrast material (cannot completely exclude space-occupying lesion as in
- "nuclear trail" sign = linear high-attenuation lesion in inferior endplate of adjacent vertebra
- soft tissue extending from apex of cord displacement through dural defect into epidural
- ± concomitant scalloping of vertebral body

### MR

- obliteration of CSF space ventral to cord
- widened dorsal CSF space
- absence of solid / cystic mass posterior to cord
- small amount of extradural soft tissue extending from ventral apex of displaced cord into
- NO enhancement
- ± cord atrophy and high T2 signal intensity

## Clinical

- **N.B.:** arachnoid cyst must be excluded!
- **Rx:** postoperatively improved neurologic symptoms (88%)
