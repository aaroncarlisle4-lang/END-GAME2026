# Disease: MULTILOCULAR CYSTIC RENAL TUMOR

**Source section:** GANGLIONEUROMA

## Definition

MCRT = MULTILOCULAR CYSTIC NEPHROMA (MLCN) = POLYCYSTIC NEPHROBLASTOMA = WELL- rare nonhereditary benign renal neoplasm originating from metanephric blastema possibly

## Key Facts

- **Age:** bimodal age + sex distribution: < 4 years in 73% male, > 4 years in 89% female
- **Path:** solitary large well-circumscribed multiseptated mass of noncommunicating loculi,
- **Histo:** (gross anatomic + radiologic features are identical)

## Imaging Findings

### General

- tumor tendency to herniate into collecting system
- contrast enhancement of capsule + septations ← tortuous fine vessels course through septa
- curvilinear to flocculent calcification of septa / capsule in 5%
- absence of intratumoral / perinephric hemorrhage
- distortion of calices / hydronephrosis ← nonfunctional mass
- tendency for herniation of tumor cysts into renal pelvis and proximal ureter is

### US

- cystic mass with claw / beak shape of adjacent normal renal parenchyma ← intrarenal
- cluster of cysts separated by thick septa (SUGGESTIVE):
- NO solid / nodular elements
- occasionally solid echogenic character ← closely packed acoustic interfaces with
- ± associated urinary tract obstruction

### CT

- well-circumscribed (CHARACTERISTIC) multiseptated cystic renal mass:
- tumor encapsulated by thick fibrous capsule
- attenuation of cysts equal to / higher than water ← gelatinous fluid
- multicystic = cluster of noncommunicating honeycombed cysts between a few mm and 4
- cysts separated by variably enhancing thick septa
- closely packed septa may appear as solid nodules but are separated by very small cystic

### MR

- multicystic mass of low signal intensity on T1WI + hyperintense on T2WI
- hypointense capsule and septa on all sequences
- variable SI of cyst content on T1WI ← old hemorrhage / concentrated protein
- enhancing septa

## Clinical

- **Cx:** local recurrence / coexistent Wilms tumor (very rare)
- **Rx:** nephrectomy with excellent prognosis
