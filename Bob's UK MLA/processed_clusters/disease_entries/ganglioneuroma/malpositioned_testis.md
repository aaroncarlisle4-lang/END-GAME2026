# Disease: MALPOSITIONED TESTIS

**Source section:** GANGLIONEUROMA

## Definition

MALDESCENDED TESTIS arrested migration of testis along its normal course through internal inguinal ring, inguinal deviation from the usual pathway RETRACTILE TESTIS unusually spastic cremasteric muscle retractile testis + cryptorchidism

## Key Facts

- **Prevalence:** early 3rd trimester in 10%; at birth in 3.7-6.0% (in babies > 2,500 g in 3.4%; in
- **Associated with:** prune belly syndrome (bilateral cryptorchidism), Prader-Willi syndrome,
- **Location:** bilateral in 10%

## Imaging Findings

### CT

- no spermatic cord in inguinal canal
- failure to visualize testis within scrotum
- empty ipsilateral scrotal sac + intra-abdominal mass

### US

- often small atrophic testis with generalized decreased / similar echogenicity compared to
- identification of mediastinum testis is necessary
- ± testicular microlithiasis
- signal characteristics similar to contralateral / normal testis
- usually homogeneously hypointense on T1WI
- homogeneously hyperintense on T2WI
- markedly hyperintense on DWI

## Clinical

- **Rx:** surgery / orchiopexy at 9-12 months of age
