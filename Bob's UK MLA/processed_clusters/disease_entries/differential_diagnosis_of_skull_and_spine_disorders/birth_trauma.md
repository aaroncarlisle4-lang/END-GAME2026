# Disease: BIRTH TRAUMA

**Source section:** DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS OF SKULL AND SPINE DISORDERS

## Key Facts

- **Location:** commonly at vertex
- **Frequency:** 1% of all deliveries

## Imaging Findings

### General

- crosses suture lines
- crescent-shaped lesion adjacent to outer table of skull
- will not cross cranial suture line
- usually resolved by a few weeks to 3-4 months
- may calcify / ossify causing thickening of diploe ← prolonged resorption (= chronic

### MR

- T1- and T2-hyperintense lesion ← subacute hemorrhage
- CT shows associated intracranial hemorrhage
- extracerebral fluid collection accompanied by ventricular dilatation (= communicating

## Clinical

- **Cx:** infection
