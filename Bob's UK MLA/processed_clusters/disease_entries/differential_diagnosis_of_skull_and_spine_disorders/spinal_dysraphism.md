# Disease: SPINAL DYSRAPHISM

**Source section:** DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS OF SKULL AND SPINE DISORDERS

## Definition

abnormal / incomplete fusion of midline embryologic mesenchymal, neurologic, bony incomplete closure of bony elements of the spine (lamina + spinous processes) posteriorly OCCULT SPINAL DYSRAPHISM cleft / tethered cord WITH skin cover SPINA BIFIDA CYSTICA posterior protrusion of all / parts of the contents of the spinal canal through a bony malformation of distal spine and cord

## Key Facts

- **Frequency:** 15% of spinal dysraphism
- **Associated with:** hydrocephalus, Arnold-Chiari II malformation
- **Location:** usually in lower thoracic + lumbar spine

## Imaging Findings

### General

- complete absence of vertebral body
- hypoplastic posterior elements may be present
- right / left hemivertebra
- scoliosis at birth
- rapidly progressive kyphoscoliosis
- vertical radiolucent band just behind midportion of vertebral body; disappears mostly
- widened vertebral body with butterfly configuration (AP view)
- adaptation of vertebral endplates of adjacent vertebral bodies
- height of fused vertebral bodies equals the sum of heights of involved bodies +
- "waist" at level of intervertebral disk space
