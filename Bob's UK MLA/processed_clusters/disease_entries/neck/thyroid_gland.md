# Disease: THYROID GLAND

**Source section:** NECK

## Definition

small temporary channel along which thyroid primordium descends in midline of neck to its

## Key Facts

- **Location:** (a) base of the tongue (90%);

## Imaging Findings

### General

- intense homogeneous enhancement

### CT

- homogeneous mildly hyperattenuating relative to surrounding neck musculature with an
- mildly increased attenuation of 70 HU ± 10 relative to adjacent muscle ← intrinsic
- well-circumscribed homogeneous avidly enhancing mass
- islands of low attenuation within ectopic thyroid tissue = clue to diagnosis of ectopic

### MR

- slightly hyperintense on T1WI + iso- to slightly hyperintense on T2WI relative to the neck
- symmetric uniform uptake in both lobes
- absence of orthotopic thyroid tissue (in 70-80%)
- thyroid tissue identified in usual thyroid bed / at ectopic sites
- isointense to mildly hyperintense relative to musculature on T1WI
- mildly hyperintense with variable enhancement on T2WI

## Clinical

- **N.B.:** intravenous contrast medium impairs thyroid uptake of radioactive iodine for 4-8
