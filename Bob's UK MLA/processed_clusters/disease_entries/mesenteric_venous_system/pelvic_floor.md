# Disease: PELVIC FLOOR

**Source section:** MESENTERIC VENOUS SYSTEM

## Definition

musculofascial diaphragm inserted on pelvic bones network of connective tissue that lies deep to peritoneum and supports pelvic organs connective tissue condensations of obturator + levator ani fascia that runs obliquely diamond-shaped structure consisting of two triangles with transverse perineus muscle

## Imaging Findings

### MR

- too thin to be recognized except for its condensations: cardinal lig., uterosacral
- integrity inferred by efficiency of provided support
- nonvisualized U-shaped sling inserting on inner pubis + passing around anorectal
- encloses urogenital hiatus (= space for urethra + vagina + anal canal)
- encircles urogenital hiatus
- responsible for angle between rectum + anal canal
- thin sagittally oriented fan-shaped structure
- curved shape with inferior concavity (COR plane)
- inserts laterally to pelvic sidewalls on ATLA
- inserts medially to pelvic organs
- forms posteriorly raphe with attachment to external anal sphincter →
