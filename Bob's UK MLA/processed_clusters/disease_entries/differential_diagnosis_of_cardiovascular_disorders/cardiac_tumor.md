# Disease: CARDIAC TUMOR

**Source section:** DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS OF CARDIOVASCULAR DISORDERS

## Definition

neoplasm arising in infradiaphragmatic sites focal area of attenuation < 20 HU / fat-suppression on MR

## Key Facts

- **Prevalence:** 0.002-0.250%; 0.001-0.030% (autopsy series)
- **Age:** typically > 60 years with disseminated tumor
- **Location:** anterolateral free wall + RVOT

## Imaging Findings

### CXR

- cardiomegaly, pericardial effusion
- signs of CHF
- abnormal cardiac contour
- pleural effusion
- localized bulge of left cardiac contour
- curvilinear / spotty calcifications (resembling myocardial aneurysm)
- nonspecific imaging features

### MR

- multicystic mass
- hypointense mass on all sequences
- increase of total myocardial thickness: RV > LV
- thin linear / curvilinear configuration

### CT

- well-defined peripherally calcified mass
- central region of variable attenuation

## Clinical

- **Cx:** may rupture into cardiac chamber / pericardium
- **Prognosis:** death (⅓) ← cardiac tamponade / CHF / coronary artery invasion, sinoatrial
