# Disease: COMPLICATIONS OF BRAIN SURGERY

**Source section:** TRIGEMINAL NEUROPATHY

## Definition

EXTRACRANIAL HERNIATION herniation of brain tissue through skull defect after trauma / therapeutic craniectomy SYNDROME OF THE TREPHINED commonly following neurosurgical decompression of subdural hematoma / posterior

## Key Facts

- **Location:** fluid collection ipsilateral to craniectomy / contralateral / interhemispheric

## Imaging Findings

### General

- bulging skin flap + subgaleal fluid collection
- depressed skin flap at craniectomy site
- concave deformity of adjacent brain
- "zebra" sign = streaky curvilinear areas of increased attenuation in cerebellar sulci +
- sunken skin flap
- subfalcine / transtentorial herniation of brain away from craniectomy defect:
- midline shift
- compression of midbrain
- effacement of basal cisterns
- "peaking" sign = subdural air collections compress both frontal lobes
- "Mount Fuji" sign = compression and separation of frontal lobes by air → widening of
- transtentorial ± tonsillar herniation

## Clinical

- **Cx:** compression of cortical veins → venous infarction; contusion of brain at
- **Rx:** Trendelenburg position, clamping of ventricular shunt / drain, intravenous fluid,
