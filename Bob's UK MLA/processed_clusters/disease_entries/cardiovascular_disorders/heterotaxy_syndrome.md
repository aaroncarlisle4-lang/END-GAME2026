# Disease: HETEROTAXY SYNDROME

**Source section:** CARDIOVASCULAR DISORDERS

## Definition

CARDIOSPLENIC SYNDROMES situs ambiguus with a spectrum of various congenital truncal abnormalities + frequently BILATERAL RIGHT-SIDEDNESS = RIGHT ISOMERISM IVEMARK SYNDROME juxtaposed "piggybacked" IVC (aorta usually posterior) (MOST RELIABLE INDICATOR) BILATERAL LEFT-SIDEDNESS = LEFT ISOMERISM

## Key Facts

- **Prevalence:** 1÷1,750-1÷40,000 livebirths; M > F
- **Age:** presentation in infancy / adulthood; M < F

## Imaging Findings

### General

- right bronchial isomerism:
- bilateral trilobed lungs = bilateral minor fissures (SPECIFIC)
- bilateral eparterial bronchi (MR / tomogram):
- main bronchus passes superior to ipsilateral main pulmonary artery
- pulmonary arteries inferior to bronchi (on PA view) + projecting anterior to
- diminished pulmonary vascularity / pulmonary venous hypertension (TAPVR below

### Heart & great vessels

- bilateral systemic / right morphologic atria with broad-based appendages
- ipsilaterality of abdominal aorta + IVC
- bilateral SVC
- bilateral pulmonary / left morphologic atrium with pointed tubular narrow-based
- cardiac apex on right / in midline
- bilateral SVC (50%)
- interruption of hepatic segment of IVC with azygos / hemiazygos continuation in 65-

### Abdomen

- asplenia = absent spleen (risk of sepsis)
- midline liver = centrally located "bridging" liver = hepatic symmetry
- variably located stomach (on right / left side / in central position) and small
- cardiac apex discordant from stomach + liver
- absence of IVC (on LAT CXR)
- large azygos vein (on AP CXR) may mimic aortic arch

### Lung

- left bronchial isomerism:
- bilateral morphologic left lungs with 2 lobes (55-68%), normal (18%), bilateral R-
- bilateral hyparterial bronchi = main bronchus passes inferior to ipsilateral main
- arteries projecting superior to bronchi (on PA view) + posterior to
- normal / increased pulmonary vascularity
- absence of middle lobe fissure

### Abdominal heterotaxy

- polysplenia = presence of ≥ 2 spleens (usually two major + indefinite number of
- midline centrally located liver = hepatic symmetry
- absence of gallbladder (50%)
- variably located stomach → always on same side of spleen(s)
- malrotation of bowel (80%)
- preduodenal portal vein
- absence of intrahepatic IVC
- aorta anterior to spine in midline
- "double vessel" sign = 2 vessels of similar size in paraspinous location posterior to

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** up to 90% mortality by end of 1st year of life
