# Disease: TRUNCUS ARTERIOSUS

**Source section:** CARDIOVASCULAR DISORDERS

## Definition

PERSISTENT TRUNCUS ARTERIOSUS = SINGLE OUTLET OF THE HEART = COMMON ARTERIAL TRUNK septation failure of the conotruncus characterized by infundibular VSD present pseudotruncus VSD absent rare acyanotic anomaly characterized by TRUNCUS TYPE IV severe form of tetralogy of Fallot with atresia of the pulmonary trunk; entire pulmonary

## Key Facts

- **Prevalence:** 1% of CHD detected in fetus; 94÷1,000,000 live births
- **Associated with:** PDA (80%), VSD, tetralogy (usually isolated to left PA)

## Imaging Findings

### CXR

- cardiomegaly:
- increased volume of both ventricles
- enlarged LA (50%) ← increased pulmonary blood flow
- wide mediastinum ← large "aortic shadow" = truncus arteriosus
- "waterfall / hilar comma" sign = elevated right hilum (30%); elevated left hilum (10%)
- concave pulmonary segment (50%) = type I has left convex pulmonary segment
- markedly ⇑ pulmonary blood flow, may be asymmetric
- single arterial vessel overriding the interventricular septum (DDx: tetralogy of Fallot)
- frequently dysplastic single semilunar valve with 3-6 leaflets (most commonly
- truncal valve may be stenotic
- truncal valve insufficiency with age (in 25%)
- concavity in area of pulmonary segment
- commalike abnormal appearance of pulmonary artery
- absent normal right + left pulmonary artery (lateral chest film)
- esophageal indentation posteriorly ← large systemic collaterals
- prominent hilar + intrapulmonary vessels (= systemic collaterals)
- "coeur en sabot" = RV enlargement
- prominent ascending aorta with hyperpulsations

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** 40% (20%) 6-month (1-year) survival rate
- **Rx:** Rastelli procedure (30% no longer operable at 4 years of age) = (a) artificial valve placed
