# Disease: PAPILLARY FIBROELASTOMA

**Source section:** CARDIOVASCULAR DISORDERS

## Definition

FIBROELASTIC PAPILLOMA = PAPILLOMA / MYXOMA / FIBROMA OF VALVES = GIANT LAMBL MYXOFIBROMA = HYALINE FIBROMA rare benign excrescence predominantly affecting surface of cardiac valves

## Key Facts

- **Prevalence:** 25% of all cardiac valvular tumors (most common valvular tumor); 10% of all
- **Peak age:** 7th and 8th decade; M÷F = 1÷1
- **Path:** gelatinous mass with "sea anemone" appearance ← multiple delicate branching
- **Histo:** avascular papilloma = avascular connective tissue composed of fibrous core + lined by
- **Size:** usually < 1.0-1.5 cm in diameter (may be as large as 5 cm)

## Imaging Findings

### General

- homogeneous mobile pedunculated mass:
- elongated strandlike projection / well-defined head
- CHARACTERISTIC stippled edge with a "shimmer / vibration" at interface between tumor
- flutters / prolapses with cardiac motion
- turbulent blood flow

### CT

- slightly hypoattenuating mass

### MR

- intermediate SI / hypointense compared to myocardium
- mobile mass (on cine gradient-echo images)

## Clinical

- **Rx:** surgical excision ± leaflet repair / valve replacement
