# Disease: AORTIC PROSTHETIC GRAFT INFECTION

**Source section:** CARDIOVASCULAR DISORDERS

## Key Facts

- **Frequency:** 1.3-6% of prosthetic graft procedures

## Imaging Findings

### General

- ring of fat attenuation in early postoperative period < 5 mm between aneurysm wall and
- perigraft fluid
- perigraft soft-tissue attenuation with indistinctness of graft margins
- ectopic gas ← fistulous communication with bowel / gas-producing organism
- pseudoaneurysm (25%)
- focal bowel wall thickening → indicates fistula
- > 5 mm soft tissue between graft + surrounding wrap (beyond 7th postoperative week)
- focal discontinuity of calcified aneurysmal wrap

### NUC

- uptake of 99mTc-hexametazine labeled leukocytes (drawbacks: not performed quickly,

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** 17-75% mortality; 30-50% morbidity
