# Disease: CARDIAC PARAGANGLIOMA

**Source section:** CARDIOVASCULAR DISORDERS

## Definition

extremely rare, usually benign sporadic neoplasm arising from intrinsic cardiac sympathetic

## Key Facts

- **Prevalence:** < 50 cases
- **Mean age:** 40 (range, 18-85) years
- **Path:** 2-14 cm large encapsulated / poorly circumscribed and infiltrative highly vascular
- **Histo:** monomorphic tumor composed of nests of paraganglial cells (= "Zellballen")
- **Location:** posterior wall of left atrium > roof of left atrium > atrial cavity > interatrial septum >

## Imaging Findings

### CXR

- middle mediastinal mass splaying carina simulating left atrial enlargement (for typically
- large echogenic left atrial mass
- compression of SVC, encasement of coronary arteries
- for total body imaging with a sensitivity of 90%

### NECT

- circumscribed / ill-defined heterogeneous mass:
- hypoattenuating
- isoattenuating to cardiac structures (may be missed)
- ± tumor calcifications
- ± extracardiac extension

### CECT

- markedly enhancing mass adherent to / involving left atrium / anterior to aortic root
- central area of low attenuation (in 50%) from necrosis

### MR

- mass iso- / hypointense to myocardium on T1WI
- very hyperintense mass on T2WI
- intense often heterogeneous enhancement
