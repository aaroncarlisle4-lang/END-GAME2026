## Definition

temporary obstruction of venous outflow by pneumatic cuff around mid-thigh inflated above

## Imaging Findings

### General

- intraluminal filling defect constant on all images
- nonfilling of calf veins
- inadequate filling of common femoral vein and external + common iliac veins
- NO complete luminal collapse with venous compression (DDx: deformity + scarring
- visualization of clot within vein (DDx: slow flowing blood; machine noise):
- homogeneous poorly echogenic / anechoic substance within venous lumen
- smooth border of thrombus
- < 75% increase in diameter of common femoral vein during Valsalva
- venous diameter at least twice that of adjacent artery suggests thrombus < 10 days old
- echogenic material within venous lumen
- irregular surface of thrombus
- incomplete compressibility of vein
- absence of spontaneity (= any waveform recording): NOT RELIABLE in peripheral veins
- continuous venous signal = absence of phasicity (= no cyclic variation in flow velocity
- attenuation / absence of augmentation (= no increase in flow velocity with distal
- pulsatile venous flow is a sign of congestive heart failure / pericardial effusion / cardiac
- high SI / absence of low SI flow within vein on true FISP sequence
- low-signal-intensity filling defect in a high-signal-intensity patent vein on true FISP
- absence of normal low-signal-intensity flow void in a patent vessel on T2WI
- variable signal intensity on T1WI depending on age of luminal blood products
- ↓ initial rise in venous capacitance (= venous volume)
- delay in venous outflow (= "fall" of curve) measured at 3 sec

## Clinical

- **N.B.:** limited by flow signal artifacts
