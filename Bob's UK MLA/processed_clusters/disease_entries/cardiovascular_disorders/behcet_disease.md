# Disease: BEHÇET DISEASE

**Source section:** CARDIOVASCULAR DISORDERS

## Definition

rare multisystemic chronic relapsing inflammatory vasculitis of variable-sized vessels in develops > 10 years after onset of Behçet disease develops within 10 years after onset of Behçet disease

## Key Facts

- **Histo:** nonspecific necrotizing vasculitis with deposition of immune complexes in walls of
- **Associated with:** human leukocyte antigen HLA-B51; genetic mutations including factor V
- **Path:** lymphocytic vasculitis of venules of bowel wall → chronic inflammation + intestinal
- **Location:** terminal ileum (most common), often together with proximal cecum

## Imaging Findings

### Joints

- mild nondestructive arthritis
- single / multiple erosions / ulcerations
- diffuse esophagitis, esophageal stenosis
- mucosal dissection, intramural hematoma
- downhill esophageal varices ← thrombotic occlusion of SVC → fibrosing
- geographic large collar-button-shaped deep ulcer
- aneurysmal dilatation of terminal ileum
- cecal mass

### Large intestine

- multiple discrete punched-out ulcers throughout colon mimicking Crohn disease

### Parenchymal Neurobehçet

- isolated / confluent irregular T2 hyperintense foci of circular / linear / crescent shape
- atypically space-occupying lesion masquerading as unilateral brain tumor
- leptomeningeal enhancement ← cranial nerve palsy /meningoencephalitis

### Nonparenchymal Neurobehçet

- cerebrovascular disorders:
- dural sinus thrombosis (5-25%) → raised intracranial pressure
- multiple arterial aneurysm
- arterial dissection / occlusion (rare)
- aseptic meningitis (0.05-8.00%)
- endocarditis, myocarditis, pericarditis
- endomyocardial fibrosis
- coronary artery disease, myocardial infarction
- intracardiac thrombosis
- aneurysm of sinus of Valsalva, periaortic pseudoaneurysm
- valve dysfunction, conduction system disturbances

### Veins (25%): migratory thrombophlebitis

- subcutaneous / superficial venous thrombophlebitis
- mediastinal widening ← edema ← thrombosis of SVC
- thrombosis of IVC, veins in upper extremities
- deep vein thrombosis in lower extremities
- Budd-Chiari syndrome (3%)
- portal vein thrombosis (9%)

### Systemic arteries

- saccular arterial aneurysm formation (⅓)
- arterial occlusion/ pulseless disease (²/³)

### Pulmonary artery

- increased perihilar radiopacity ← pulmonary artery aneurysm
- in situ pulmonary artery thrombosis
- pulmonary artery thromboembolism ← DVT ← hypercoagulable state

### Lung

- multiple subpleural wedge-shaped / ill-defined increased opacities ← areas of
- ill-defined ground-glass opacities with consolidation = hemorrhage ← ruptured
- pleural effusion

## Clinical

- **Rx:** systemic corticosteroids for disease exacerbation
- **Cx:** perforation, fistula, hemorrhage
