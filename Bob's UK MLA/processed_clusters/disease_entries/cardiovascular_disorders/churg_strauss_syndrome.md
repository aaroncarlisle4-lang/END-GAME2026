# Disease: CHURG-STRAUSS SYNDROME

**Source section:** CARDIOVASCULAR DISORDERS

## Definition

ALLERGIC ANGIITIS AND GRANULOMATOSIS = EOSINOPHILIC GRANULOMATOSIS WITH rare multisystemic disorder with necrotizing vasculitis of small vessels associated with

## Key Facts

- **Etiology:** ? hypersensitivity response to an inhaled agent
- **Incidence:** 1-7÷1,000,000 annually
- **Mean age:** 28 (range, 20-40) years; M÷F = 1÷1

## Imaging Findings

### General

- vascular aneurysms + thrombosis

### HRCT

- subpleural consolidation / ground-glass attenuation in a lobular distribution (59%)
- bilateral centrilobular nodules
- diffuse interstitial reticular / reticulonodular opacities
- interlobular septal thickening
- bronchial wall thickening
- uni- / bilateral pleural (30%)
- hyperinflation
- mediastinal / hilar lymphadenopathy
- pericardial effusion

### Lung (most frequent): intraalveolar hemorrhage

- normal CXR (25%)
- often transient peripheral widespread nonsegmental air-space opacities without zonal
- diffuse miliary nodules:
- nodules may coalesce up to 2 cm (rare)
- cavitation is atypical (and suggests infection)
- eosinophilic pleural effusions (29%)

### GI tract (20%): ulceration, hemorrhage, perforation

- mesenteric vasculitis (= polyarteritis nodosa)
- eosinophilic gastroenteritis (= bowel wall infiltration by eosinophils)

### CNS (commonly involved)

- micro- or macrohemorrhages of cerebral ischemia

### Kidney:

- focal segmental glomerulonephritis

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** 85% 5-year survival; death from intraabdominal /cardiac complications, cerebral
- **Rx:** corticosteroids, cyclophosphamide
