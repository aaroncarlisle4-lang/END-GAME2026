# Disease: TAKAYASU ARTERITIS

**Source section:** CARDIOVASCULAR DISORDERS

## Definition

PULSELESS DISEASE = AORTITIS SYNDROME AORTOARTERITIS = IDIOPATHIC MEDIAL AORTOPATHY = AORTIC ARCH SYNDROME = MARTORELL idiopathic chronic relapsing necrotizing obliterative segmental large-vessel panarteritis

## Key Facts

- **Etiology:** probably cell-mediated autoimmune process
- **Prevalence:** 1÷1,000,000; 2.2% (at autopsy)
- **Incidence:** 2.6÷1,000,000 annually (USA)
- **Age:** 12-66 years; M÷F = 1÷8; especially in Asians
- **Histo:** (a)   Acute stage: granulomatous infiltrative process focused on elastic fibers of media
- **Location:** abdominal aorta > descending thoracic > arch

## Imaging Findings

### General

- arterial wall thickening + contrast enhancement
- full-thickness calcification (= chronic disease)
- mural thrombi

### CXR

- widened supracardiac shadow > 3.0 cm
- wavy / scalloped appearance of lateral margin of descending aorta
- aortic calcifications (15%) commonly in aortic arch + descending aorta
- focal decrease of pulmonary vascularity

### NECT

- high-attenuation arterial walls of variable thickness
- calcifications in aorta + its branches
- concentric thickening of vessel wall > 3 mm:
- "double ring" appearance = poorly enhanced internal ring (= swollen intima) +
- mural thrombus, stenosis, occlusion
- vessel ectasia, aneurysm, ulcer
- typically linear wall calcifications after > 5 years (usually sparing ascending aorta)

### MR

- thickened enhancing arterial wall (on fat-suppressed T1)
- mural edema of arterial wall (STIR) = measure of disease activity
- bright T2 signal around inflamed vessel
- mural thrombus, multilevel stenosis
- thickening of aortic valve cusps
- pericardial effusion
- dilatation of ascending aorta
- segmental dilatation with stenotic regions of CCA + subclavian artery
- complete occlusion of supra-aortic arteries at their origin, + multiple collaterals

### US

- "macaroni sign" long segment of smooth homogeneous concentric wall thickening
- increased intimal-medial thickness = reliable marker for disease activity

### Brachiocephalic arteries

- multisegmented dilatation of carotid artery producing segmental septa
- diffuse homogeneous circumferential thickening of vessel wall in proximal common
- increase in flow velocity + turbulence
- distal CCA, ICA, ECA spared with dampened waveforms

### Pulmonary arteries (50-80%)

- dilatation of pulmonary trunk (19%)
- nodular thrombi (3%)
- "pruned tree" appearance / flame-shaped stenosis of pulmonary arteries (66%)
- unilateral occlusion / aneurysm of pulmonary artery
- systemic-pulmonary artery shunts
- wall thickening with enhancement (acute)
- luminal + mural calcium deposition (chronic)
- pulmonary artery stenosis / occlusion (chronic)

## Clinical

- **Cx:** rapid aneurysm expansion → aortic rupture (33%)
- **Rx:** high-dose glucocorticoids, angioplasty after decline of active inflammation
