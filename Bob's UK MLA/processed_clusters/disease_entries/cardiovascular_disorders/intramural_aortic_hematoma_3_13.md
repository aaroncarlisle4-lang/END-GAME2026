# Disease: INTRAMURAL AORTIC HEMATOMA (3-13%

**Source section:** CARDIOVASCULAR DISORDERS

## Definition

ATYPICAL AORTIC DISSECTION = IMH aortic dissection without demonstrable intimal flap / penetrating aortic ulcer

## Imaging Findings

### General

- NO intimal tear / flap at initial imaging
- NO penetrating atherosclerotic ulcer
- DIAGNOSTIC eccentric cuff / crescent of aortic wall > 7 mm thick with a density of 60-70
- attenuation identical to intraluminal blood after 1 week
- decreased thickness within a few weeks
- complete resolution within 1 year
- displacement of intimal calcifications maintaining a semicircular / circular curvilinear

### CECT

- nonenhancing mural region of low attenuation with smooth border maintaining a constant
- predictors of outcome:

### MR

- crescent-shaped eccentric wall thickening
- T2 white blood imaging:
- hyperintense signal (< 7 days of age)
- intermediate signal intensity (≥ 7 days)
- subacute / chronic hematoma on T1 black blood imaging:
- subacute isointense (= oxyhemoglobin)
- hyperintense hematoma (= methemoglobin)
- tiny intimal orifice ± connection with intercostal / lumbar artery

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** good
