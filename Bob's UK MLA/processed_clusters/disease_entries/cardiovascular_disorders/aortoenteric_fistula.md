# Disease: AORTOENTERIC FISTULA

**Source section:** CARDIOVASCULAR DISORDERS

## Definition

life-threatening condition complication of atherosclerotic aortic aneurysms WITHOUT previous aortic surgery / trauma complication of aortic reconstructive surgery / stent graft rare life-threatening subtype of intramural hematoma

## Key Facts

- **Incidence:** 0.007÷1,000,000 annually
- **Location:** horizontal + ascending duodenum (80%)
- **Frequency:** 3.5% of all deaths ← upper esophageal bleeding

## Imaging Findings

### General

- ectopic gas adjacent to / within aorta (KEY FINDING)
- obliteration of fat plane between aorta and bowel
- ± hematoma in retroperitoneum / bowel wall or lumen
- loss of fat plane between aorta and adjacent bowel, pseudoaneurysm, disruption of aortic
- tethering / puckering of bowel wall toward aorta
- perigraft soft-tissue edema, fluid / hematoma:
- hyperattenuating fluid collection / mass acute = hematoma / partially thrombosed
- ectopic gas
- focal bowel wall thickening (challenging if involved bowel loop not distended)
- breach of the aortic wall
- extravasation of contrast material into bowel lumen (extremely rare but most SPECIFIC)
- leakage of enteric contrast into periaortic space (rare)

### CT

- enhancing subepithelial esophageal mass with fistulous tract between aorta +

## Clinical

- **N.B.:** resolution within 2-3 months after surgery
