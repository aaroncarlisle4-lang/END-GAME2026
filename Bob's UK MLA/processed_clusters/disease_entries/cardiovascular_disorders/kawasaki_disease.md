# Disease: KAWASAKI DISEASE

**Source section:** CARDIOVASCULAR DISORDERS

## Definition

MUCOCUTANEOUS LYMPH NODE SYNDROME acute febrile generalized multisystem vasculitis of unknown cause involving medium-sized

## Key Facts

- **Incidence:** 20.8÷100,000 annually in children < 5 years of age; highest in Asian countries
- **Histo:** necrotizing panvasculitis
- **Age:** < 5 years of age (in 85%); peak age of 2-3 years;
- **Associated with:** polyarthritis (30-50%), aseptic meningitis (25%), hepatitis (5-10%),

## Imaging Findings

### General

- mild coronary artery dilatation (in up to 50%)
- coronary artery aneurysm: LCA (²/³), RCA (⅓); proximal segment in 70%; as early
- coronary artery stenosis (39%) ← thrombus formation in aneurysm with intimal
- coronary artery occlusion (8%) in aneurysms > 9 mm

### CNS

- subdural effusion
- cerebral infarction, atrophy
- lesion with reversible T2 hyperintensity in splenium of corpus callosum + subcortically
- posterior reversible encephalopathy syndrome

### Neck

- typically unilaterally enlarged nonsuppurative lymph nodes

### Abdomen

- intestinal pseudoobstruction
- focal colitis
- bowel infarction → ischemic stricture
- transient gallbladder hydrops (15%)

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** 0.4-3.0% mortality (from myocardial infarction / myocarditis with congestive heart
- **Rx:** aspirin (100 mg/kg per day) + gamma globulin
