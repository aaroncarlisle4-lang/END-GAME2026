# Disease: MAY-THURNER SYNDROME

**Source section:** CARDIOVASCULAR DISORDERS

## Definition

ILIAC VEIN COMPRESSION SYNDROME = ILEOCAVAL COMPRESSION SYNDROME = COCKETT obstruction of left common iliac vein caused by crossing of right common iliac artery

## Key Facts

- **Path:** spurlike projection in proximal left CIV in 22% (autopsy), in 49-62% of left
- **Incidence:** 0.2% annually in patients evaluated for venous thrombosis of left lower extremity
- **Age:** 2nd-4th decade; M < F

## Imaging Findings

### General

- compression of left common iliac vein on CT / venography
- tortuous pelvic venous collaterals joining contralateral veins

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** 60% of individuals with May-Thurner syndrome develop postthrombotic syndrome
- **Cx:** deep vein thrombosis (L÷R = 5÷1), chronic venous stasis ulcer; pulmonary embolism;
