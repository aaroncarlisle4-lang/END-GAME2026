# Disease: CONGESTIVE HEART FAILURE

**Source section:** CARDIOVASCULAR DISORDERS

## Definition

increase in circulating blood volume with diminishing cardiac function → elevation of

## Key Facts

- **Frequency:** most common cause of interstitial + airspace edema of lungs

## Imaging Findings

### General

- enlarged heart:
- enlargement of LA (mitral stenosis)
- enlargement of LA + LV (mitral regurgitation)
- enlargement of LV (aortic valve disease)
- basal oligemia (= arterial + venous constriction)
- hyperemia of upper lobes:
- vessel diameter equal to / greater than comparable lower zone vessels
- upper zone arteries wider than accompanying bronchus
- vessel diameter in 1st anterior intercostal space > 3 mm
- perihilar haze = loss of sharp definition of vascular markings in large perihilar
- thickening of interlobular septa (Kerley lines)
- peribronchial cuffing = poorly defined increased bronchial wall thickness
- thickening of interlobar fissures ← fluid accumulation in subpleural connective tissue
- usually right pleural effusion (if PAWP > 20 mmHg)
- pulmonary venous pressure > 20 mmHg
- poorly defined patchy acinar opacities
- coalescence of acinar consolidation, particularly in medial ⅓ of lung
- air bronchograms
- butterfly / bat-wing distribution of consolidation (= consolidated hilum + uninvolved
- always coexists with interstitial pulmonary edema
- abnormal hepatic vein Doppler spectrum:
- abnormally tall a wave (increased right atrial pressure + volume toward end-diastole)
- abnormally tall v wave (increased right atrial pressure + volume toward end systole) of
- normal relationship between S and D waves

### Hepatobiliary

- GB wall edema
- periportal edema
- enlarged IVC

## Clinical

- **N.B.:** flow inversion is never seen in pulmonary edema of renal failure / overhydration /
