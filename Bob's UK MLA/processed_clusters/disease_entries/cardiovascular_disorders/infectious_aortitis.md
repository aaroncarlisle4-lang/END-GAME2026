# Disease: INFECTIOUS AORTITIS

**Source section:** CARDIOVASCULAR DISORDERS

## Definition

inflammation of aorta caused by bacterium / virus / fungus LUETIC AORTITIS

## Key Facts

- **Prevalence:** in 10-15% of untreated patients (accounts for death in ⅓)
- **Path:** periaortitis (via lymphatics), mesaortitis (via vasa vasorum) = primarily disease of
- **Age:** 40-65 years

## Imaging Findings

### General

- thickening of aortic wall + intramural gas (KEY FEATURE)
- thick aortic wall ← fibrous + inflammatory tissue
- saccular (75%) / fusiform (25%) dilatation of ascending aorta:
- small saccular aneurysm often protrudes from fusiform aneurysm
- fine pencil-like calcifications of intima (15-20%) in ascending aorta, late in disease

## Clinical

- **N.B.:** aorta may appear normal on imaging
- **Rx:** empiric antibiotic therapy against S. aureus and gram-negative rods
