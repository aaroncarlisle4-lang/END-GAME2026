# Disease: CARDIAC LIPOMA

**Source section:** CARDIOVASCULAR DISORDERS

## Definition

very rare benign neoplasm; 2nd most common cardiac tumor

## Key Facts

- **Prevalence:** 60 reported cases
- **Age:** wide range; typically in adults
- **Path:** encapsulated spherical / elliptical solitary mass, often very large (up to 4,800 g) by the
- **Histo:** mature adipocytes surrounded by capsule
- **Associated with:** tuberous sclerosis (with multiple lipomas)
- **Location:** broad-based from

## Imaging Findings

### General

- cardiomegaly, globular-shaped heart
- echogenic / hypoechoic broad-based nonmobile mass
- round mass with smooth contour

### CT

- homogeneous mass of ≤ −50 HU in cardiac chamber / pericardial space

### MR

- mass similar to adjacent subcutaneous / mediastinal fat:
- homogeneously hyperintense on T1WI
- intermediate to high signal intensity on T2WI
- ± few thin septations
- uniform drop in signal intensity with fat saturation
- NO contrast enhancement

### PET

- NO FDG activity

## Clinical

- **Rx:** surgical resection
