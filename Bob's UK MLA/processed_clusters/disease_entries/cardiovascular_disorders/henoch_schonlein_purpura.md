# Disease: HENOCH-SCHÖNLEIN PURPURA

**Source section:** CARDIOVASCULAR DISORDERS

## Definition

## Key Facts

- **Incidence:** 20÷100,000 annually; mostly occurring in winter
- **Peak age:** 7 (range, 3-15) years + adults > 20 years (in up to 30%); M÷F = 1.5÷1.0
- **Path:** acute leukocytoclastic small-vessel vasculitis with IgA deposits at dermoepidermoid

## Imaging Findings

### General

- multifocal short-segment (< 15 cm) small bowel wall thickening of 7-12 mm (←
- "comb" sign = hypervascular engorged vasa recta
- intestinal dilatation
- ± bowel ulceration
- mesenteric fat stranding + adenopathy of < 1.5 cm

### Renal disease (20-100%)

- normal / bilaterally enlarged kidneys
- increased echogenicity of renal cortex
- ± intramural hematoma of urinary bladder / ureter

### Scrotal disease (15-37%)

- mostly bilateral scrotal wall thickening
- epididymal enlargement, reactive hydrocele
- ± orchitis

### CNS (rare)

- hypertensive / uremic encephalopathy
- focal ischemic / hemorrhagic lesions

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** self-limiting without requiring treatment in 37% of adults + 60% of children
