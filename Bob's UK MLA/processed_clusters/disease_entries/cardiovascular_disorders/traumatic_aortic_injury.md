# Disease: TRAUMATIC AORTIC INJURY

**Source section:** CARDIOVASCULAR DISORDERS

## Definition

AORTIC LACERATION = BLUNT TRAUMA TO THORACIC AORTA laceration that disrupts the physical integrity of > 1 structural layer of the aorta aneurysm that exists for > 3 months

## Key Facts

- **Incidence:** > 100,000 people in USA annually
- **Prevalence:** 10-16-20% of all fatalities in high-speed deceleration accidents

## Imaging Findings

### General

- normal admission CXR in 28% (radiographic signs may not develop until 6-36 hours):
- deviation of nasogastric / endotracheal tube to the right of T3-T4 spinous process
- depression of left mainstem bronchus anteroinferiorly > 40° below the horizontal +
- indistinct / blurred aortic contour at arch / descending aorta (53-100% sensitive, 21-
- mediastinal widening > 8 cm at level of origin of left subclavian artery (present in 75-
- mediastinal width to chest width > 0.25
- obscuration of aortopulmonary window (40-100% sensitive, 56-83% specific)
- widened left paraspinal "stripe" > 5 mm (12-83% sensitive, 89-97% specific)
- thickening of right paratracheal stripe > 4-5 mm ← hematoma between pleura + trachea
- left / right "apical pleural cap" sign in 37%
- tracheal compression + displacement toward right (61%)
- rapidly accumulating commonly left-sided hemothorax without evident rib fracture ←
- fractures of 1st + 2nd rib (17%)
- mediastinal fluid often of high density
- obliteration of aorta-fat interface with increased attenuation ← mediastinal / periaortic
- intraluminal low-density filling defect:
- linear = intimal flap
- polypoid = clot
- contour deformity of outer aortic wall = pseudoaneurysm
- contour deformity of inner aortic wall:
- intramural hematoma
- pseudocoarctation = abrupt tapering of the diameter of descending aorta compared
- extravasation of contrast material (extremely rare)
- intimal flap
- intraluminal thick stripes
- pseudoaneurysm
- aortic occlusion (= pseudocoarctation)
- fusiform aneurysm
- aortic wall hematoma
- resistance in advancing guide wire
- intimal irregularity, linear defect, filling defect = intimal flap = posttraumatic dissection
- intramural injury:
- thickening of aortic wall
- posttraumatic coarctation
- transmural laceration:
- contained extravasation = traumatic false aneurysm
- free extravasation = aortic rupture
- well-defined rounded mass in left paramediastinal region
- ± inferior displacement of left mainstem bronchus

## Clinical

- **N.B.:** There are no plain CXR findings of aortic injury (since aortic integrity is maintained
- **Cx:** postoperative paraplegia (9%) ← aortic cross clamping > 30 minutes
- **Prognosis:** enlargement + eventual rupture;
