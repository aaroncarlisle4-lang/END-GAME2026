## Key Facts

- **Prevalence:** 0.3-8.0%
- **Age:** 20-40 years; M÷F = 1÷3 to 1÷4
- **Incidence:** 1-2÷100,000 annually

## Imaging Findings

### General

- complete cessation of flow in one position
- complete disappearance of pulse in one position

### ANGIO

- abnormal course of distal subclavian artery
- focal stenosis / occlusion
- poststenotic dilatation of distal subclavian artery
- aneurysm
- stress test: bandlike / concentric constriction
- mural thrombus ± distal embolization
- venous thrombosis / obstruction
- vascular compression
- collateral arterial circulation

### MR

- denervation-related fatty atrophy of muscles
- effacement of fat planes around compressed plexus
- abnormal intramuscular course of brachial plexus components

## Clinical

- **Rx:** physical therapy; NSAID; muscle relaxants
