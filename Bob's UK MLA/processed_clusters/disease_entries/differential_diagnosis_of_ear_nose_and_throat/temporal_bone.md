# Disease: TEMPORAL BONE

**Source section:** DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS OF EAR, NOSE, AND THROAT

## Definition

osteogenesis imperfecta + hearing loss + blue sclerae in patients in late 2nd / early 3rd

## Key Facts

- **Location:** external ear, middle ear, inner ear

## Imaging Findings

### External auditory canal

- usually lytic changes beginning in petrous pyramid + progressing laterally; otic capsule
- calvarial changes ± basilar impression
- homogeneously dense thickened structurally weak bone (fibro-osseous tissue less dense
- bone expansion with preserved cortex → osseous narrowing of vascular channels +
- lytic lesions (less frequent)
- sparing of membranous labyrinth, facial nerve canal, IAC is the rule
- changes similar to otosclerosis
- moth-eaten permeative osteolysis of temporal bone
