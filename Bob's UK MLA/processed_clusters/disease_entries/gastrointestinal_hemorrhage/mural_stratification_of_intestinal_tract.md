# Disease: MURAL STRATIFICATION OF INTESTINAL TRACT

**Source section:** GASTROINTESTINAL HEMORRHAGE

## Definition

bowel wall thickening with abnormal separation of bowel layers on cross-sectional imaging = layer of fat attenuation (-18 to -64 HU) in submucosa sandwiched between inner + outer hemorrhage secondary to ulcer crater surrounded by a rim of edema overlying submucosal lymphoid follicle

## Imaging Findings

### CECT

- "double halo / target" sign = trilaminar appearance during late arterial phase:
- contrast enhancement of inner layer:
- nonenhanced interposed layer of (3) submucosa with various degrees of attenuation:
- contrast enhancement of outer layer:
- low-density / water-density separation
- signs of bowel infarction:
- free peritoneal fluid
- asymmetric bowel wall enhancement
- persistent enhancement of bowel wall / segmental arteries
- arterial / venous filling defects
- increased density of mesentery
- bowel obstruction
- "snow-cone" appearance of duodenum
