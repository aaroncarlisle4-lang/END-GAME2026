# Disease: SYSTEMIC LUPUS ERYTHEMATOSUS

**Source section:** PNEUMONECTOMY CHEST

## Definition

SLE = most prevalent of the potentially grave connective tissue diseases characterized by (aPL-ab syndrome) characterized by arterial + veno-occlusive disease, thrombocytopenia, small granular vegetations on mitral / aortic valve

## Key Facts

- **Incidence:** 1-10 ÷ 100,000 persons per year; Blacks÷Caucasians = 3÷1; increased risk in
- **Age:** 16-41 years; in adults M÷F = 1÷11 (in ≥ 90% in women of childbearing age); in children
- **Path:** antibodies target cell surface molecules on vascular endothelium and platelets;
- **Histo:** myositis (myocarditis) with perivascular infiltration by lymphocytes and
- **Location:** small joints of hand, wrist, knee, shoulder
- **Prevalence:** kidneys involved in 100% with renal disease developing in 30-50%

## Imaging Findings

### US

- kidney enlarged (early) / diminutive (late stage)
- increased parenchymal echogenicity

### CT

- multiple linear hypoattenuating bands ← vasculitis
- recurrent small uni- / bilateral (½) pleural effusions (in 30-50% during course of
- pleural thickening / fibrosis (50-83%)

### Cardiac valves (18-74%)

- valve leaflet thickening
- Libman-Sacks endocarditis:
- 1-4 mm single / multiple verrucous vegetations

### Pericardium (17-50%)

- exudative pericardial effusion = serositis of pericardium
- abnormal thickening + enhancement of pericardium

### Myocardium

- global left ventricular dysfunction (uncommon)
- cardiomegaly = primary lupus cardiomyopathy

### Vasculature

- coronary atherosclerosis
- marked arterial wall thickening < 100 μm in diameter
- aortitis (uncommon) → dissection, aneurysm
- pulmonary + pleural disease more common than in SLE
- hypomotility of distal ⅓ (13-32%) similar to scleroderma
- mucosal granularity ± ulceration ← reflux esophagitis

### Pancreas

- peripancreatic mesenteric fatty infiltration, phlegmon formation ± glandular
- calcium deposition within ductal system + organ atrophy = chronic pancreatitis

### Liver, spleen

- hepatomegaly, hepatitis, cirrhosis, splenomegaly
- "painful" ascites

### Bowel

- features of gastritis
- features of bowel ischemia with ← small vessel vasculitis
- mesenteric ischemia: colitis, pseudoobstruction, ileus, thumbprinting, luminal

### Joints

- CHARACTERISTIC symmetric nonerosive nondeforming polyarthritis of hands
- Jaccoud arthropathy (10%)
- tumoral calcinosis

### Ligaments

- deformities in initially reversible SLE ← ligamentous instability and laxity

### Bone

- osteonecrosis / AVN (5-50%) of weight-bearing joints:
- insufficiency fracture
- septic arthritis and osteomyelitis
- aneurysms in interlobular + arcuate arteries (similar to but less frequent than polyarteritis
- normal / decreased renal size
- hydronephrosis ← detrusor muscle spasm with vesicoureteral reflux / fibrosis of

### Lung (> 50%)

- parenchymal opacification:
- pneumonia (most common) ← bacteria / opportunistic organism
- diffuse alveolar hemorrhage (2-5%):
- bilateral areas of consolidation + ground-glass opacities + septal thickening in
- pulmonary edema
- acute lupus pneumonitis (10-14%):
- uni- / bilateral poorly defined patchy areas of increased density peripherally at
- ground-glass opacity on CT
- cavitating nodules (vasculitis)
- pulmonary fibrosis (< 3%):
- architectural distortion, honeycombing
- subpleural interstitial reticulations in lower lung fields (chronic form) in 3%
- fleeting platelike atelectasis in both bases (? infarction due to vasculitis)
- hilar + mediastinal lymphadenopathy (extremely rare)

### Diaphragm

- linear atelectasis + ill-defined juxtadiaphragmatic areas of increased opacity
- shrinking lung syndrome (0.6%):
- progressive loss of lung volume
- elevated diaphragms
- sluggish diaphragmatic excursions
- cerebral ischemia + infarction:
- reduced diameter / occlusion of ICA
- intracranial venous occlusion (in 29%)
- intracranial hemorrhage (in up to 42%) = parenchymal / subarachnoid hemorrhage,
- cerebral white matter lesions in subcortical and periventricular regions at FLAIR + T2WI
- cerebral atrophy (in about 43%)
- myelopathy (in 1-3%)
- optic neuritis (in 21-48%)

## Clinical

- **Rx:** nonsteroidal antiinflammatory drugs / oral steroids
- **Cx:** valve destruction
- **Prognosis:** end-stage renal disease is common cause of death
