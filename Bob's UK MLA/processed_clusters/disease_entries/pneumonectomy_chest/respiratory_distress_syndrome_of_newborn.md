# Disease: RESPIRATORY DISTRESS SYNDROME OF NEWBORN

**Source section:** PNEUMONECTOMY CHEST

## Definition

RDS = SURFACTANT DEFICIENCY DISORDER = HYALINE MEMBRANE DISEASE acute pulmonary disorder characterized by generalized atelectasis, intrapulmonary shunting,

## Key Facts

- **Frequency:** 6÷1,000 neonates (in 2002); M÷F = 1.8÷1
- **Histo:** uniformly collapsed alveoli + variable distension of alveolar ducts + terminal

## Imaging Findings

### General

- decreased lung expansion (counteracted by respirator therapy)
- symmetric generalized consolidation of variable severity:
- complete "white out" of lung
- diffuse reticulogranular texture (coincides with onset of clinical signs, maximum severity at
- evolving hazy opacities to clearing over several days
- effacement of normal pulmonary vessels
- prominent air bronchograms (distension of compliant airways)
- "pseudoclearing" of RDS
- streaky / mottled lucencies radiating from hila without branching / tapering often outlining
- large subpleural cysts without definable wall usually at diaphragmatic + mediastinal
- pneumothorax (in up to 25%)

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** spontaneous clearing within 7-10 days (mild course in untreated survivors); death
- **Rx:** (1)  Antenatal maternal corticosteroid therapy
