# Disease: LIVER MASS

**Source section:** AND SPLENIC DISORDERS

## Definition

unique group of entities with derangement of embryonic biliary ductal plate development quantification of water diffusion + microcapillary blood perfusion ← based on incoherent less dense than adjacent liver parenchyma during arterial + portal venous phase increased uptake of injected colloid by bone marrow

## Key Facts

- **Associated with:** autosomal recessive (juvenile) polycystic kidney disease

## Imaging Findings

### General

- well-defined lesion with predominant near-water attenuation (0-30 HU) / signal intensity
- variability of cystic component accounts for differences in attenuation / signal intensity:
- septa may contain a range of tissues including epithelium, fibrotic tissue, stroma, neoplastic tissue,
- bright on T1WI ← subacute hematoma
- dark on T2WI ← hemosiderin of chronic hematoma
- detected during hepatic arterial + portal venous phase
- moderately high SI on T2WI within nodule of generally low SI
- marked arterial enhancement on T1WI
- hypointense peripheral rim relative to center (best seen 10 minutes after contrast
- calcium involves entire lesion
- curvilinear / ring calcification
- turtleback / tortoise shell calcifications
- large coarse centrally located calcification (in 10-20%)
- calcification accompanied by desmoplastic reaction
- increased perfusion of quadrate lobe located at posterior aspect of medial segment left
- "increased" perfusion of caudate lobe ← actually decrease of activity elsewhere in
- hot (DIAGNOSTIC) / cold / isoactive with surrounding parenchyma
