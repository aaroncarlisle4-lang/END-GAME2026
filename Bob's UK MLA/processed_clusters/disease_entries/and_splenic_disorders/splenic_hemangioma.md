# Disease: SPLENIC HEMANGIOMA

**Source section:** AND SPLENIC DISORDERS

## Key Facts

- **Prevalence:** 0.03-14% (autopsy); M > F
- **Age:** 20-50 years
- **Histo:** proliferation of vascular channels lined by single layer of endothelium; mostly of
- **Associated with:** generalized angiomatosis, Klippel-Trénaunay-Weber syndrome, Beckwith-

## Imaging Findings

### General

- usually small single lesion < 4 cm, up to 17 cm in size
- foci of speckled / snowflakelike calcifications

### US

- well-marginated predominantly hyperechoic lesion

### CT

- predominantly avascular cystic lesion
- solid areas hypo- / isodense to normal spleen and enhancing

### MR

- hypo- / isointense on T1WI + hyperintense on T2WI
- hypointense areas ← hemosiderin deposits
- progressive centripetal enhancement with persistent uniform enhancement on delayed

### NUC

- no uptake of 99mTc-sulfur colloid

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** slow growth, thus becoming symptomatic in adulthood
