# Disease: MESENCHYMAL HAMARTOMA OF LIVER

**Source section:** AND SPLENIC DISORDERS

## Definition

rare developmental benign cystic liver tumor characterized by proliferative extension along

## Key Facts

- **Path:** well-marginated solitary liver mass with cysts (in 83%) of a few mm up to 14 cm in size
- **Histo:** disordered arrangement of primitive fluid-filled mesenchyme, bile ducts, hepatic
- **Location:** right lobe÷left lobe = 3÷1; 20% pedunculated

## Imaging Findings

### General

- grossly discernible cysts in 80%

### US

- multiple rounded cystic areas on an echogenic background:
- low-level echoes within cysts (= gelatinous contents)
- some blood flow in solid portions + septa (color Doppler)
- may appear solidly echogenic in fetus / younger infant ← microcysts create innumerable

### CT

- multiple lucencies of variable size + attenuation (depending on composition of stromal
- hemorrhage (rare and atypical)
- enhancement of stromal component

### MR

- varying SI (varying concentrations of protein in cystic predominance type) / hypointense on
- marked hyperintensity of cystic locules on T2WI
- solid portions of fibrosis hypointense on all sequences

### NUC

- one / more areas of diminished uptake on sulfur colloid scan

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** 90% long-term survival
- **Rx:** resection
