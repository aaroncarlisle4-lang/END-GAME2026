# Disease: PANCREATIC ACINAR CELL CARCINOMA

**Source section:** AND SPLENIC DISORDERS

## Definition

rare neoplasm of exocrine origin disseminated subcutaneous + intraosseous fat necrosis (usually distal to knees / elbows)

## Key Facts

- **Frequency:** 1-2% of pancreatic exocrine tumors
- **Mean age:** 62 (range, 40-81) years; M÷F = 86÷14; 87% Caucasian; more common than ductal
- **Path:** similar to pancreatoblastoma (= embryonic counterpart)
- **Prevalence:** 4-16% of adult patients
- **Location:** anywhere in pancreas; frequently exophytic
- **Size:** 10 (range, 2-30) cm

## Imaging Findings

### General

- soft lobulated well-defined heterogeneous mass
- partial / complete thin enhancing capsule
- tumor necrosis usually present
- punctate / chunky peripheral / central tumor calcifications (33-50%)
- enhancement less than normal pancreas (greater than ductal adenocarcinomas + less than islet
- moderately vascular tumor + neovascularity + arterial and venous encasement

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** median survival of 7-9 months; 57% (26%) [6%] 1-year (3-year) [5-year] survival,
