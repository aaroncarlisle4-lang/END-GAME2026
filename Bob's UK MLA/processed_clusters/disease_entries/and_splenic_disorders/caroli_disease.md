# Disease: CAROLI DISEASE

**Source section:** AND SPLENIC DISORDERS

## Definition

COMMUNICATING CAVERNOUS ECTASIA OF INTRAHEPATIC BILE DUCTS rare congenital autosomal recessive disorder characterized by multifocal segmental saccular both features of Caroli disease and congenital hepatic fibrosis arrest of bile duct remodeling during embryogenesis + during later development of more

## Key Facts

- **Path:** communication with biliary tree
- **Age:** childhood + 2nd-3rd decade, occasionally in infancy; M÷F = 1÷1
- **Associated with:** benign renal tubular ectasia, medullary sponge kidney (in 80%), infantile
- **Location:** diffuse / segmental or lobar

## Imaging Findings

### General

- multiple small localized / diffusely scattered cysts converging toward porta hepatis
- beaded ducts with alternating dilated and strictured segments
- sludge / pus / calculi in dilated ducts
- "central dot" sign = enhancing fibrovascular bundle of portal vein radicles completely
- segmental saccular > fusiform beaded dilatation of intrahepatic bile ducts extending to
- ± filling defects ← intraductal calculi
- bridge formation across dilated lumina
- intraluminal bulbar protrusions
- frequent ectasia of extrahepatic ducts + CBD
