# Disease: HEPATIC ADENOMA

**Source section:** AND SPLENIC DISORDERS

## Definition

HEPATOCELLULAR ADENOMA = LIVER CELL ADENOMA epithelial neoplasm composed of normal hepatocytes TELANGIECTATIC FOCAL NODULAR HYPERPLASIA = TELANGIECTATIC ADENOMA usually incidentally discovered tumor (multiple in about 50%) occurring exclusively in rare condition with > 10 adenomas in both hepatic lobes without history of steroid therapy /

## Key Facts

- **Prevalence:** half as common as FNH; increasing with duration of oral contraceptive use + size
- **Path:** spherical benign growth with high incidence of hemorrhage + necrosis + fatty change;
- **Histo:** hepatocytes arranged in sheets without acinar structure (no portal or central vein),
- **Peak age:** 40 years; young women in childbearing age (90%); not seen in males unless on
- **Location:** right lobe of liver in subcapsular location (75%); multiple in up to 21%
- **Age:** young woman with a history of oral contraceptive usage; obese patient
- **Genetics:** nonfunctioning tumor suppressor gene located on chromosome 12q24 ← biallelic
- **Associated with:** male hormone administration, glycogen storage disease, familial

## Imaging Findings

### General

- spherical / ovoid well-circumscribed mass
- frequently heterogeneous ← dilated sinusoids, blood-filled (pelioid) spaces, focal necrosis,
- pseudocapsule (17-31%)
- intraparenchymal / pedunculated (in 10%)
- unusual "nodule-in-nodule" appearance in large tumors (DDx: hepatocellular carcinoma)
- eccentric dystrophic calcifications (5-15%)

### CT

- round usually isoattenuating mass (without hemorrhage)
- mass of decreased density ← gross fat (7-10%) + areas of necrosis (30-40%)
- hyperdense areas of fresh intratumoral hemorrhage (15-50%)
- macroscopic fat in tumor (7%)

### CECT

- iso- / hypoattenuating on portal venous + delayed-phase images
