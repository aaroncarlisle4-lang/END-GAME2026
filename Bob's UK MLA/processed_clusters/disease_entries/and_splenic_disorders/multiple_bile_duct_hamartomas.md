# Disease: MULTIPLE BILE DUCT HAMARTOMAS

**Source section:** AND SPLENIC DISORDERS

## Definition

BILIARY MICROHAMARTOMAS = VON MEYENBURG COMPLEX

## Key Facts

- **Prevalence:** 0.7% at autopsy, 2.8% at microscopy
- **Etiology:** failure of involution of embryonic interlobular bile ducts = part of fibropolycystic
- **Path:** communication with biliary system usually obliterated
- **Histo:** cluster of proliferated bile ducts lined by single layer of cuboidal cells embedded in
- **Size:** 0.1-15 mm
- **Location:** random / scattered; periportal; multiple / solitary

## Imaging Findings

### General

- nonspecific imaging appearance of small well-defined lesions
- variable enhancement pattern: none / rim / slow homogeneous

### CT

- multiple scattered round / oval / irregular hypodense lesions (44% occult on CT)
- rim of little peripheral / no enhancement

### US

- multiple small cysts / echogenic areas (if size not resolved)
- ± comet-tail (ring-down) artifact

### MR

- small well-defined hypointense lesions on T1WI
- iso- / slightly hyperintense on T2WI
