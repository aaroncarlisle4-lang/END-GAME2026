# Disease: EMBRYONAL RHABDOMYOSARCOMA OF BILIARY TREE

**Source section:** AND SPLENIC DISORDERS

## Definition

rare tumor most commonly arising from CBD

## Key Facts

- **Frequency:** 1% of liver tumors in children
- **Path:** intraluminal biliary mass / cluster of grapelike masses (similar to rhabdomyosarcoma of
- **Histo:** same as sarcoma botryoides; positive reactions for muscle markers
- **Location:** common bile duct (most frequently) and intrahepatic bile ducts → invasion of liver
- **Size:** 8-20-cm

## Imaging Findings

### General

- bulky heterogeneous mass in porta hepatis
- intrahepatic bile duct dilatation
- regional lymphadenopathy
- displacement / invasion of duodenum, stomach, pancreas
- large bulky intraluminal mass / grapelike cluster of intraluminal masses → focally

### US

- solitary heterogeneous hypoechoic mass
- multiple hypoechoic nodules separated by septa
- portal vein displacement without thrombosis
- multiple tumor arteries with low-resistance spectrum

### CT

- homo- / heterogeneous hypo- / hyperattenuating mass
- ± prominent fluid component
- NO / intense heterogeneous globular enhancement

### MR

- T1 hypointense + T2 hyperintense / predominantly fluid-intensity mass in CBD or biliary
- heterogeneous intrahepatic mass with large areas of fluid
- partially cystic mass within / adjacent to CBD causing mural irregularity of duct

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** 78% survival with local disease after multimodal therapy; total resection possible
