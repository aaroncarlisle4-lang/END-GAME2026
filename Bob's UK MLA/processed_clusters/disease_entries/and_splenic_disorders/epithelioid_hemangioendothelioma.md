# Disease: EPITHELIOID HEMANGIOENDOTHELIOMA

**Source section:** AND SPLENIC DISORDERS

## Definition

rare primary low-grade malignant vascular tumor of liver (soft tissue, skin, bone, lung)

## Key Facts

- **Incidence:** 0.1÷100,000 annually
- **Path:** multifocal nodules varying in size from a few mm to several cm involving both lobes of
- **Histo:** dendritic spindle-shaped cells + epithelioid round cells in a matrix of myxoid + fibrous

## Imaging Findings

### General

- multiple predominantly peripheral nodules
- coalescence of nodules into confluent masses
- peripheral subcapsular growth (in 75%) without capsular bulge
- capsular retraction (in 25-69%) ← fibrotic reaction
- increased tumor vascularity
- hypertrophy of uninvolved liver
- hepatic calcifications within myxoid stroma (in 15%)

### US

- individual / confluent nodules or diffusely heterogeneous echotexture
- nodules with echogenicity unrelated to size:
- hypoechoic ← central core of myxoid stroma
- hyperechoic ± hypoechoic rim
- isoechoic with hypoechoic halo
- no blood flow

### CT

- homogeneously ↓ attenuation ← myxoid + hyalinized stroma
- capsular retraction in peripheral lesion

### CECT

- target / halo pattern = nonenhanced outer rim of avascular tissue + enhanced inner
- peripheral + progressive centripetal enhancement with incomplete fill-in on delayed phase
- enhanced / nonenhanced tumor center on delayed imaging

### MR

- central hypointensity on T1WI
- "target" sign / concentric zones = alternating high- and low-signal-intensity rings on T2WI:
- central zone of fibrosis / hemorrhage / necrosis
- rim of cellular proliferation + edema
- hyperintense center + thin rim of hypointensity on T2WI
- multilayered targetlike appearance with prominent rimlike enhancement
- central nodular enhancement (38%)
- "lollipop" sign = synchronous targetlike enhancement of tumor mass + along hepatic /

### ANGIO

- hyper- and hypovascularity (dependent upon degree of sclerosis + hyalinization)
- invasion ± occlusion of portal + hepatic veins

### NUC

- decreased perfusion to central myxoid tumor portion + increased perfusion to cellular areas
- photopenic defect on static sulfur colloid scan
- NOT gallium avid
- moderate to intense uptake on PET

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** 20% die within 2 years, 20% survive for 5-28 years ± treatment
- **Rx:** liver transplantation (without spread); radical resection; transarterial chemoembolization
