# Disease: HEPATIC ANGIOSARCOMA

**Source section:** AND SPLENIC DISORDERS

## Definition

HEMANGIOENDOTHELIAL SARCOMA = KUPFFER CELL SARCOMA = HEMANGIOSARCOMA

## Key Facts

- **Prevalence:** 0.14-0.25÷1,000,000; < 2% of all primary liver neoplasms; most common
- **Associated with:** hemochromatosis, anabolic steroids, cirrhosis, von Recklinghausen disease
- **Histo:** vascular derivation confirmed by reactivity to factor VIII-related antigen, CD31 and
- **Age:** 6th-7th decade; M÷F = 4÷1

## Imaging Findings

### General

- portal vein invasion
- hemorrhagic ascites
- unifocal / multifocal / infiltrative lesion
- circumferential displacement of residual thorotrast

### NUC

- single / multiple photopenic areas on sulfur colloid scan
- increased gallium uptake
- perfusion blood pool mismatch (initial decrease followed by slow increase in RBC

### US

- solid / mixed mass with anechoic areas (hemorrhage / necrosis)
- multiple nodules
- diffuse heterogeneous echotexture of entire liver

### CT

- hypodense masses with high-density regions (hemorrhage) / low-attenuation regions (old
- focal areas of peripheral enhancement with fill-in on dynamic CT as in large hemangioma

### MR

- hypointense on T1WI with irregular areas of high signal (hemorrhage)
- heterogeneously hyperintense on T2WI + fluid-fluid levels
- markedly heterogeneous enhancement: predominantly peripheral, progressive on delayed

### ANGIO

- hypervascular stain around tumor periphery in late arterial phase with puddling; NO arterial

### PET

- may demonstrate marked uptake in liver tumor
- localization of extrahepatic disease
