# Disease: PANCREAS DIVISUM

**Source section:** AND SPLENIC DISORDERS

## Definition

most common congenital anomaly of pancreatic ductal system

## Key Facts

- **Prevalence:** 4-14% in autopsy series; 3-8% in ERCP series; 5-10% on MRCPs of normal
- **Age:** young / middle-aged adult; M<F

## Imaging Findings

### General

- "dominant dorsal duct" sign = larger dorsal duct relative to smaller ventral duct
- continuity of large dorsal pancreatic duct with duct of Santorini draining through minor
- small ventral pancreatic duct (of Wirsüng) drains a portion of pancreatic head including the

### US

- bulky hypoechoic pancreas + peripancreatic inflammation / pseudocyst formation
- absence of "stack sign" (= visualization of bile duct and pancreatic duct running parallel
- contrast injection into major papilla shows CBD + short narrow ventral pancreatic duct
- contrast injection into minor papilla fills dominant dorsal pancreatic duct of larger caliber
- no communication between ventral + dorsal ducts
- oblique fat cleft between ventral + dorsal pancreas (25%)
- failure to see union of dorsal + ventral pancreatic ducts (rare)
- noninvasive multiplanar visualization of pancreatic duct:
- visualization of main pancreatic (dorsal) duct coursing anterior to CBD before draining
- usually prominent dorsal duct with depiction of relative functional duct obstruction
- findings of acute pancreatitis: bulky pancreas, pancreatic necrosis, peripancreatic
- findings of chronic pancreatitis: significant atrophy of ventral duct and dilatation of dorsal

## Clinical

- **Cx:** ERCP-induced pancreatitis (5% risk)
- **Rx:** surgical / endoscopic minor papillotomy / sphincterotomy; insertion of stent into minor
