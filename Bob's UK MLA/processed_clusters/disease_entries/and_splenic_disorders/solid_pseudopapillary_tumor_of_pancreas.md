# Disease: SOLID PSEUDOPAPILLARY TUMOR OF PANCREAS

**Source section:** AND SPLENIC DISORDERS

## Definition

SOLID AND PAPILLARY NEOPLASM = SOLID AND CYSTIC TUMOR = PAPILLARY-CYSTIC NEOPLASM = rare slow-growing low-grade malignant tumor; often misclassified as nonfunctioning

## Key Facts

- **Prevalence:** 1-2% of all pancreatic tumors
- **Mean age:** 28 (range, 2-85) years; M÷F = 1÷9; predilection for black and East Asian patients
- **Path:** large well-encapsulated solitary mass with considerable hemorrhagic necrosis + cystic
- **Histo:** sheets + cords of cells arranged around a fibrovascular stroma
- **Location:** pancreatic tail (36%), pancreatic head (34%); frequently exophytic obscuring

## Imaging Findings

### General

- well-encapsulated inhomogeneous round / lobulated pancreatic mass with completely solid ±
- may be completely cystic (when complicated by extensive necrosis + internal hemorrhage)
- compression of adjacent structures without invasion
- fluid-fluid or fluid-debris level (10-20%) ← hematocrit effect
- ± stippled / punctate / amorphous dystrophic calcification (33%) rimlike in tumor capsule or
- hypovascular with contrast enhancement less than pancreas / enhancement of solid tissue

### US

- echogenic rim of tumor capsule
- echogenic mass with necrotic center / completely cystic with subcapsular rim of tumor

### CT

- solid tumor component isoattenuating to pancreas
- cystic component of 20-50 HU
- hypoattenuating tumor pseudocapsule

### MR

- hypointense fibrous capsule on T1WI + T2WI (80-100%)
- solid well-circumscribed tumor:
- iso- to hypointense on T1WI compared to pancreas
- mildly increased T2 signal intensity
- foci of high SI on T1WI (in 80-100%) + variable T2 signal intensity ← variable
- T2 signal intensity close to fluid in mostly cystic variety
- enhancing soft-tissue components are uniformly present
- early peripheral heterogeneous / ring enhancement greater than pancreas (70%)
- gradual accumulation (= progressive fill-in) of contrast material within tumor
- hypointense to pancreas on delayed images

### ANGIO

- hypo- to avascular with displacement of vessels
- mild peripheral blush

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** (1) excellent after excision
- **Rx:** complete surgical excision (95% cure rate)
