- **Genetics:** sporadic (mostly), familial syndrome (1-2%) with genetic alterations involving
- **Prevalence:** 1-5÷100,000 population; 1-2% of all pancreatic neoplasms
- **Mean age:** 47 (range, 7-83) years; 25 years for MEN 1; M=F
- **Histo:** sheets of uniform polygonal cells resembling normal islet cells with round / oval nuclei
- **Location:** gastrinoma triangle (most common)
- **Size:** mostly 1-5 (range, < 1 cm to > 20) cm in diameter
- **Peak age:** 5th decade; 8% in patients < 20 years; M÷F = 1.3÷1
- **Incidence:** 1÷20,000,000 annually (rare)
- **Age:** 40-60 (range, 19-84) years; M = F
- **Associated with:** MEN 1, NF1
- **Frequency:** 50% of all pancreatic endocrine tumors
- **Path:** adenoma / hyperplasia
