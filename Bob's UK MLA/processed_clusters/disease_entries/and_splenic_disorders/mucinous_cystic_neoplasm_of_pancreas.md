## Key Facts

- **Frequency:** 10% of pancreatic cystic neoplasm; 1% of all pancreatic neoplasms
- **Mean age:** 47 (range, 20-95) years; in 50% between 40-60 years; M÷F = 1÷9
- **Path:** large smooth round / lobulated multiloculated cystic mass encapsulated by a layer of
- **Histo:** cysts lined by tall columnar, mucin-producing cells subtended by a densely cellular
- **Location:** often in pancreatic tail (90%) / body, head (rare)

## Imaging Findings

### General

- well-demarcated thick-walled mass with < 2 mm thin septa
- solid papillary excrescences protrude into the interior of tumor (= sign of malignancy)
- amorphous discontinuous peripheral mural calcifications (10-15%)
- hypovascular mass with sparse neovascularity
- ± vascular encasement and splenic vein occlusion
- great propensity for invasion of adjacent organs

### US

- cysts may contain low-level echoes

### CT

- fine internal septa + small intramural nodules may not be visible ± contrast enhancement →
- cysts with attenuation values of water; may have different levels of attenuation within
- enhancement of cyst walls

### MR

- unilocular / mildly septated typically mucin-filled cystic lesion of homogeneous T1
- mildly thick enhancing septa (frequent)
- thick delayed-enhancing fibrotic wall
- pertinent negatives:
- NO communication with pancreatic duct
- NO enhancing soft-tissue components

### ANGIO

- predominantly avascular mass
- cyst wall + solid components may demonstrate small areas of vascular blush +
- displacement of surrounding arteries + veins by cysts
- round thick-walled cystic lesions in liver
- incidental small (rarely large) unilocular cyst ± thick wall indistinguishable from
- NO ductal communication / papillary projections

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** invariably transformation into cystadenocarcinoma; 17-63% 5-year survival rate
- **Rx:** complete surgical excision (5-year survival rate of 74-90%)
