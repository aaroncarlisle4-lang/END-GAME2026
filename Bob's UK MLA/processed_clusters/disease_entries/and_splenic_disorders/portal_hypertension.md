# Disease: PORTAL HYPERTENSION

**Source section:** AND SPLENIC DISORDERS

## Definition

portal venous pressure > 10 mmHg increase in sinusoidal pressure ← deposition of collagen in spaces of Disse + splenic vein occlusion / superior mesenteric vein occlusion

## Imaging Findings

### General

- portal vein > 13 mm (57% sensitive, 100% specific)
- SMV + splenic vein > 10 mm; coronary vein > 4 mm; recanalized umbilical vein > 3 mm
- loss of respiratory increase of splanchnic vein diameters of < 20% (81% sensitive, 100%
- portal vein aneurysm
- portal vein thrombosis
- cavernous transformation of portal vein
- increased echogenicity + thickening of portal vein walls
- continuous monophasic portal venous flow pattern without respiratory fluctuations
- loss of flow increase in portal venous system during expiration
- DIAGNOSTIC reduction of peak portal vein velocity to < 16 cm/sec (normally 16-40
- DIAGNOSTIC hepatofugal flow in main portal vein
- congestive index > 0.13 cm•sec (= ratio of area of portal vein divided by flow
- may have bidirectional / hepatofugal (< 10%) flow within spontaneous splenorenal
- dilated hepatic artery may demonstrate elevated resistive index > 0.78

### Spontaneous portosystemic shunts / collaterals:

- varices = serpentine tubular rounded structures
- coronary (left gastric) vein > 5-6 mm (in 26%)
- gallbladder wall varices in thickened gallbladder wall (in 80% associated with portal
- lateral bulging of paraspinal interfaces on CXR
- obliteration of azygoesophageal recess on CXR
- cardiophrenic angle masses (with membranous obstruction of IVC)
- hypoechoic channel in ligamentum teres
- arterial signal on Doppler US in 38%
- hepatofugal venous flow (82% sensitive, 100% specific for portal hypertension)

### Spleen

- splenomegaly (nonspecific; absence does NOT rule out portal hypertension)
- Gamna-Gandy nodules in 13% (= small foci of perifollicular + trabecular hemosiderin
- multiple 3-8-mm low-intensity (blooming) spots on FLASH / GRASS images
- multiple hyperechoic spots on US
- multiple faint calcifications on CT
- ascites (nonspecific)

## Clinical

- **Cx:** Acute gastrointestinal bleeding (mortality of 30-50% during 1st bleeding)
