# Disease: ECTOPIC PANCREAS

**Source section:** AND SPLENIC DISORDERS

## Definition

PANCREATIC REST / CHORISTOMA = HETEROTOPIC PANCREAS = MYOEPITHELIAL HAMARTOMA presence of abnormally located pancreatic tissue with its own ductal system and NO vascular

## Key Facts

- **Frequency:** 1-14% of population; M÷F = 2÷1
- **Location:** lesions may be multiple
- **Prevalence:** 1%-2%: in ¾ submucosal
- **Size:** usually 0.5-2.0 (up to 5.0 cm in its largest diameter)

## Imaging Findings

### General

- smooth cone- / nipple-shaped submucosal nodule 1-5 cm (rarely > 3-4 cm) in size
- central umbilication representing orifice of filiform duct

### CT

- flat / ovoid irregularly marginated intramural mass
- homogeneously enhancing with intraluminal extension (= enhancement pattern similar to
- 5-20 mm small cystic change in markedly thickened wall of 2nd part of duodenum ←

### MR

- area with SI similar to native pancreas
- ± surrounding inflammatory changes
- PATHOGNOMONIC presence of ducts on MRCP
- solid mass of intermediate echogenicity

## Clinical

- **Cx:** biliary + intestinal obstruction, massive GI bleeding, pancreatitis, paraduodenal
