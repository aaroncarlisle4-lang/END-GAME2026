# Disease: LIVER TRANSPLANT

**Source section:** AND SPLENIC DISORDERS

## Definition

most common type of liver transplantation in adults

## Key Facts

- **Frequency:** 112,931 liver transplantations in US between 1988 and 2012; annually 6,000 liver
- **Location:** at / near anastomosis

## Imaging Findings

### General

- "starry sky" pattern = diminished liver echogenicity with accentuation of echogenic
- "periportal collar" of low attenuation on CT
- resolution within weeks to months
- present during 1st day; disappears within a few weeks
- wide range of portal vein velocities (15-400 cm/s) with average decrease of 20% over
- dampened mono- / biphasic hepatic vein waveform ← graft edema / compression by

### Thrombosis / stenosis of IVC / hepatic vein (< 1%)

- compression of IVC ← swelling of graft
- reduction in caliber of poststenotic IVC
- prestenotic dilatation of IVC + hepatic veins
- persistent monophasic waveform (sensitive but not specific for significance of stenosis;
- focal narrowing on B-mode imaging
- 3-4-fold velocity increase compared with prestenotic segment
- intraluminal echogenic thrombus
- pressure gradient of > 5 mmHg
- mosaic pattern of perfusion on CT / MR (Budd-Chiari)

### Thrombosis / stenosis of portal vein (1-3%)

- 3- to 4-fold increase in peak systolic flow velocity compared with prestenotic segment
- poststenotic turbulence
- narrowing of portal vein + poststenotic dilatation
- increase in number / caliber of collateral vessels
- filling defect / focal narrowing at anastomosis

### Hepatic artery

- < 72 hours after transplantation RI frequently > 0.8 with return to normal in a few days
- marked focal increase in peak systolic velocity > 200-300 cm/sec or 2-3 x of
- intrahepatic tardus et parvus waveform:
- slowed systolic acceleration time (SAT > 70-80 msec) distal to stenosis (73% sensitive)
- diminished pulsatility (RI < 0.55) ← ischemia
- biliary dilatation (← stricture), infarction, biloma
- abrupt cutoff of flow in hepatic artery (usually at anastomotic site)
- nonenhancement of hepatic artery
- decreased enhancement of hepatic parenchyma ← ischemia / infarction
- lack of hepatic artery flow
- "syndrome of impending thrombosis":
- loss of arterial diastolic flow
- dampening of systolic peak
- multiple hypoechoic lesions in liver periphery (= infarcts)
- may calcify
- may liquefy developing into intrahepatic biloma
- extrinsic mass compressing CHD
- fluid collection adjacent to CHD
- free leakage into peritoneal cavity
- loculated perihepatic collection

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** 1- and 5-year graft survival of 82% and 65%
- **Rx:** percutaneous transluminal angioplasty ± stent placement, surgical thrombectomy,
- **Cx:** hepatic artery thrombosis, hepatic ischemia, biliary stricture, sepsis, graft loss
