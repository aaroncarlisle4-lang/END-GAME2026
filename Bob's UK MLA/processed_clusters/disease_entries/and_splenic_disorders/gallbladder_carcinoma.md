# Disease: GALLBLADDER CARCINOMA

**Source section:** AND SPLENIC DISORDERS

## Key Facts

- **Incidence:** 0.4-4.6% of biliary tract operations; 5th most common gastrointestinal malignancy
- **Prevalence:** 3÷100,000 (USA)
- **Path:** diffusely infiltrating lesion (68%), intraluminal polypoid growth (32%)
- **Location:** fundus (60%), body (30%), neck (10%)

## Imaging Findings

### General

- mass replacing the gallbladder (37-70%) with engulfed gallstones
- irregular eccentric thickening of GB wall (20-30%) ← submucosal spread:
- focal (59%) / diffuse (41%) wall thickening
- intraluminal polypoid / fungating "cauliflower-like" mass with wide base (15-25%)
- pericholecystic infiltration: in 76% focal, in 24% diffuse
- dilatation of biliary tree (38-70%):
- infiltrative tumor growth along cystic duct
- lymph node enlargement causing biliary obstruction
- intraductal tumor spread
- fine granular / punctate flecks of calcification (= mucinous adenocarcinoma)
- lymph node enlargement in porta hepatis
- calcified gallstones
- porcelain gallbladder
- RUQ gas collection (after invasion of adjacent bowel)
- malignant stricture / obstruction of extrahepatic bile ducts / right + left bile duct confluence,
- intraluminal GB filling defect (= tumor / stones)
- mass displacing / invading gallbladder
- intraductal filling defects (= tumor / stones)

### US

- gallbladder replaced by mass with irregular margins + heterogeneous echotexture (= tumor
- immobile intraluminal well-defined round / oval polypoid mass (15-25%) hypoechoic
- solitary polyp > 10 mm
- wide polyp base
- focal wall thickening > 3 mm
- coexisting gallstones
- focal / diffuse thickening of gallbladder wall (20-30%)
- echogenic foci = coexisting gallstones / wall calcifications / tumoral calcification
- tumor inseparable from liver
- hypovascular on color Doppler

### CT

- hypo- / isoattenuating mass in gallbladder fossa:
- low-attenuation areas of necrosis
- areas of enhancement (= viable tumor)
- subtle extension beyond wall of GB
- invasion of liver (40-65%) with protrusion of anterior surface of medial segment of left

### MR

- hypointense mass on T1WI relative to liver
- ill-defined early contrast enhancement
- hyperintense mass on T2WI
- lower apparent diffusion coefficient than for benign lesion

### PET

- FDG uptake within GB polyp greater than liver background
- gallstones located within abscess

## Clinical

- **N.B.:** misdiagnosis by US / CT in 50%, especially in the presence of gallstones
- **Cx:** perforation of gallbladder → abscess formation
- **Prognosis:** 75% unresectable at presentation; average survival is 6 months; 5% (< 5%) 1-year
