# Disease: SEROUS CYSTADENOMA OF PANCREAS

**Source section:** AND SPLENIC DISORDERS

## Definition

MICROCYSTIC CYSTADENOMA = GLYCOGEN-RICH CYSTADENOMA = MICROCYSTIC PANCREATIC benign lobulated neoplasm composed of 1-20 mm small honeycomb cysts containing

## Key Facts

- **Frequency:** ~ 50% of all cystic pancreatic neoplasms
- **Path:** cysts separated by fibrous septa that radiate from a central scar, which may be calcified;
- **Histo:** cyst walls lined by cuboidal / flat glycogen-rich epithelial cells derived from
- **Location:** pancreatic head
- **Mean age:** 65 (range, 34-88) years; in 82% over 60 years of age; M÷F = 1÷2-4
- **Associated with:** von Hippel-Lindau syndrome

## Imaging Findings

### General

- lobulated contour, lack of wall enhancement
- well-demarcated lobulated mass with smooth / nodular contour
- cysts of honeycomb / bunch of grapes appearance
- prominent central stellate scar in 30% (CHARACTERISTIC) ← fibrotic walls of collapsed
- amorphous central calcifications (in 18%) in dystrophic area of stellate central scar
- ± progressive enlargement over months / years
- pancreatic duct + CBD may be displaced / encased / obstructed

### US

- mass as mixture of hypo- and hyperechoic areas
- may appear as solid predominantly echogenic mass ← multiple acoustic interfaces

### CT

- attenuation values close to water
- may appear solid ← small cysts with large amount of fibrous tissue
- contrast enhancement of septa (may be misleading)

### MR

- cluster of small hyperintense cysts on T2WI
- NO visible communication between cysts + pancreatic duct
- late enhancement of thin fibrous septa
- signal void in central scar ← coarse calcifications
- delayed enhancement of scar on contrast-enhanced FLASH

### ANGIO

- hypervascular mass with dilated feeding arteries, dense tumor blush, prominent draining

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** no malignant potential
- **Rx:** surgical excision / follow-up examinations
