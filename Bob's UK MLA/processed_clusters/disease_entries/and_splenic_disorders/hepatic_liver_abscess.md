# Disease: HEPATIC (LIVER) ABSCESS

**Source section:** AND SPLENIC DISORDERS

## Definition

localized collection of pus in the liver ← any infectious process with destruction of the

## Key Facts

- **Location:** multiple in 50%
- **Etiology:** spread of viable amebae from colon → portal system → liver
- **Frequency:** in 1-25% of intestinal amebiasis
- **Age:** 3rd-5th decade; M÷F = 4÷1
- **Size:** 2-12 cm
- **Prevalence:** 0.016%
- **Path:** liquefaction necrosis of liver parenchyma (→ central fluid collections of pus) +
- **Mean age:** 56-64 years; M > F

## Imaging Findings

### General

- hepatomegaly
- elevation of right hemidiaphragm
- pleural effusion
- right lower lobe atelectasis / infiltration
- gas within abscess: esp. Klebsiella

### MR

- hypointense on T1WI + hyperintense on T2WI (72%)
- perilesional edema (35%)
- "double target" sign on T2WI = hyperintense center (fluid) + hypointense sharply
- rim enhancement (86%)
- nonspecific variable appearance indistinct from subacute pyogenic abscess
- nodularity of abscess wall (60%)
- internal septations (30%)
- NOT gas-containing (unless hepatobronchial / hepatoenteric fistula present)
- ± disruption of diaphragm
- very low SI on T1WI + very high SI on T2WI (varies with protein content)
- intense peripheral rim enhancement of capsule of variable thickness + septa
