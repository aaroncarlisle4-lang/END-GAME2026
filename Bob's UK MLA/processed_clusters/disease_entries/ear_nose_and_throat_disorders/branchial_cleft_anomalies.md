# Disease: BRANCHIAL CLEFT ANOMALIES

**Source section:** EAR, NOSE, AND THROAT DISORDERS

## Definition

BRANCHIAL CLEFT CYST failure of involution of branchial cleft PAROTID LYMPHOEPITHELIAL CYST incomplete obliteration of 2nd branchial cleft tract (cervical sinus of His) resulting in sinus above superior laryngeal nerve below superior laryngeal nerve

## Key Facts

- **Frequency:** 2nd most common congenital lesion of head & neck after thyroglossal duct cyst!
- **Age:** middle-aged women
- **Location:** from submandibular triangle (posterior / inferior to angle of mandible) near
- **Path:** 1-10-cm large thin-walled cyst, lined by stratified squamous epithelium overlying

## Imaging Findings

### General

- performed for localization of a fistulous tract in 2nd + 3rd arch anomalies
- cystic mass within parotid or immediate periparotid region (superficial to / deep to parotid
- may extend into adjacent fat-containing parapharyngeal space ± connection to EAC
- oval / round cyst near mandibular angle
- displacement of sternocleidomastoid muscle posteriorly, carotid artery + jugular vein
- cyst may enlarge after upper respiratory tract infection / injury

### US

- well-marginated thin-walled ovoid compressible hypoechoic mass:
- occasionally fine internal echoes = internal debris (due to hemorrhage / infection)
- lack of internal flow
- "beak" sign = curved rim of tissue pointing medially between internal + external carotid
- slight enhancement of capsule
- cystic mass projecting from deep margin of faucial tonsil toward skull base
- unilocular cystic mass within posterior cervical space

## Clinical

- **Cx:** high incidence of infection
