# Disease: LARYNGEAL CARCINOMA

**Source section:** EAR, NOSE, AND THROAT DISORDERS

## Key Facts

- **Frequency:** 98% of all malignant laryngeal tumors; 1% of all cancers; 89,081 cases in USA
- **Age:** 5th / 6th decade of life; M > F
- **Histo:** squamous cell carcinoma (95%)

## Imaging Findings

### General

- nodal involvement
- invasion of cricoid / thyroid cartilage:
- tumor within fatty medullary cavity
- tumor on outer extralaryngeal side of thyroid cartilage
- invasion of pre- and paraepiglottic fat (clinically not detectable!)
- fixation of carotid artery
- extranodal spread
- uptake on PET-CT (50-94% sensitive, 82-100% specific depending on lymph node size)
- enlarged lymph node:
- round lymph node with loss of reniform shape
- necrotic lymph node (= central low attenuation)
- amorphous node with spiculated / indistinct border suggests extracapsular spread
- proximity to laryngeal mass
- cluster of > 3 lymph nodes 6-15 mm in size
- circumferential relatively symmetric growth
- extension into preepiglottic space ± base of tongue ± paraglottic space
- exophytic growth from medial surface of aryepiglottic fold
- growth into fixed portion of epiglottis + paraglottic (= paralaryngeal) space
- submucosal spread into paraglottic space
- ± destruction of thyroid cartilage
- ± involvement of true vocal cords
- > 1 mm thickness of anterior commissure
- invasion of contralateral vocal cord via anterior commissure
- FDG uptake in opposite cord at PET
- tumor > 5 mm inferior to level of vocal cords
- any tissue seen on airway side of cricoid cartilage

## Clinical

- **N.B.:** Reactive periostitis may lead to sclerosis of cartilage
- **Prognosis:** 64% 5-year survival (2013)
