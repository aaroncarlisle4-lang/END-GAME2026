# Disease: MALIGNANT EXTERNAL OTITIS

**Source section:** EAR, NOSE, AND THROAT DISORDERS

## Definition

NECROTIZING EXTERNAL OTITIS severe bacterial infection of the soft tissues + bones at base of skull

## Key Facts

- **Age:** elderly
- **Location:** at bone-cartilage junction of EAC

## Imaging Findings

### General

- osseous erosion of external auditory canal

### CT

- soft-tissue density in external auditory canal (100%)
- fluid in middle ear / mastoid air cells (89%)
- disease around eustachian tube (64%)
- obliteration of fat planes beneath temporal bone (64%)
- involvement of parapharyngeal space (54%)
- masticator space disease (27%)
- mass effect in nasopharynx (54%)
- bone erosion of clivus (9%)
- intracranial extension (9%)
- abnormal signal intensity + obliteration of fat within stylomastoid foramen

## Clinical

- **Cx:** bone destruction; skull base osteomyelitis (clivus, jugular foramen); abscess of epidural
- **Prognosis:** 20% recurrence rate; high mortality rate
