# Disease: LEMIERRE SYNDROME

**Source section:** EAR, NOSE, AND THROAT DISORDERS

## Definition

SEPTIC JUGULAR VEIN THROMBOPHLEBITIS = POSTANGINAL SEPSIS = uncommon potentially life-threatening complication of acute oropharyngeal infection

## Key Facts

- **Age:** adolescent, teenager, young adult > infant, child

## Imaging Findings

### General

- internal jugular venous thrombosis
- edema of the surrounding soft tissues + wall of jugular vein
- patchy consolidation = bronchopneumonia
- septic pulmonary emboli:
- multiple peripheral round / wedge-shaped opacities
- "feeding vessel" sign = vessel leading into nodule
- peripheral enhancement with central areas of reduced attenuation = cavitation
- pleural effusion → empyema

## Clinical

- **Cx:** systemic dissemination of infection: pleuropulmonary septic emboli (97%), septic arthritis
- **Rx:** prolonged IV antibiotics with anaerobic activity, anticoagulation
