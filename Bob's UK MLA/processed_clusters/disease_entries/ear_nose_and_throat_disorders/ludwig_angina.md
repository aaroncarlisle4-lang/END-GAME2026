# Disease: LUDWIG ANGINA

**Source section:** EAR, NOSE, AND THROAT DISORDERS

## Definition

serious potentially life-threatening necrotizing infection of floor of mouth rapidly involving

## Key Facts

- **Age:** usually in young adults
- **Location:** bilateral sublingual and submandibular spaces

## Imaging Findings

### General

- underlying dental disease
- local skin thickening, muscle enlargement
- increased attenuation of subcutaneous fat
- loss of fat planes within submandibular space
- soft tissue emphysema
- ill-defined abscesses in submandibular / sublingual spaces

### CT

- inflammatory change with small cystic spaces = serosanguinous accumulation in floor of
- ± extension deep into submandibular + parapharyngeal spaces + pharynx → airway
- well-defined fluid collection with rimlike enhancement

## Clinical

- **Spread:** by direct extension of infection = tooth apices of 2nd / 3rd molar of jaw extend
- **Cx:** mandibular osteomyelitis, spread of infection into deep fascial spaces of neck,
- **Rx:** IV antibiotics, aggressive drainage, surgical airway creation
