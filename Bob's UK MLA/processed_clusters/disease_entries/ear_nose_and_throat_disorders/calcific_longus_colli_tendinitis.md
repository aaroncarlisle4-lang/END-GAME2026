# Disease: CALCIFIC LONGUS COLLI TENDINITIS

**Source section:** EAR, NOSE, AND THROAT DISORDERS

## Definition

ACUTE CALCIFIC RETROPHARYNGEAL TENDINITIS calcification anterior to C1-C2 ← deposition of calcium hydroxyapatite

## Key Facts

- **Location:** near insertion of longus colli tendon in close proximity to anterior arch of C1

## Imaging Findings

### General

- prevertebral / retropharyngeal soft-tissue swelling
- amorphous calcification anterior to C1-C2 (CT most sensitive)
- fluid originating in prevertebral space ± extension to retropharyngeal space:
- retropharyngeal space smoothly expanded in all directions from C1 tapering inferiorly to
- effusions may involve both lateral atlantoaxial joints
- no rimlike enhancement

## Clinical

- **Rx:** antiinflammatory medication
