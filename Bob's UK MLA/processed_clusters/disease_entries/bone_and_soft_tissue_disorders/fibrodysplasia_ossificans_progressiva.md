# Disease: FIBRODYSPLASIA OSSIFICANS PROGRESSIVA

**Source section:** BONE AND SOFT-TISSUE DISORDERS

## Definition

MYOSITIS OSSIFICANS PROGRESSIVA (misnomer since primarily connective tissues are rare slowly progressive sporadic / autosomal dominant disease with variable penetrance usually only one large phalanx present / synostosis of metacarpal + proximal phalanx

## Key Facts

- **Histo:** edema with proliferating fibroblasts in a loose myxoid matrix; subsequent collagen
- **Age:** presenting by age 2 years (50%)

## Imaging Findings

### General

- rounded / linear calcification in neck / shoulders, para-vertebral region, hips, proximal
- ossification of voluntary muscles, complete by 20-25 years (sparing of sphincters + head)
- microdactyly of big toes (90%) and thumbs (50%)
- phalangeal shortening of hand + foot (middle phalanx of 5th digit)
- shortened 1st metatarsal + hallux valgus (75%)
- shortened metacarpals + metatarsals
- shallow acetabulum
- short widened femoral neck
- thickening of medial cortex of tibia
- progressive fusion of posterior arches of cervical spine
- narrowed AP diameter of cervical + lumbar vertebral bodies
- ± bony ankylosis
