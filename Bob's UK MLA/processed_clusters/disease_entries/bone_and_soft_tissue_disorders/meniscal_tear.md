# Disease: MENISCAL TEAR

**Source section:** BONE AND SOFT-TISSUE DISORDERS

## Definition

synovial fluid accumulation in degenerated tissue peripheral margin of meniscus extends ≥ 3 mm beyond edge of tibial plateau superficial edema adjacent to meniscal attachment site paralleling articular surface < CLEAVAGE TEAR tear oriented parallel to tibial plateau tear oriented parallel to long axis / outer margin of meniscus + perpendicular to tibial TRANSVERSE TEAR tear perpendicular to tibial plateau + long axis / free edge of meniscus → disruption of radial-type tear combination of radial, horizontal, longitudinal components → frequent fragmentation of free edge tear with vertical + horizontal component composite of radial tear that curves into longitudinal tear longitudinal vertical tear with attached unstable central migration of inner "handle" surface irregularity along meniscal free edge without discrete tear Peripheral Tear tearing of peripheral attachments of meniscus

## Key Facts

- **Prevalence:** increases with age
- **Associated with:** ligamentous injury
- **Location:** intrameniscal, meniscocapsular margin, parameniscal
- **Incidence:** increased if ACL tear present
- **Frequency:** most common type of tear
- **Age:** frequently in young individuals
