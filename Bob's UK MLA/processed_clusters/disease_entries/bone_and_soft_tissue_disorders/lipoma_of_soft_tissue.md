# Disease: LIPOMA OF SOFT TISSUE

**Source section:** BONE AND SOFT-TISSUE DISORDERS

## Definition

lesion composed of fat separated by small branching vessels long-standing lipoma with chondroid + osseous metaplasia INTRAMUSCULAR LIPOMA relatively common benign lipomatous tumor extending between muscle fibers that become DIFFUSE SYNOVIAL LIPOMA rare idiopathic intraarticular lesion characterized by replacement of subsynovial tissue by FIBROLIPOMATOUS HAMARTOMA OF NERVE rare tumorlike condition characterized by sausage-shaped / fusiform enlargement of a nerve

## Imaging Findings

### General

- well-defined encapsulated mass of fat opacity / density / intensity identical to subcutaneous
- no enhancement
- cortical thickening (with adjacent parosteal lipoma)

### CT

- well-defined + homogeneous tumor with low attenuation coefficient (-65 to -120 HU)
- no enhancement following IV contrast material

### MR

- well-defined + homogeneous, often with septations
- SI characteristics similar to subcutaneous fat: hyperintense on T1WI + moderately intense
- thin septations of low SI (not uncommon) correspond to fibrous connective tissue
- differentiation from other lesions by fat suppression technique
- septa > 2 mm thick + septal nodularity
- soft tissue component with attenuation / SI of muscle / fibrous tissue / hemorrhage /
- signal characteristics of fat + mixed with varying numbers of large / small vessels
- mostly encapsulated lesion, may infiltrate
- frondlike synovial mass isointense to fat on all sequences (PATHOGNOMONIC)
- joint effusion
- lack of magnetic susceptibility artifact
- may not be visible radiographically
- longitudinally oriented, cylindrical, linear / serpiginous structures of signal void about 3

### XRAY

- joint fullness with radiolucent areas
- (frequently) osteoarthritic changes

### US

- hyperechoic frondlike mass bending and waving in real time with joint manipulation
- "cablelike appearance" = alternating hyper- and hypoechoic bands on US

## Clinical

- **Rx:** resection with < 5% recurrence rate
