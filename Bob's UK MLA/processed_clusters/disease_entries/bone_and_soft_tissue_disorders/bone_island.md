# Disease: BONE ISLAND

**Source section:** BONE AND SOFT-TISSUE DISORDERS

## Definition

ENOSTOSIS = ENDOSTEOMA = COMPACT ISLAND FOCAL SCLEROSIS = SCLEROTIC BONE ISLAND CALCIFIED MEDULLARY DEFECT focal lesion of densely sclerotic (compact) bone nesting within spongiosa

## Key Facts

- **Age:** any age (mostly 20-80 years of age); grows more rapidly in children
- **Histo:** nest of lamellar compacted bone with haversian system embedded within medullary
- **Size:** usually 2-10 mm; lesion > 2 cm in longest axis = giant bone island

## Imaging Findings

### General

- round / oval / oblong solitary osteoblastic lesion with abrupt transition to surrounding normal
- "brush border" = "thorny radiations" = sharply demarcated margins with feathery
- long axis of bone island parallels long axis of bone
- may demonstrate slow growth / decrease in size (32%)
- NO involvement of cortex / radiolucencies / periosteal reaction

### MR

- low signal intensity at T1WI and T2WI

### NUC

- may show activity on bone scan (< 10%), esp. if large

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** may increase to 8-12 cm over years (40%); may decrease / disappear
