# Disease: NONOSSIFYING FIBROMA

**Source section:** NEUROPATHIC OSTEOARTHROPATHY

## Definition

FIBROXANTHOMA = NONOSTEOGENIC FIBROMA = XANTHOMA = nonossifying fibroma with extraskeletal manifestations in children

## Key Facts

- **Frequency:** up to 40% of all children > 2 years of age
- **Etiology:** lesion resulting from proliferative activity of a fibrous cortical defect that has
- **Histo:** interlacing whorled bundles of spindle-shaped fibroblasts + scattered multinucleated
- **Age:** 8-20 years; 75% in 2nd decade of life
- **Associated with:** neurofibromatosis, fibrous dysplasia, Jaffé-Campanacci syndrome

## Imaging Findings

### General

- well-circumscribed multiloculated bubbly oval osteolytic area
- alignment along long axis of bone, > 2 cm in length
- scalloped sclerotic margin toward medulla; V- or U-shaped at one end
- mild expansile remodeling = endosteal scalloping + thinning ± overlying bulge
- no periosteal reaction
- migrates toward center of diaphysis
- resolves with age

### NUC

- minimal / mild uptake on bone scan

### MR

- 80% hypointense on T1WI + T2WI ← extensive hypocellular fibrous tissue, hemosiderin
- 20% hypointense on T1WI + hyperintense on T2WI ← massive aggregation of foamy
- peripheral hypointense rim + internal septation ← marginal reactive sclerosis +
- adjacent marrow edema generally absent
- intense contrast enhancement (in 80%) / marginal septal enhancement (in 20%) on T1WI

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** spontaneous healing in most cases
