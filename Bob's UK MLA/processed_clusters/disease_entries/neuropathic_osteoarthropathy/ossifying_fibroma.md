# Disease: OSSIFYING FIBROMA

**Source section:** NEUROPATHIC OSTEOARTHROPATHY

## Definition

encapsulated circumscribed benign neoplasm closely related to fibrous dysplasia + PSAMMOMATOID OSSIFYING FIBROMA nonmetastasizing benign tumor that arises in sinonasal region of young patients often

## Key Facts

- **Incidence:** peaks in 3rd-4th decade
- **Age:** 2nd-4th decade; M < F
- **Histo:** highly cellular fibrous connective tissue composed of spindle cells with osteoblastic
- **Location:** frequently in face / posterior mandible

## Imaging Findings

### General

- intense focal uptake on bone scan

### RADIOGRAPHY

- monostotic round / ovoid well-demarcated expansile lesion of mixed lytic + sclerotic
- ballooned / bowed appearance of sclerotic margin

### CT

- predominantly soft-tissue attenuation with multiple foci of calcifications
- ± multiloculated internal composition with sclerotic septa / enhancing septa of soft-tissue
- lower-attenuation areas ← cystic changes
- usually surrounded by a possibly partially disrupted sclerotic rim / shell
- enhancement of shell + solid tumor portions

### MR

- lesion isointense relative to muscle on T1WI
- hypointense relative to muscle on T2WI
- foci of high T2-signal intensity ← fluid-filled cystic spaces

### Tibia

- eccentric ground-glass lesion (resembling fibrous dysplasia)
- initially lucent (= nonmineralized osteoid) + later often opaque lesion (depending on
- surrounded by thin line of lucency (= fibrous capsule) + thin outer rim of sclerotic
- ± soft-tissue enhancement

### Mandible, maxilla

- 1-5 cm well-circumscribed round / oval tumor
- moderate unilocular expansion of intact cortex
- homogeneous tumor matrix
- dislodgment of teeth
- NO halo of low attenuation; sclerotic border (occasionally)
- intracranial extension

## Clinical

- **Cx:** significant potential for centrifugal growth perpendicular to long axis of bone; frequent
