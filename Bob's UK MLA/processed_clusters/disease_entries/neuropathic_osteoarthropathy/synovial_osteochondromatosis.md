# Disease: SYNOVIAL OSTEOCHONDROMATOSIS

**Source section:** NEUROPATHIC OSTEOARTHROPATHY

## Definition

SYNOVIAL CHONDROMATOSIS = JOINT CHONDROMA benign self-limiting monoarticular disorder characterized by proliferation + metaplastic joint surface disintegration

## Key Facts

- **Histo:** foci of hyaline cartilage with mineralized chondroid matrix beneath synovial surface
- **Age:** 3rd-5th decade; M÷F = 2÷1 - 4÷1

## Imaging Findings

### General

- pathognomonic multiple calcified / ossified loose bodies in a single joint (bony shell of
- varying degrees of bone mineralization (25-30% of chondromas show no radiopacity)
- characteristically uniform size of nodules that may vary between a few mm and several cm
- marginal pressure erosion of adjacent bone in joints with tight capsule (eg, hip)
- widening of joint space (from accumulation of loose bodies)
- joint effusion uncommon
- NO osteoporosis

### CT

- multiple calcified / ossified intraarticular bodies
- intraarticular soft-tissue mass of near-water attenuation containing multiple small

### MR

- homogeneous lobulated intraarticular mass isointense to muscle on T1WI + hyperintense
- osteochondral bodies:
- multiple foci of low signal intensity ← calcifications
- peripheral contrast enhancement of chondral lesions
- intraarticular bodies with central area of high SI on T1WI = with fatty marrow
- intraarticular bodies tend to be larger, less numerous, more varied in size compared to
- prominent osteoarthritis

## Clinical

- **Rx:** surgical synovectomy with removal of loose bodies (recurrence is common)
