# Disease: OSTEOFIBROUS DYSPLASIA

**Source section:** NEUROPATHIC OSTEOARTHROPATHY

## Definition

entity previously mistaken for fibrous dysplasia

## Key Facts

- **Age:** newborn up to 5 years
- **Histo:** fibrous tissue surrounding trabeculae in a whorled storiform pattern
- **Location:** normally confined to tibia (middiaphysis in 50%), lesion begins in anterior cortex;

## Imaging Findings

### General

- enlargement of tibia with anterior bowing
- cortex thin / invisible
- periosteal expansion
- sclerotic margin (DDx: nonosteogenic fibroma, chondromyxoid fibroma)
- spontaneous regression in ⅓

## Clinical

- **Cx:** pathologic fracture in 25%, fractures will heal with immobilization; infrequently
