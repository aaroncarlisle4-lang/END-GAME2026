# Disease: OSTEOCHONDROSIS DISSECANS

**Source section:** NEUROPATHIC OSTEOARTHROPATHY

## Definition

OSTEOCHONDRITIS DISSECANS = OSTEOCHONDRAL FRACTURE fragmentation + possible separation of a portion of the articular surface disease manifestation before physeal closure

## Key Facts

- **Age:** adolescence; M > F
- **Location:** posterior region of knee joint, olecranon fossa, axillary / subscapular recess of
- **Etiology:** repetitive trauma

## Imaging Findings

### General

- purely cartilaginous fragment unrecognized on plain film
- fracture line parallels joint surface
- mouse = osteochondrotic fragment
- mouse bed = sclerosed pit in articular surface
- soft-tissue swelling, joint effusion

### MR

- focus of abnormal signal in subarticular marrow
- defect in overlying cartilage
- loose bodies of heterogeneous low-SI in coronoid and olecranon fossa outlined by
- signs of lesion instability (= high-grade lesion):
- line of fluid signal intensity on T2WI between fragment + parent bone surrounded by
- multiple / > 5 mm cysts near lesion
- fracture in articular cartilage
- fluid-filled osteochondritis dissecans lesion
- lucent defect within capitellum
- flattening of anterior border of capitellum
