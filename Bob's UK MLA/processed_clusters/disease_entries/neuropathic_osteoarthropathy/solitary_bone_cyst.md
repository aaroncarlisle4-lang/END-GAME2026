# Disease: SOLITARY BONE CYST

**Source section:** NEUROPATHIC OSTEOARTHROPATHY

## Definition

UNICAMERAL / SIMPLE BONE CYST

## Key Facts

- **Frequency:** up to 5% of primary bone lesions
- **Etiology:** ? trauma (synovial entrapment at capsular reflection), ? vascular anomaly (blockage
- **Histo:** cyst filled with clear yellowish fluid often under pressure, wall lined with fibrous
- **Age:** 3-19 years (80%); occurs during active phase of bone growth; M÷F = 3÷1

## Imaging Findings

### General

- 2-3 cm oval radiolucency with long axis parallel to long axis of host bone
- fine sclerotic boundary
- scalloping + erosion of internal aspect of underlying cortex
- photopenic area on bone scan (if not fractured)
- "fallen fragment" sign if fractured (20%) = centrally dislodged fragment falls into a dependent

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** mostly spontaneous regression
- **Cx:** pathologic fracture (65%)
