# Disease: SUBUNGUAL EXOSTOSIS

**Source section:** NEUROPATHIC OSTEOARTHROPATHY

## Definition

DUPUYTREN EXOSTOSIS uncommon solitary benign bone lesion arising from distal phalanx beneath the nail producing

## Key Facts

- **Age:** 2nd-3rd decade (range, 7-58 years); M÷F = 1÷2
- **Histo:** inflammatory growth of proliferating fibroblasts and cartilage undergoing enchondral
- **Location:** toes (86-90%, big toe in 77-80%), thumb + index finger (10-14%, dominant hand in

## Imaging Findings

### General

- radiographic appearance similar to osteochondroma:
- broad sessile / narrow pedunculated base of bone spur
- indistinct / well-demarcated cartilage cap larger than base
- NO continuity to cortex / medulla of host bone (DDx to osteochondroma)
- ossific mass with trabecular bony overgrowth distal to physeal scar: ± defined cortex

### US

- heterogeneously hyperechoic lesion
- well-defined margins + calcifications
- hypoechoic fibrocartilaginous cap of < 20 mm in diameter
- no / mild vascularity

### MR

- subungual exostosis hypointense on all sequences

## Clinical

- **Rx:** complete surgical excision
- **Prognosis:** 11-53% recurrence rate
