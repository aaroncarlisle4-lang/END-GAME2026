# Disease: TRANSIENT REGIONAL OSTEOPOROSIS

**Source section:** NEUROPATHIC OSTEOARTHROPATHY

## Definition

TRANSIENT BONE MARROW EDEMA rapid onset of self-limiting episodes of severe localized osteoporosis and pain but variant of regional migratory osteoporosis with more focal pattern of osteoporosis, which self-limiting disease of unknown etiology

## Key Facts

- **Age:** middle-aged males
- **Location:** usually lower extremity (ie, ankle, knee, hip, foot)

## Imaging Findings

### General

- rapid localized osteoporosis within 4-8 weeks after onset migrating from one joint to
- linear / wavy periosteal reaction
- preservation of subchondral cortical bone
- no joint space narrowing / bone erosion

### MR

- affected area has low SI on T1WI, high SI on T2WI (= bone marrow edema)
- diffuse bone marrow edema involving femoral head + neck + sometimes intertrochanteric
- small joint effusion

### NUC

- increased activity
- progressive marked osteoporosis of femoral head, neck, acetabulum (3-8 weeks after onset
- virtually PATHOGNOMONIC striking loss of subchondral cortex of femoral head + neck
- NO joint space narrowing / subchondral bone collapse
- markedly increased uptake on bone scan without cold spots / inhomogeneities (positive

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** persists for 6-9 months in one area; cycle of symptoms may last for several years
- **Rx:** variable response to analgesics / corticosteroids
- **Cx:** pathologic fracture common
