**Source section:** ERDHEIM-CHESTER DISEASE

## Key Facts

- **Associated with:** minor trauma, surgery, malignancy, occult infection (Epstein-Barr virus,
- **Prevalence:** < 1% of all tumors of lung + airways
- **Path:** nonencapsulated circumscribed nodular mass, occasionally with infiltrative margins
- **Histo:** mixture of inflammatory cells (plasma cells, lymphocytes, eosinophils, neutrophils) +
- **Age:** young patient
- **Location:** described in all major organs

## Imaging Findings

### US

- solid process with variable echogenicity ± Doppler flow

### CT

- variable heterogeneous attenuation ± central calcification
- no / heterogeneous / peripheral enhancement

### MR

- hypointense lesion ← fibrotic component
- NO restricted diffusion

### Abdomen: liver (2nd most common), spleen, pancreas, adrenal gland, kidney,

- well-defined smoothly marginated / infiltrating mass

## Clinical

- **Rx:** surgical excision
