# Disease: NEUROENDOCRINE TUMOR OF LUNG

**Source section:** ERDHEIM-CHESTER DISEASE

## Definition

tumors arising from Kulchitsky cells (normally present in bronchial mucosa) NEUROENDOCRINE CARCINOMA / NEOPLASM poorly differentiated high-grade neuroendocrine tumor as a variant of large-cell carcinoma

## Key Facts

- **Incidence:** 25% of all pulmonary neoplasms
- **Histo:** neuroendocrine morphology = organoid nesting, palisading, rosettes, trabecular growth
- **Frequency:** 1-2% of all pulmonary neoplasms; 20-30% of all carcinoid tumors; 20-25% of
- **Mean age:** 46 (range, 4-95) years; 4% occur in children + adolescents; M÷F = 1÷1; very
- **Associated with:** MEN 1 in < 4% (almost all hormonally inactive)
- **Prevalence:** 3% of all resected lung cancers; 19% of pulmonary endocrine tumors; 12% of
- **Location:** peripheral (70-80%) / central (20-30%)

## Imaging Findings

### CXR

- well-defined central hilar / perihilar mass:
- ± consolidation ← atelectasis / postobstructive pneumonitis / recurrent pneumonia
- ± mucus plugging = "gloved finger" appearance
- solitary well-defined round / ovoid peripheral nodule / mass distal to segmental
- ± lobulated borders < 3 cm in size
- 3.7-cm peripheral pulmonary mass

### CT

- spherical / ovoid polypoid nodule / mass (with long axis parallel to adjacent
- well-defined slightly lobulated border
- "bronchus sign" (= bronchus leading directly to the tumor) may allow diagnosis
- "collar button" lesion = extension through bronchial wall involving bronchial lumen
- diffuse scattered eccentric punctate calcifications / dense ossification (26-33%):
- cavitation (rare)
- intense > 30 HU net enhancement ← vascular tumor supplied by bronchial circulation
- bronchiectasis, mucoid impaction, atelectasis, air trapping
- postobstructive pneumonia
- hilar / mediastinal lymphadenopathy (6-25%) ← metastasis / reactive hyperplasia ←
- metastases in liver, bone, adrenal glands
- nonspecific well-defined lobulated nodule / mass:
- ± air bronchogram, cavity, bubbly lucency, necrosis
- intratumoral calcifications (9%)
- homo- / heterogeneous enhancement
- attendant atelectasis / distal mucus plugging
- pleural effusion (24%)

### MR

- mass of high T2 + STIR signal intensity
- intense enhancement during systemic circulatory phase

### NUC

- typically for staging / assessing response to therapy
- helpful in detecting occult tumor (depending on degree of somatostatin-receptor
- MIBG less sensitive than octreotide
- NO (most often) / increased activity (occasionally)

### PET

- avid FDG uptake; SUV > 13.7 suggests shortened survival

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** death from valvular disease
- **Rx:** lobectomy, pneumonectomy, endobronchial resection, bronchial sleeve resection,
- **Spread:** mediastinal lymphadenopathy (27%); extrathoracic metastasis (36%)
