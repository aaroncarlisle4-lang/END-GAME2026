# Disease: PITUITARY GLAND

**Source section:** ANATOMY OF THE NERVOUS SYSTEM

## Definition

hypophysis cerebri within hypophyseal fossa of sphenoid, covered superiorly by sellar larger anterior portion of adenohypophysis comprising 80% of pituitary gland volume posterior portion of adenohypophysis, separated from anterior lobe by hypophyseal cleft in major portion of neurohypophysis termination point of neurosecretory axons from supraoptic and paraventricular nuclei of

## Key Facts

- **Size:** (adult size is achieved at puberty)
- **Histo:** formed from axons of cells lying in supraoptic + paraventricular nuclei of hypothalamus

## Imaging Findings

### General

- flat / downwardly convex superior border
- upwardly convex during puberty, pregnancy, in hypothyroidism (due to hyperplasia)

### MR

- larger homogeneous component isointense to white matter on T1WI + T2WI
- prominent contrast enhancement (during first 3 minutes) ← lack of blood-brain barrier
- hyperintense in newborn fading to normal adult signal by 2nd month of life
- not visible with imaging techniques
- crescent of T1-hyperintensity + T2-isointensity compared with anterior pituitary lobe ←
- isointense in 10% of normal individuals
- joins posterior lobe at junction of anterior + posterior lobes
- up to 3 mm thick superiorly, up to 2 mm thick inferiorly
- usually in midline, may be slightly tilted to one side
- prominent contrast enhancement
