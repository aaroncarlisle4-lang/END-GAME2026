# Disease: GLIOBLASTOMA MULTIFORME

**Source section:** CENTRAL NEUROCYTOMA

## Definition

GBM

## Key Facts

- **Incidence:** most common primary brain tumor; 50% of all intracranial tumors; 1-2% of all
- **Age:** all ages; peak incidence at 65-75 years; M÷F = 3÷2; more frequently in whites
- **Genetics:** Turcot syndrome, neurofibromatosis type 1, Li-Fraumeni syndrome (familial
- **Path:** multilobulated appearance; quite extensive vasogenic edema (transudation through
- **Histo:** highly cellular, often bizarrely pleomorphic / undifferentiated multipolar astrocytes;

## Imaging Findings

### General

- readily crosses midline = "butterfly" glioma (clue: invasion of septum pellucidum)
- frontal + temporal gliomas tend to invade basal ganglia
- may invade pia, arachnoid and dura (mimicking meningioma)
- osteoblastic bone lesion

### NECT

- inhomogeneous low-density mass with irregular shape + poorly defined margins
- considerable mass effect → compression + displacement of ventricles, cisterns, brain
- iso- / hyperdense portions (= hemorrhage) in 5%
- rarely calcifies (if coexistent with lower-grade glioma / after radio- or chemotherapy)

### CECT

- almost always ring blush of variable thickness: multiscalloped ("garland"), round / ovoid;
- sedimentation level ← cellular debris / hemorrhage / accumulated contrast material in

### MR

- poorly defined lesion with some mass effect / vasogenic edema / heterogeneity
- hemosiderin deposits (gradient echo images)
- hemorrhage (= hypointensity on T2WI and T2*WI)
- T1WI + gadolinium-DTPA enhancement separates tumor nodules from surrounding edema,

### ANGIO

- wildly irregular neovascularity + early draining veins
- avascular lesion

### PET

- increase in glucose utilization rate

## Clinical

- **Rx:** surgery + radiation therapy + chemotherapy
- **Prognosis:** 16-18 months postoperative survival (frequent tumor recurrence ← during surgery
