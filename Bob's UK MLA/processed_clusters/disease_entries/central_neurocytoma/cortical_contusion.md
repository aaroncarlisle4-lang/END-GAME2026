# Disease: CORTICAL CONTUSION

**Source section:** CENTRAL NEUROCYTOMA

## Definition

CEREBRAL CONTUSION = BRAIN CONTUSION traumatic injury to cortical surface of brain

## Key Facts

- **Incidence:** most common type of primary intraaxial lesion; in 21% of head trauma patients;
- **Path:** petechial hemorrhage (= admixture of blood with native tissue) followed by liquefaction
- **Associated with:** skull fracture
- **Location:** multiple bilateral lesions;

## Imaging Findings

### General

- "salt and pepper lesion" = mottled / speckled densities as focal / multiple (29%) poorly
- diffuse cerebral hypodensity + swelling without hemorrhage in immediate posttraumatic
- some degree of contrast enhancement ← leaking new capillaries
- hemorrhage isodense after 2-3 weeks
- true extent of lesions becomes more evident with progression of edema + cell necrosis +
- hemorrhagic lesion (detected in 50% of all contusions):
- initially decreased intensity (← deoxyhemoglobin of acute hemorrhage) surrounded by
- hyperintense on T1WI + T2WI in subacute phase ← Met-Hb
- hyperintense gliosis + hypointense hemosiderin on T2WI in chronic phase
- nonhemorrhagic lesion hypointense on T1WI + hyperintense on T2WI

## Clinical

- **Cx:** (1)  Progression to cerebral hematoma
