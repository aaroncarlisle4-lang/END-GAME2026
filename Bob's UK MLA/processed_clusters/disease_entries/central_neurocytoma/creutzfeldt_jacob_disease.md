# Disease: CREUTZFELDT-JACOB DISEASE

**Source section:** CENTRAL NEUROCYTOMA

## Definition

rare subacutely progressive neurological disorder due to transmissible infectious disease

## Key Facts

- **Age:** older adults (57-73 years)
- **Histo:** classified as spongiform encephalopathy "mad cow disease"
- **Location:** cerebral cortex, basal ganglia (caudate nucleus), thalamus

## Imaging Findings

### CT

- usually normal
- rapidly progressive atrophic changes

### MR

- hyperintense T2 lesions in basal ganglia (head of caudate nucleus + putamen), occipital
- high-SI lesions on DWI (← vacuolization of neutrophil leading to gliosis + astrocytosis and
- restricted diffusion is more sensitive than T2-FLAIR, especially for cortical lesions ←
- "pulvinar" sign / "hockey stick" sign = T2 prolongation + restricted diffusion in medial
- NO gadolinium enhancement of lesions
- NO white matter involvement

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** usually fatal within 2 year of onset of symptoms (mean, 8.8 months)
