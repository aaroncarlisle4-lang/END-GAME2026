# Disease: PELVIC CONGESTION SYNDROME

**Source section:** OBSTETRIC AND GYNECOLOGIC DISORDERS

## Definition

chronic (> 6 months) noncyclical pelvic pain associated with pelvic venous distension + obstructive uropathy ← ureteral compression at pelvic brim by dilated / aberrant /

## Key Facts

- **Prevalence:** ovarian varices in 10% in general population
- **Location:** around uterus, ovary, broad ligament, pelvic sidewall, paravaginal plexus

## Imaging Findings

### US

- multiple tortuous pelvic veins > 4-6 mm in diameter around uterus + ovaries
- slow (≤ 3 cm/s) / reversed (caudal) flow
- dilated arcuate vein in myometrium
- polycystic changes of ovaries
- dilated tortuous enhancing tubular structures
- ovarian vein diameter > 10 mm
- retrograde flow in ovarian / pelvic veins
- tortuous collateral pelvic venous pathways
- delayed / stagnant clearance of injected contrast from veins
- oblique extrinsic impression on ureter:
- proximal hydroureteronephrosis + normal pelvic ureter
- medial deviation of obstructed segment (with retrocaval ureter) = characteristic
- concurrent evaluation of cause of compression, implicated vessel + proximal dilatation

## Clinical

- **Rx:** hormone analogues (medroxyprogestrone, GnRH analog goserelin) + analgesics;
- **Cx:** spontaneous rupture with life-threatening hemorrhage (rare)
