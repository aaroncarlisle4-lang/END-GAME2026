# Disease: ENDOCERVICAL POLYP

**Source section:** OBSTETRIC AND GYNECOLOGIC DISORDERS

## Definition

most common benign cervical growth

## Key Facts

- **Incidence:** up to 4%
- **Age:** multiparous women in their 30s and 40s
- **Histo:** connective-tissue stalk with focal hyperplasia of glandular columnar epithelium ±

## Imaging Findings

### General

- typically slightly hyperechoic polyp compared with normal mucosa
- may undergo cystic change → confusion with nabothian cyst
- may be mobile at dynamic imaging with transducer pressure
- ± vascular stalk arising from endocervical mucosa (Doppler US)
- coexisting endometrial polyp (in 25%)

## Clinical

- **Rx:** management of prolapsed lesions tends to be more complicated ← more extensive blood supply
- **Cx:** malignancy / dysplasia (in 0.2-1.5%)
