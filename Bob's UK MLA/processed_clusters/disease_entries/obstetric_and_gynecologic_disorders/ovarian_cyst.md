# Disease: OVARIAN CYST

**Source section:** OBSTETRIC AND GYNECOLOGIC DISORDERS

## Definition

hemorrhage into mature corpus luteum from corpus luteum following regression of luteal tissue; no hormone production multiple bilateral corpus luteum cysts ← ovarian hyperstimulation ← abnormally high does not satisfy criteria for hemorrhagic cyst / endometrioma functional cyst (follicular cyst / corpus luteum) that developed internal hemorrhage →

## Key Facts

- **Size:** usually 3-8 cm; occasionally up to 10 cm (if cyst remains hormonally sensitive); up
- **Age:** any; in newborns (influence of maternal estrogen)
- **Prevalence:** 3-5-17% in postmenopausal women
- **Location:** uni- > bilateral; intra-abdominal > intrapelvic
- **Path:** functional cyst + corpus luteum cyst

- **Cx:** ovarian torsion
- **Prognosis:** spontaneous regression is common but unpredictable; typically resolve within 2
- **N.B.:** may occasionally contain single avascular septation
- **Rx:** follow-up in 6-12 weeks to ensure resolution
