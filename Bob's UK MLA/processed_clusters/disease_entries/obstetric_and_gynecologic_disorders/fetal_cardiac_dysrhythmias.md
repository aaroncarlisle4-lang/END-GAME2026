# Disease: FETAL CARDIAC DYSRHYTHMIAS

**Source section:** OBSTETRIC AND GYNECOLOGIC DISORDERS

## Definition

PAC = most common benign rhythm abnormality intermittent conduction with a ventricular rate as a submultiple of the atrial rate

## Key Facts

- **Prevalence:** 1÷25,000; most frequent tachyarrhythmia in children
- **Etiology:** viral infection, hypoplasia of sinoatrial tract

## Imaging Findings

### General

- transient tachycardia
- transient bradycardia ← atrial bigeminy if every other beat is nonconducted
- atrial rate of 180-300 bpm
- ventricular response of 1÷1
- atrial rate of 300-460 bpm
- ventricular rate of 60-200 bpm
- atrial rate of 400-700 bpm
- ventricular rate of 120-200 bpm
- M-mode echocardiography with simultaneous visualization of atrial + ventricular
- normal heart rate + rhythm (not reportedly diagnosed in utero)
- a few atrial contractions → NOT followed by a ventricular contraction
- atrial contraction NOT followed by a ventricular contraction in a constant
- slow atrial + ventricular contractions independent from each other

## Clinical

- **Cx:** supraventricular tachycardia (unusual)
- **Rx:** discontinue smoking, alcohol, caffeine
