# Disease: LIMB-BODY WALL COMPLEX

**Source section:** OBSTETRIC AND GYNECOLOGIC DISORDERS

## Definition

BODY STALK ANOMALY characterized by attachment of visceral organs to placenta with short / absent umbilical cord

## Key Facts

- **Prevalence:** 0.12÷10,000 live and still births
- **Location:** L÷R = 3÷1 (DDx: gastroschisis)

## Imaging Findings

### General

- large eccentric defect
- fetus usually in a fixed position
- cord insertion site not identifiable
- NO free-floating loops of cord
- severe kyphoscoliosis (frequent)
- ± oligohydramnios → MRI
- complete evisceration of abdominal contents with adherence to placental surface
- persistence of extraembryonic coelom (= separation of amnion + chorion)

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** invariably fatal shortly after birth
