# Disease: GRANULOSA CELL TUMOR

**Source section:** OBSTETRIC AND GYNECOLOGIC DISORDERS

## Key Facts

- **Frequency:** 2-3% of all ovarian neoplasms
- **Age:** puberty (5%), reproductive age (45%), postmenopausal (50%)
- **Location:** unilateral in 95%; bilateral in 2-5%
- **Size:** average size of 12.5 cm (up to 40 cm) in diameter
- **Histo:** macrofollicular (multiple cysts resembling follicles), microfollicular (with Call-
- **Mean age:** 13 years; 80% of GCT occur in first 2 decades; only in 3% in women > 30 years
- **Path:** solid component of granulosa + theca cells; follicles lined by granulosa cells staining
- **Associated with:** Ollier disease, Maffucci syndrome

## Imaging Findings

### US

- large complex multilocular cystic mass with fluid / blood (most frequently) + solid
- thick irregular septations but NO intracystic papillary projections
- solid component isoechoic to myometrium with Doppler flow
- predominantly solid small heterogeneous hypoechoic mass simulating fibroid (uncommon)
- calcifications (rare)

### CT

- iso- to hyperattenuating enhancing solid component
- hyperattenuating hemorrhage within cystic component

### MR

- intermediate SI of solid component + multiple cystic spaces on T2WI = "spongelike"
- hyperintense on T1WI ← hemorrhage within tumor cysts
- enhancement of solid component
- ascites
- uterine enlargement
- endometrial thickening / hemorrhage
- endometrial polyps / hyperplasia (80%) / carcinoma (10%)
- local extension
- peritoneum-based metastases similar to epithelial neoplasm (rare)
- cystic liver metastases

## Clinical

- **N.B.:** Androgenic effect with virilization extremely rare
- **Prognosis:** low malignant potential; late recurrence following surgery after > 3 decades
- **Rx:** uni- / bilateral salpingo-oophorectomy ± postoperative chemotherapy
- **Cx:** malignant degeneration (rare)
