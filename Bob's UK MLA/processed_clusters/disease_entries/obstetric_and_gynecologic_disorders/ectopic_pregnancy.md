# Disease: ECTOPIC PREGNANCY

**Source section:** OBSTETRIC AND GYNECOLOGIC DISORDERS

## Definition

implantation of blastocyst outside the endometrial cavity ectopic pregnancy in an eccentric location relative to endometrium + close to uterine serosa ovum fertilized + retained within ovary implantation within peritoneal cavity excluding tubal + ovarian + intraligamentous "stone child" = very rare obstetric complication consisting of a dehydrated + calcified implantation of blastocyst within the endocervical canal and subsequently fibrous wall of implantation within a scar of a prior cesarean section in the anterior lower uterine segment implantation of blastocyst within one horn (= cornua) of a bicornuate / septate uterus simultaneous occurrence of intra- + extrauterine pregnancy gestational sac completely surrounded by myometrium implantation within lateral angle of uterus medial to uterotubal junction

## Key Facts

- **Incidence:** 2% of all pregnancies in USA (1992); higher for in vitro fertilization; 73,700 cases
- **Location:** bilateral (0.1%)
- **Frequency:** 1÷6,000 ectopic pregnancies
- **Path:** blastocyst surrounded by myometrium + fibrous tissue
- **Prevalence:** 1÷21,000-30,000 pregnancies; 1-3% in patients undergoing ovulation induction
