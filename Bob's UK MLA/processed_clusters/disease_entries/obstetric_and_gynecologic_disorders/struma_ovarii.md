# Disease: STRUMA OVARII

**Source section:** OBSTETRIC AND GYNECOLOGIC DISORDERS

## Definition

rare highly specialized form of ovarian teratoma

## Key Facts

- **Prevalence:** 0.30-0.65% of ovarian tumors; 5-20% of all mature cystic ovarian teratomas
- **Age:** premenopausal women
- **Path:** unilateral; often < 10 cm in size
- **Histo:** mature thyroid tissue of variably sized acini filled with colloid: lined by single layer of

## Imaging Findings

### General

- multilocular cystic mass with smooth margins + large cystic spaces = dilated thyroid follicles

### US

- nonspecific solid + cystic tumor
- characteristic struma pearls = well circumscribed round vascularized echogenic solid

### CT

- high-attenuation loculi = iodinated thyroid hormone and calcifications
- intense enhancement
- ± loculi of fat attenuation = sebaceous material

## Clinical

- **Prognosis:** benign in 95%
- **Cx:** malignancy (in 5-10%)
