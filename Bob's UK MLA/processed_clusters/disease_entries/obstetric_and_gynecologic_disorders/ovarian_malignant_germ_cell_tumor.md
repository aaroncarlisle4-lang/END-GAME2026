# Disease: OVARIAN MALIGNANT GERM CELL TUMOR

**Source section:** OBSTETRIC AND GYNECOLOGIC DISORDERS

## Definition

OMGCT = heterogeneous group of tumors sharing the primitive germ cell of the embryonic malignant germ cell tumor of ovary homologous to testicular seminoma EMBRYONAL TERATOMA = MALIGNANT TERATOMA SOLID TERATOMA enlarging teratoma of mature elements arising during / after chemotherapy for treatment composed of > 1 germ cell element (mainly dysgerminoma, teratoma, and yolk sac tumor) usually component of mixed OMGCT (immature teratoma + yolk sac tumor), extremely rare ENDODERMAL SINUS TUMOR OF OVARY

## Key Facts

- **Incidence:** 2.6% of all malignant ovarian neoplasms
- **Mean age:** highest incidence at age 14 (range, 15-19) years
- **Frequency:** 1-2% of all primary ovarian neoplasms; 35% of all ovarian malignant germ cell
- **Path:** solid well encapsulated lobulated tumor ± coagulative necrosis / hemorrhage
- **Histo:** uniform population of round cells with clear to eosinophilic cytoplasm + central large
- **Peak age:** 2nd-3rd decade (range, 7 months - 70 years)
- **Location:** usually unilateral; bilateral in 7-10%
- **Age:** 2nd-3rd decade; rare in women > 40 years

## Imaging Findings

### General

- unilateral, rarely bilateral
- calcifications (40%)
- homogeneously solid (3%), predominantly solid (85%), predominantly cystic (12%)
- multilobulated well-defined solid mass divided by fibrovascular enhancing septa
- ± areas of hemorrhage and necrosis
- speckled pattern of calcifications (rare)

### MR

- mass of low signal intensity relative to muscle on T1WI
- isointense / slightly hyperintense mass on T2WI
- hypo- / isointense septa on T2WI with contrast enhancement on T1WI
- wide variety of signal intensities on T2WI
- ± fatty fluid within cystic compartments
- solid mass + areas of cystic change ← hemorrhage / necrosis / cystic lesion with solid
- abnormal large signal voids on T2WI ← vascular structures + small cystic cavities
- high-signal-intensity foci in solid portions on T1WI ← hemorrhage
- substantial uptake of contrast material in solid portions ← highly vascularized tumor
- large unilateral mass
- unilateral smoothly contoured enhancing mixed solid + cystic mass
- "bright dot" sign = enhancing foci in wall / solid component of tumor (common) ← dilated
- cystic areas ← epithelial-lined cysts / cysts of coexisting mature teratoma / hemorrhage /
- capsular tears
- foci of high signal intensity on T1WI ← hemorrhage

### US

- heterogeneous hyperechoic solid mass
- prominent arterial color Doppler flow within septa
- predominantly solid tumor with numerous cysts of varying size + intratumoral fat
- scattered small calcifications ← invariable association with mature teratoma
- heterogeneous solid mass + scattered small calcifications
- areas of increased echogenicity = larger fat components
- predominantly solid mass + fatty elements
- numerous cysts of variable sizes
- coarse irregular calcifications
- well-defined adnexal mass of mixed echogenicity
- NO uterine / ectopic pregnancy
- predominantly heterogeneous echogenic solid tumor
- cystic spaces divided by septa

### CT

- enhancing highly vascular solid mass

## Clinical

- **Spread:** most commonly lymphatic
- **Rx:** highly radiosensitive
- **Prognosis:** responsible for 30% of deaths from ovarian cancer in women < 20 years
