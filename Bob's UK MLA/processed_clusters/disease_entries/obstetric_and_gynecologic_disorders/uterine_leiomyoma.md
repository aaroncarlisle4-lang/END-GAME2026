# Disease: UTERINE LEIOMYOMA

**Source section:** OBSTETRIC AND GYNECOLOGIC DISORDERS

## Definition

FIBROID = MYOMA benign overgrowth of smooth muscle + connective tissue homogeneous eosinophilic bands / plaques of proteinaceous material in gelatinous intratumoral foci of hyaluronic acid-rich mucopolysaccharides massive hemorrhagic infarction ← venous thrombosis / rupture of intratumoral uncommon benign uterine neoplasm with substantial amount of fat due to fatty smooth muscle tumors in lung, lymph nodes, abdomen

## Key Facts

- **Prevalence:** in 20-25% of White women; in 50% of Black women; Black÷White women = 3÷1
- **Path:** whorl-like trabeculated mesenchymal tumor surrounded by pseudocapsule; may outgrow
- **Histo:** monoclonal proliferation of smooth muscle cells (NOT myometrial hyperplasia)
- **Age:** usually > 30 years; rare in girls < 18 years + in postmenopausal women
- **Associated with:** leiomyomas
- **Location:** corpus; predominantly intramurally, endophytic or exophytic
