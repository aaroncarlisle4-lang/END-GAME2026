# Finding: GALLBLADDER

**Source section:** Size of Liver
**Quality score:** 0.7
**Differential count:** 19

## Differentials

- Wall thickness:   2-3 mm
- Width:   2.0-3.5 cm
- Capacity:   30-50 mL
- Bile volume:   250-1,000 mL/d secreted by hepatocytes
- Gallbladder Function
- HIDA scintigraphy
- MR cholangiography with mangafodipir trisodium = MnDPDP = manganese (II) DPDP [N,N′-dipyridoxylethylenediamine-N,N′-diacetate 5,5′-bis(phosphate)]
- MR cholangiography with gadolinium BenzylOxyPropionicTetraAcetate (Gd-BOPTA) =
- ↑ sensitivity of study for acute (acalculous) cholecystitis
- in patients with abdominal pain and no cholelithiasis
- to establish that gallstones are the primary cause of abdominal pain
- Discontinue opioids for > 24 hours
- Select ROI over GB
- Administer Sincalide 1 hour post HIDA in a dose of 0.02 µg/kg body weight IV over 30 minutes (with infusion pump)
- Image acquisition for 20 more minutes post infusion to detect paradoxical filling of gallbladder in sphincter of Oddi dysfunction
- LONGITUDINAL SEPTA
- Bilobed gallbladder = duplication of gallbladder =  two separate lumina + two cystic ducts
- Bifid gallbladder = double gallbladder =  two separate lumina with one cystic duct
- Triple gallbladder (extremely rare)

## Context

Size & Capacity & Wall Thickness Physiologic distension:   optimal after 8-12 hours of fasting GB function:   concentration of bile through absorption of 90% of water Wall layers: (a)   mucosa (b)   lamina propria (c)   muscularis propria (d)   serosa N.B.:   The GB has NO muscularis mucosa / submucosa Length: (a)  infant < 1 year old:  1.5-3.0 cm (b)  older child:  3-7 cm (c)  adult:  7-10 cm =  evaluation of volume and ejection fraction following ingestion of a fatty meal / infusion of cholecystokinin Methods: Dose:  0.5 mL/kg of MnDPDP up to 35-50 mL »  slow IV injection for 1-2 minutes »
