# Finding: Upper & Midesophageal Stricture

**Source section:** B. RADIATION INJURY
**Quality score:** 0.4
**Differential count:** 9

## Differentials

- Barrett esophagus
- Mediastinal radiation therapy
- Caustic esophagitis
- Drug-induced stricture
- Esophageal intramural pseudodiverticulosis
- Dermatologic disorder: benign mucous membrane pemphigoid, epidermolysis bullosa dystrophica, erythema multiforme major
- Graft-versus-host disease
- Congenital esophageal stenosis =  tracheobronchial remnant / cartilaginous ring in esophageal wall
- Metastatic cancer (from subcarinal nodes / left mainstem bronchus)

## Context

In the setting of hiatal hernia and gastroesophageal reflux, a focal stricture in the midesophagus is highly suggestive of Barrett esophagus. DDx:  Primary carcinoma: squamous cell carcinoma
