# Finding: Pleural Effusion & Lobar Densities

**Source section:** Spontaneous Pneumothorax
**Quality score:** 0.7
**Differential count:** 9

## Differentials

- Pneumonia with empyema
- Pulmonary embolism
- Neoplasm: bronchogenic carcinoma > lymphoma
- Tuberculosis
- massive hemothorax = blood exceeding 1 L + clinical signs of shock and hypoperfusion
- TRAUMA (a)  thoracic injury
- Closed / penetrating injury to lung, chest wall, heart, great vessels
- Surgery
- Interventional procedures: thoracentesis, pleural biopsy, catheter placement (b)  abdominal injury with diaphragmatic rupture: trauma to liver / spleen

## Context

Hemothorax =  blood in pleural space √  rapidly enlarging pleural effusion of 35-70 HU attenuation √  heterogeneous attenuation √  hyperattenuating areas of debris √  fluid-hematocrit level
