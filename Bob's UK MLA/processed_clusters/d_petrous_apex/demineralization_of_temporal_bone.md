# Finding: Demineralization of Temporal Bone

**Source section:** D. PETROUS APEX
**Quality score:** 0.3
**Differential count:** 6

## Differentials

- Otosclerosis = otospongiosis
- Paget disease = osteoporosis circumscripta
  - sensorineural / mixed hearing loss (cochlear involvement / stapes fixation in oval window)
- Fibrous dysplasia monostotic with temporal bone involvement Cause:   inability to form mature lamellar bone owing to disordered osteoblastic activity
  - painless mastoid swelling
  - conductive hearing loss ← narrowing of EAC / middle ear
- Osteogenesis imperfecta
- Otosyphilis: labyrinthitis + gummatous lesion of internal auditory canal + inflammatory
- Metastasis

## Context

√  usually lytic changes beginning in petrous pyramid + progressing laterally; otic capsule last to be affected √  calvarial changes ± basilar impression √  homogeneously dense thickened structurally weak bone (fibro-osseous tissue less dense than calvarial bone) √  bone expansion with preserved cortex → osseous narrowing of vascular channels + neuroforamina √  lytic lesions (less frequent) √  sparing of membranous labyrinth, facial nerve canal, IAC is the rule √  changes similar to otosclerosis van der Hoeve-de Kleyn syndrome =  osteogenesis imperfecta + hearing loss + blue sclerae in patient
