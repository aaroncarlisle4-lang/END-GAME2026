# Finding: FRACTURE

**Source section:** FLORID REACTIVE PERIOSTITIS
**Quality score:** 1.0
**Differential count:** 13

## Differentials

- OPEN / CLOSED open Fx =  communication between fractured bone + skin
- COMPLETE / INCOMPLETE complete Fx =  all cortical surfaces disrupted incomplete Fx =  partial separation of bone
- SIMPLE / COMMINUTED simple Fx =  noncomminuted comminuted Fx =  > 2 fragments segmental Fx =  isolated segment of shaft butterfly fragment = V-shaped fragment not completely circumscribed by cortex
- DIRECTION OF FRACTURE LINE relative to long axis of bone:
- Acute phase (3-4 weeks) abnormal in 80% < 24 hours, in 95% < 72 hours ◊  Elderly patients show delayed appearance of positive scan
- Subacute phase (2-3 months) = time of most intense tracer accumulation
- Chronic phase (1-2 years)
- Simple fractures: 90% normal by 2 years
- Open reduction / fixation: < 50% normal by 3 years
- Delayed union: slower than normal for type of fracture
- Nonunion: persistent intense uptake in 80%
- Complicated union (true pseudarthrosis, soft-tissue interposition, impaired blood supply, presence of infection)
- Vertebral compression fractures: 60% normal by 1 year; 90% by 2 years; 97% by 3 years

## Context

=  soft-tissue injury with break in continuity of bone or cartilage General description: Incomplete pediatric fractures: (a)  longitudinal compressive force: buckle / torus Fx bowing Fx =  plastic deformity of thin long bone (ulna > clavicle, fibula) (b)  force perpendicular to long axis of bone greenstick Fx (c)  combination fracture lead-pipe Fx =  combination of greenstick + torus Fx transverse, oblique, oblique-transverse, spiral Special terminology: avulsion Fx = fragment pulled off by tendon / joint capsule / ligament from parent bone transchondral Fx = cartilaginous surface involved cho
