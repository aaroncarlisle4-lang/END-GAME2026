# Finding: Gangrene of Gallbladder = Gangrenous Cholecystitis

**Source section:** CHOLANGITIS
**Quality score:** 1.0
**Differential count:** 19

## Differentials

- positive Murphy sign (33%)
- Emergency operation
- Antibiotic treatment + elective operation
- Percutaneous abscess drainage
- Acute free perforation with peritonitis → pericholecystic abscess in 33%
- Subacute localized perforation → pericholecystic abscess in 48%
- Chronic perforation → internal biliary fistula → pericholecystic abscess in 18%
- Debilitated patients: depressed motility / starvation in trauma, burns, surgery, total parenteral nutrition, anesthesia, positive pressure ventilation, narcotics, shock, vasoactive amines, congestive heart failure, arterio-sclerosis, polyarteritis nodosa,
- Obstruction of cystic duct by extrinsic inflammation, lymphadenopathy, metastases
- Infection (only in 50%): Salmonella, Helicobacter, cholera, Kawasaki syndrome, cytomegalovirus, cryptosporidiosis
- WBC count may be normal (⅓)
  - point tenderness rare (diabetic neuropathy)
- Cx:     gangrene (75%); gallbladder perforation (20%)
- Mortality:  15%
- Rx:     urgent cholecystectomy; temporizing percutaneous cholecystostomy + antibiotics
- Enteric fistula
- Incompetent sphincter of Oddi
- Air-containing periduodenal abscess
- Periappendiceal abscess in malpositioned appendix
- Lipomatosis of gallbladder
