# Finding: A. NONIATROGENIC

**Source section:** D. VASCULAR
**Quality score:** 0.4
**Differential count:** 14

## Differentials

- Liver abscess
- Mesenteric infarction, necrotizing enterocolitis, colitis → portal venous gas
- Hepatic artery thrombosis in liver transplant
- Inflammatory bowel disease
- Abdominal trauma
- Emphysematous cholecystitis, ascending cholangitis
- IATROGENIC Hepatic gas is to be expected after therapeutic interventions like surgery, embolization, or tumor ablation.
- Biliary gas: sphincterotomy / choledochojejunostomy
- Colonoscopy, barium enema examination
- Liver biopsy
- Hepatic artery embolization: chemoembolization, brachytherapy, posttraumatic embolization
- Percutaneous tumor ablation
- Migration of hepatic venous gas: femoral venous / spinal / epidural catheter or lumbar puncture
- Oxidized cellulose sponge (SURGICEL®) Hepatic venous gas may be seen after placement of a femoral central venous catheter and aggressive fluid resuscitation.
