# Finding: Acute Renal Failure (ARF

**Source section:** DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS OF UROGENITAL DISORDERS
**Quality score:** 0.6
**Differential count:** 6

## Differentials

- PRERENAL =  renal hypoperfusion ← systemic illness
- Fluid + electrolyte depletion
- Hemorrhage
- Hepatic failure + hepatorenal syndrome
- Congestive heart failure
- Sepsis

## Context

=  clinical condition associated with rapid steadily increasing azotemia ± oliguria (< 500 mL urine per day) over days / weeks Etiology: √  abnormally elevated resistive index √  resistive index < 0.75 in 80% of kidneys Frequency:   70% of community-acquired ARF (glomerulonephritis); 40% of hospital- acquired ARF (acute tubular necrosis)
