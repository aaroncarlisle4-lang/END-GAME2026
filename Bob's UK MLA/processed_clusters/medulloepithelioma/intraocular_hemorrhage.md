# Finding: Intraocular Hemorrhage

**Source section:** MEDULLOEPITHELIOMA
**Quality score:** 0.7
**Differential count:** 3

## Differentials

- Vitreous hemorrhage (53%) Mechanism:   disruption of retinal blood vessels → hemorrhage into vitreous humor of posterior segment
  - "black rain" / decreased visual acuity (frequent)
- Retrohyaloid hemorrhage (2%)
- Hematoma in retroocular space

## Context

US: √  normal / slightly increased vitreous echogenicity (= mild acute bleeding) √  poorly defined low-level echoes / hypoechoic clots within vitreous (= abundant hemorrhage) √  echogenic material moving freely within vitreous chamber during eye movement CAVE:   Severe vitreous hemorrhage may obscure other ocular findings! CT: √  hyperattenuating fluid in posterior segment Cx:   retinal detachment ← vitreous traction ← fibrovascular ingrowth following hemorrhage Prognosis:   resolution may occur within 2-8 weeks Rx:   vitrectomy √  echogenic material remaining behind detached vitreous capsule
