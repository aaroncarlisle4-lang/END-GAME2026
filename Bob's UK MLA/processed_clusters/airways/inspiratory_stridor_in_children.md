# Finding: Inspiratory Stridor in Children

**Source section:** AIRWAYS
**Quality score:** 1.0
**Differential count:** 15

## Differentials

- Croup
- Congenital subglottic stenosis
- Subglottic hemangioma
- Airway foreign body
- Esophageal foreign body
- Epiglottitis
- Narrower nasopharynx
- Shorter trachea
- Larynx location more anterosuperiorly at level of C3-C4
- Cricoid cartilage = narrowest part
- Conus elasticus (located 1 cm below glottis): very susceptible to edema
- Vocal cords more anteriorly angled
- Epiglottis broader and longer
- Tongue larger
- Intercostal and diaphragmatic muscles weaker In acute upper airway obstruction obtain lateral and frontal soft-tissue radiographs of the neck in upright position. If unstable, a single upright soft-tissue lateral radiograph is usually sufficiently diagnostic.

## Context

Airway Obstruction in Childhood Predisposing anatomy compared to adult: In acute lower airway obstruction → upright frontal CXR during inspiration with depiction of > 6 anterior ribs at mid-hemidiaphragmatic level + lateral neck radiograph and also expiratory CXR with possible foreign body aspiration.
