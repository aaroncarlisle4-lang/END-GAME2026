# Finding: Hyperthyroidism with Low Radioiodine Uptake

**Source section:** THYROID
**Quality score:** 0.633
**Differential count:** 7

## Differentials

- Iodine-induced hyperthyroidism = Jod-Basedow
- Foods: seaweed, sushi, Japanese cuisine, Portuguese stew
- Health food supplement: Kelp tablets
- Radiographic contrast agent: renografin, Gastrografin®
- Topical antiseptic: povidone iodine, iodoform gauze
- Medication: amiodarone, Entero-Vioform®, COMBID®, iodine-containing expectorants
- Iodine solutions: KI, Lugol's solution, saturated solution of potassium iodide

## Context

Mechanism of low thyroid uptake: large serum concentration of exogenous non-radioactive iodine dilutes radioiodine tracer Sources of iodine: Wolff-Chaikoff effect = continued organification of iodine is inhibited once intrathyroidal iodide stores reach a critical level Escape from the Wolff-Chaikoff effect = intrathyroidal level of iodide decreases despite persistent high serum iodine levels → iodide organification and thyroid hormone synthesis resume Duration:  weeks - months
