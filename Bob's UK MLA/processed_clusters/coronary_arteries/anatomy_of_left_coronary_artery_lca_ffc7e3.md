# Finding: Anatomy of Left Coronary Artery (LCA

**Source section:** CORONARY ARTERIES
**Quality score:** 0.533
**Differential count:** 7

## Differentials

- Left main coronary artery (LM)
- Left anterior descending (LAD)
- otherwise split halfway between 1st septal perforator and apex into:
- Left circumflex artery (LCx) travels within left atrioventricular sulcus (groove);
- Conus artery (CB) =  1st branch of RCA (in 50-60% of patients); may originate directly from coronary sinus of aorta (in 30-35%)
- Sinoatrial node artery (SANA) =  2nd branch of RCA (in 60%) / from LCx (in 40%)
- Marginal branches (M1, M2, etc) have an anterior course ›  acute marginal branch = at junction of middle + distal RCA
