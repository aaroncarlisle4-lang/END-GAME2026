# Finding: Spontaneous Pneumoperitoneum

**Source section:** ABNORMAL INTRAABDOMINAL AIR
**Quality score:** 0.7
**Differential count:** 16

## Differentials

- Perforated peptic ulcer
- Ischemia
- Bowel obstruction
- Toxic megacolon
- Inflammation: appendicitis, tuberculosis, necrotizing enterocolitis
- Benign pneumoperitoneum of systemic sclerosis
- Malignancy (8-10%): colorectal carcinoma (3-10%), gastrointestinal lymphoma
- Perforating foreign body: eg, thermometer injury to rectum, vaginal stimulator in rectum
- Barotrauma:
- ABDOMINAL GAS =  presence of gas confined to inner layer of abdominal wall but external to parietal peritoneum (a)  gastrointestinal gas
- "Pseudo-wall" sign = apposition of gas-distended bowel loops
- Chilaiditi syndrome
- Diaphragmatic hernia
- Diverticulum of esophagus / stomach / duodenum (b)  extraintestinal gas
- Retroperitoneal air: eg, rectal injury
- Subdiaphragmatic abscess
